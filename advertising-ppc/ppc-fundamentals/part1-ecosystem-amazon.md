## SECTION 1: PPC ADVERTISING ECOSYSTEM OVERVIEW

### 1.1 What is PPC in Marketplace Context

PPC (Pay-Per-Click) advertising is a performance-based digital marketing model where advertisers pay a fee each time a prospective customer clicks on their advertisement. In marketplace environments, PPC represents a direct relationship between ad spend and visibility, separate from algorithmic ranking.

**Core Definition:**
- Advertiser creates ad campaign with budget, targeting parameters, and bid amounts
- Marketplace displays ad to qualifying users based on search terms, browsing behavior, or product attributes
- Each click on the ad charges the advertiser the agreed bid amount (or lower, depending on auction mechanics)
- Conversion of clicked visitor is attributed back to the ad impression/click

**Key Distinction from Organic Search:**
- Organic ranking (algorithmic) determined by relevance, velocity, reviews, feedback
- PPC advertising independent of organic ranking; poor organic ranking ≠ no PPC visibility
- High-bid low-relevance product can dominate PPC if budget allows
- PPC allows paid "shortcuts" to visibility that organic ranking does not permit
