## 3. UK POST-BREXIT LOGISTICS

### 3.1 GB-EU Border Procedures

As of January 1, 2021, UK became a third country requiring full customs procedures for all EU trade.

#### Trade & Cooperation Agreement (TCA) Overview

**Key Terms:**
- Tariff-free trade (0% duty, but Rules of Origin apply)
- Rules of Origin: 45-55% local content (depending on sector)
- Customs declarations: Required for all shipments
- SPS (Sanitary & Phytosanitary) checks: Added for food/plant products

#### GB to EU Import Procedures

**Entry Point:** Any EU customs port (Rotterdam, Antwerp, Bremerhaven, Calais, etc.)

| Step | Timeline | Documents Required | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pre-notification (ENS)** | 2 hours before departure | Entry/Exit form (GB reference) | Included |
| **Transit to EU port** | 12-24 hours (typical crossing) | CMR, EORI number | €50-100 crossing fee |
| **Customs declaration (EU)** | 3 hours post-arrival | Invoice, TARIC, Rules of Origin cert., Origin declaration | €150-300 broker fee |
| **Phytosanitary check (if applicable)** | 2-8 hours | Phytosanitary certificate, product certificate | €50-200 inspection |
| **Clearance & release** | 1-2 days | Duty payment, VAT payment | Duty: 0% (TCA), VAT: 17-21% |

**Total Transit Time: 2-3 days (vs. <1 day pre-Brexit)**

#### EU to GB Import Procedures

**Entry Point:** Port of Dover, Holyhead (Wales), Belfast Port, or airport.

| Procedure | Timeline | Documents | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pre-notification (GVMS)** | 2 hours before departure | GVMS notification, invoice | Included |
| **Transit to UK port** | 1-3 days | CMR, EORI (GB), Safety & Security Entry | €40-80 crossing fee |
| **Customs declaration (UK/GB)** | 1-2 hours post-arrival | Commercial invoice, TARIC (UK equivalent), AES form | €100-200 broker fee |
| **Safety & Security check** | 2-4 hours | Trader's AEO/EIDR status reviewed | Included |
| **Customs clearance** | 1-3 days | Duty calculation, VAT calculation (20% GB rate) | 0% duty (TCA), 20% VAT |

**Post-Brexit Realities (March 2026):**
- Procedures now fully operational (grace periods ended)
- Physical inspections: 5-10% for EU goods (vs. 0.5% pre-Brexit)
- Average total time: 3-4 days (down from initial 5-7 days in 2021)

### 3.2 Customs Declarations & EORI

#### EORI (Economic Operators Registration and Identification)

**EORI Number Purpose:** Unique identifier for all customs transactions.

**EORI Structure:**
- GB EORI: GB + 12 digits (e.g., GB123456789012)
- EU EORI: 2-letter country code + digits (e.g., DE123456789012)
- Both required for GB-EU trade

**Obtaining EORI:**

| Entity | Application Process | Timeline | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **UK Importers** | HMRC online portal (www.gov.uk/eori) | 5-10 working days | Free |
| **EU Importers** | National customs authority portal | 10-15 days | Free (varies by country) |
| **Non-EU/UK operators** | Via licensed customs agent | 15-30 days | €100-500 agent fee |

#### Customs Declarations (C&E Form 3)

**Required for all goods crossing GB-EU border** (no threshold exemption post-Brexit).

**Form Elements:**
| Field | Details | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Trader EORI** | Importer's registered number | GB123456789012 |
| **Declarant info** | Broker or company authorized rep | Company name & contact |

| **Quantity & weight** | Total units and kg | 100 units, 2,500 kg |
| **Country of origin** | Non-preferential | Vietnam (example) |
| **Incoterm & value** | CIF/DAP and €uro amount | DAP €5,000 |
| **Preferential claim** | If claiming TCA rate | "TCA origin" + cert. attached |

#### Customs Declaration Costs (GB-EU per shipment)

|---|---|---|

### 3.3 Rules of Origin (RoO) for TCA Trade

**Critical for tariff-free access:** Goods must meet 45-55% local content rule to qualify for 0% duty under TCA.

#### RoO Calculation Methods

**Method 1: Value Added Method (Most Common)**

```
Local Value Added = (Finished Good Value - Non-Originating Materials Cost) / Finished Good Value × 100%
```

**Example:**
- Finished garment value: €100
- Non-originating fabric/materials: €45 (imported from non-RoO partner)
- Local value added: (€100 - €45) / €100 = 55% ✓ Qualifies (meets 45% threshold)

**Method 2: Yarn Forward Rule (Textiles & Apparel)**

Requires:
- Spinning (yarn production): EU/UK origin
- Weaving/knitting: EU/UK origin
- Garment assembly: Can be non-EU (e.g., Vietnam)

*Rationale:* Protects EU textile mills from Chinese yarn competition.

**Method 3: Chapter Rule (Varying by Sector)**

| Sector | Rule | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Steel/Metals** | 50% value added OR specific process | Hot rolling requirement |
| **Electronics** | 45% value added | Integrated circuit origin not required |
| **Automotive** | 45-55% value added depending on component | Engine: 55%; electronic components: 45% |
| **Food/Beverage** | 100% raw materials from RoO partners | No non-originating sugar/grain allowed |

#### RoO Certificate Requirements

**Certificate of Origin (Form EUR.1):**

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| **Issued by** | Chamber of Commerce (EU) or competent authority (UK) |
| **Valid for** | 12 months from issuance |
| **Cost** | €20-50 per certificate (bulk: €5-10 each) |
| **Processing time** | 3-5 working days |
| **Required copies** | 1 per customs declaration + 1 for buyer's records |

**Self-Certification (Approved Exporter Option):**

If exporter is approved exporter status (similar to AEO):
- Self-issue certificate on invoice
- No Chamber of Commerce fee
- Saves 3-5 days vs. official certificate
- Requirements: 2+ years clean record + €500-1,000 annual status fee

#### RoO Non-Compliance Consequences

| Scenario | Consequence | Cost Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **No RoO certificate provided** | Goods cleared at non-preferential rate (apply standard TARIC duty) | 8-15% additional duty |
| **Insufficient local content (40% vs. 45% required)** | Same as above; goods reclassified | 8-15% additional duty |
| **Incorrect RoO claim** | Customs audit; duty reassessment | Original duty + 10-25% penalty + interest |
| **False RoO certificate** | Criminal prosecution + €10,000-50,000 fine | Criminal liability |

**Example Impact (€10,000 shipment):**
- Without RoO: €10,000 × 12% duty = €1,200 additional cost
- vs. With valid RoO: €0 duty = €1,200 savings

### 3.4 Northern Ireland Protocol (Windsor Framework)

**Special Status:** Northern Ireland operates under hybrid customs regime (partially EU-aligned for goods).

#### Protocol Mechanics

**For GB (England, Scotland, Wales) to NI Movement:**
- Requires customs declaration (to UK customs, not EU)
- 0% duty applies (UK law)
- Form C&E 302 (Internal UK movement document)
- Phytosanitary checks at GB-NI border

**For EU to NI Movement:**
- Treated as EU import (full procedures apply)
- Customs declaration to UK (not EU)
- RoO apply if claiming preferential rate
- Exemption: Goods destined for final retail in NI (threshold: €500/30 kg)

#### Windsor Framework Simplifications (Post-2024)

**Trusted Trader Scheme (TTS):**
- Reduce bureaucracy for GB-NI movement of non-sensitive goods
- Goods pre-cleared by Trader identification
- Transit time reduction: 2-3 hours vs. 4-6 hours standard
- Cost saving: €30-50 per consignment

**Goods Movement Service (GMS):**
- Optional service allowing GB traders to "reset" goods as UK-origin in NI
- Applied to goods in temporary storage
- Reduces paper for repeat shipments
- Annual fee: €1,200-2,000

#### NI-Specific Tariff Provisions

| Provision | Impact | Sectors Affected |
|---|---|---|
| **Agricultural products** | Full EU tariff applied (generous quotas) | Dairy, meat, grains |
| **Industrial goods** | 0% (UK rate) via trusted trader | Electronics, textiles |
| **Energy** | EU price support mechanisms apply | Oil, gas, electricity trading |

### 4.3 Norwegian Import Requirements

**Norway Status:** EEA member; participates in EU customs procedures but maintains separate tariff.

**Key Points:**

Benefits similar to EU AEO:
- 50-60% faster clearance

- Lower inspection rate (0.5% vs. 3-5%)
- Cost: NOK 3,000-5,000 annual fee (~€285-475)

### 4.4 Liechtenstein Logistics

**Liechtenstein Status:** Customs union with Switzerland; goods move through Swiss system.

**Implication:** Apply Swiss procedures; Liechtenstein customs clearance handled by Swiss authorities.

### 6.3 Consolidation Services

**Definition:** Aggregating multiple shipments for economies of scale.

#### Types of Consolidation

| Type | Structure | Timeline | Cost Saving | Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Groupage (LTL)** | Shared truck, multiple customers | 5-7 days to consolidation | 25-35% vs. FTL | Sub-20 CBM shipments |
| **Less Than Container Load (LCL)** | Shared 20/40ft container, oceanborne | 14-21 days | 30-50% vs. FCL | 5-15 CBM to Asia/Americas |
| **Air consolidation** | Shared aircraft, international express | 2-4 days | 20-40% vs. single shipper | <500 kg, high-value |
| **Cross-docking** | Goods sorted at hub, no storage | 1-2 days | 15-25% handling reduction | Time-sensitive |
| **Bonded consolidation** | Customs consolidation, defer duty | 3-5 days | 5-10% (duty deferral benefit) | Import goods |

#### Consolidation Cost Example

**Scenario:** Exporter wants to send 8 CBM to Germany (normally requires 20 CBM FTL).

**Option A: Direct FTL**
- FTL rate: €1,200 for full 20 CBM truck
- Your shipment: 8 CBM = 8/20 × €1,200 = €480
- Payment: Full truck, pro-rata your share

**Option B: Groupage Consolidator**
- Consolidator collects 8 shipments, 2.5 CBM each = 20 CBM
- Rate per CBM: €45-55 (shared overhead)
- Your cost: 8 CBM × €50 = €400
- Timeline: Consolidation 3 days + transit 2 days = 5 days (vs. 2 days FTL)
- Savings: €480 - €400 = €80 (17% savings)

**When Consolidation Makes Sense:**
- Shipment size: <5 CBM
- Time sensitivity: <5 days acceptable
- Cost priority: Higher than speed
- Frequency: Irregular shipments

### 6.5 Bonded Warehousing & Free Trade Zones (FTZ)

**Bonded Warehouse:** Government-approved facility storing imported goods with duty deferred until sale/use in EU.

#### Bonded Warehouse Benefits & Costs

| Benefit | Impact | Condition |
|---|---|---|
| **Duty deferral** | Pay duty only when goods leave bonded status | Goods remain in bond until sold/transferred |
| **VAT deferral** | VAT calculation deferred until release | Improves cash flow for importers |
| **Flexible import timing** | Import goods early, release gradually | Match release to seasonal demand |
| **Inventory flexibility** | Hold goods 3-5 years without duty accrual | Hedge against tariff changes |

**Costs:**

| Cost Element | Amount | Calculation |
|---|---|---|
| **Bonded warehouse rent** | €2-4/sqm/month | Premium: +30-40% vs. regular warehouse |
| **Storage surety bond** | 0.5-1% of goods value/year | Minimum €5,000-10,000 |
| **Customs clearance per release** | €150-300 per transaction | Each movement in/out documented |
| **Handling (in-bond movement)** | €0.50-1.00/unit | Transfer between bonded locations |
| **License application fee** | €1,500-3,000 | One-time, valid 5-7 years |

**Bonded Warehouse Calculation (Example):**

**Scenario:** Importer holds 50,000 units (€250,000 value) of electronics in warehouse, released gradually over 12 months.

**Cost Comparison (12-month holding):**

| Item | Regular Warehouse | Bonded Warehouse |
|---|---|---|
| Warehouse rent (10,000 sqm @ €70 vs. €100) | €84,000 | €120,000 |
| Duty (paid immediately) | €25,000 (assumed 10%) | €0 (deferred) |
| VAT (paid immediately) | €52,500 (assumed 21%) | €0 (deferred) |
| Surety bond | €0 | €2,500 |
| **Total 12-month cost** | **€161,500** | **€122,500** |
| **Cash flow advantage** | - | **€77,500 (duty+VAT deferred)** |

**Bonded Warehouse Use Cases:**
1. **Seasonal importer:** Hold winter inventory in bonded warehouse June-August; release September-December
2. **Trade hedge:** Import at current tariff rates, hold for up to 5 years; release if tariffs increase
3. **Customs clearance optimization:** Hold goods until sales confirm, then clear exact quantity needed
4. **Multi-customer consolidation:** Multiple customers' goods stored together; release per order

#### Free Trade Zones (FTZ) in Europe

**FTZ Definition:** Geographically defined area where goods are treated as "outside" EU customs territory until processing/sale.

**Major FTZ Locations:**

| Zone | Country | Size | Key Benefit | Sectors |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Hamburg FTZ** | Germany | 74 hectares | Manufacturing/assembly allowed | Electronics, automotive |
| **Antwerp FTZ** | Belgium | 70 hectares | Deep-sea container processing | Containers, consolidation |
| **Valencia FTZ** | Spain | 60 hectares | Mediterranean distribution | Electronics, retail |
| **Dublin FTZ** | Ireland | 50 hectares | Tech hub, IP-light assembly | Software, electronics |
| **Frankfurt FTZ** | Germany | 50 hectares | Air cargo, time-critical goods | Pharmaceuticals, valuables |

**FTZ Operating Model:**
- Goods imported, no duty charged
- Can be processed/repackaged in FTZ
- If re-exported: No EU duty ever applies
- If sold in EU: Duty + VAT charged upon exit FTZ

**FTZ Cost Structure:**

| Service | Cost |
|---|---|
| Annual FTZ operator license | €2,500-5,000 |
| Warehouse rent (inside FTZ) | €3-5/sqm/month (standard rate) |
| Customs clearance (per shipment exit) | €100-200 |
| Value-added services (repackaging) | €0.50-1.50/unit |

**FTZ Example (Electronics Repackaging):**

**Scenario:** Importer receives 10,000 units of generic phones from China; needs to repackage with European branding + localization software.

**In Regular Warehouse:**
1. Import → Customs duty (€25,000 on €250,000 value @ 10%)
2. VAT (€52,500 on €277,500 @ 21%)
3. Repackaging labor: €2/unit = €20,000
4. **Total cost: €97,500**

**In FTZ Warehouse:**
1. Import → NO duty (FTZ status)
2. Repackaging in FTZ: €2/unit = €20,000
3. Exit FTZ → Apply duty (€25,000) + VAT (€52,500) only on value-added portion (~€20k software) = reduced duty exposure
4. **Total cost: ~€60,000-70,000** (30%+ savings due to timing of duty/VAT on repackaged items)
