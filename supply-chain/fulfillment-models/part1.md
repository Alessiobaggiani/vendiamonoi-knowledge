# SECTION 0: OVERVIEW & FULFILLMENT MODEL COMPARISON

## Fulfillment Models Available to Amazon EU Sellers

European marketplace sellers have access to multiple fulfillment options. Understanding the trade-offs is critical.

**Six Primary Fulfillment Models:**

1. **FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon) Pan-EU** — Amazon handles everything across entire EU
2. **FBA EFN (European Fulfillment Network)** — Single-country inventory; Amazon cross-border shipping
3. **MCI (Multi-Country Inventory)** — Seller places inventory in 2-4 countries manually
4. **3PL (Third-Party Logistics)** — Seller contracts with external fulfillment provider
5. **FBM (Fulfillment by Merchant)** — Seller handles all fulfillment from own warehouse
6. **AWD (Amazon Warehousing & Distribution)** — Storage at Amazon; seller controls fulfillment

## 2.1 Amazon FBA Fulfillment Center Network Across EU

### 2.1.1 Geographic Distribution & Capacity

**Germany (5 Fulfillment Centers):**
- Locations: Rheinberg (North Rhine-Westphalia), Bad Hersfeld (Hesse), Pforzheim (Baden-Württemberg), Graben (North Rhine-Westphalia)
- Capacity: ~2 million m³ combined
- Functions: Primary sorting hub for Pan-EU; handles ~35% of EU FBA volume
- Specialization: Rheinberg focuses on large/oversized; Bad Hersfeld handles standard items
- Inventory velocity: Highest turnover (average 4 weeks inventory residency)

**France (3 Fulfillment Centers):**
- Locations: Montélimar (South), Orléans (Central), Douchy-les-Mines (North)
- Capacity: ~800,000 m³ combined
- Functions: Handles French market + overflow from Germany
- Specialization: Montélimar handles temperature-sensitive items
- Regional preference: ~60% of inventory stays in France for French customers; 40% rebalances

**Italy (2 Fulfillment Centers):**
- Locations: Castel San Giovanni (Piacenza, North), Piacenza (logistics hub)
- Capacity: ~400,000 m³ combined
- Functions: Serves Italian market; smaller secondary hub
- Specialization: Focus on medium/oversized items common in Italian market
- Cross-border: ~50% of inventory ships cross-border to Spain/Greece

**Spain (2 Fulfillment Centers):**
- Locations: Sevilla (South), Madrid (Central)
- Capacity: ~600,000 m³ combined
- Functions: Serves Spanish market; starting point for Southern/Portugal distribution
- Geographic advantage: Central location for Southern EU
- Inventory flow: Heavy inbound from Germany during peak seasons

**Netherlands (1 Major Fulfillment Center):**
- Location: Alkmaar (North Holland)
- Capacity: ~350,000 m³
- Functions: Secondary North European hub; handles overflow from Germany
- Specialization: European rebalancing hub; inventory consolidation point
- Cross-border: Ships to Belgium, Germany, France efficiently

**Poland (1 Fulfillment Center):**
- Location: Gliwice (Upper Silesia)
- Capacity: ~250,000 m³
- Functions: Serves Polish market; growing capacity as market expands
- Geographic reach: Also serves Czech Republic, Hungary, Slovakia
- Recent expansion: Capacity doubled in 2024; planned additional site 2026

**Belgium (1 Fulfillment Center):**
- Location: Liège
- Capacity: ~200,000 m³
- Functions: Regional hub for Benelux; handles overflow from Germany
- Specialization: Cross-border logistics consolidation
- Efficiency: Serves as entry point for inbound shipments to EU

**Sweden (1 Fulfillment Center):**
- Location: Västerås (Central Sweden)
- Capacity: ~150,000 m³
- Functions: Serves Nordic region (Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Finland)
- Limited inventory: ~10-15% of EU inventory typically stored here
- Long lead times: 4-7 days to ship outside Nordic region

### 2.1.2 Fulfillment Center Routing by Product Type

**Size/Weight Categories:**
- Small standard (max 1kg): Routed to nearest center (load balanced)
- Standard (1kg-5kg): Routed to nearest center with inventory
- Large (5kg-25kg): Concentrated in specific centers (Rheinberg, Sevilla)
- Oversized (>25kg): Specialized centers (Rheinberg Germany, Sevilla Spain)

**Receiving Capacity by Season:**
- Off-season (Jan-Aug): All centers accepting 5-7 days/week; no appointment limits
- Peak season (Sept-Oct): Receiving limited to 2-3 days/week; appointment-only
- Holiday peak (Nov-Dec): Receiving severely limited (1-2 days/week); applications weeks in advance
- Christmas rush: Some centers closed to inbound from mid-Dec to early Jan

## 2.2 Pan-European FBA: Automatic Inventory Distribution

### 2.2.1 Inventory Placement Algorithm (Simplified)

**Input Variables:**
- Historical sales by country (last 90 days)
- Projected demand by country (14-day forecast)
- Current inventory levels in each FC
- Shipping cost matrix (cost to ship each route)
- Return rates by FC (damaged goods rates)
- Storage utilization rates (prioritize balanced use)

**Algorithm Output:**
Amazon's ML model outputs optimal inventory distribution across 8+ FC locations. The algorithm runs daily at 2:00 AM CET and calculates ideal inventory levels per FC.

**Rebalancing Mechanics:**
- If algorithm suggests Germany inventory should drop 20%, Amazon calculates units to move
- Movement instructions sent to robotics in FC overnight
- Units physically moved within warehouse (no shipping cost to seller)
- Shelf location updated in system (1-2 hours)
- Rebalancing typically complete before customer orders next day

### 2.2.2 Demand Forecasting & Seasonality

**Historical Data Usage:**
- Years 1-2 data: Week-by-week seasonal patterns identified
- Years 3+: Granular by day-of-week and promotion period
- Example: Amazon knows June 1-15 has specific demand pattern based on 3+ years history
- Accuracy: Forecast accuracy typically 85-92% month-to-month

**Promotion Impact:**
- Lightning deals: Amazon increases inventory forecast 300-500% for promotion week
- External promotion: If seller runs social media campaign, Amazon's system may not detect (inventory gaps possible)
- Manual inventory boost: Sellers can request higher allocation during planned promotions

**Seasonal Adjustments (Q4 Peak):**
- September: Begin shifting inventory toward all EU countries (prepare for October demand)
- October: Aggressive rebalancing; heavy outbound from Germany towards all countries
- November: Maximize inventory distribution (Black Friday preparation)
- December: Shift to southern EU (holiday shopping peaks later in South vs. North)

### 2.2.3 VAT Implications of Pan-EU Inventory Placement

**VAT Registration Triggering Events:**
- VAT registration required in country where Amazon warehouse **holds inventory**
- Pan-EU automatically triggers warehousing in 8 countries = 8 VAT registrations required
- Even if single unit occasionally stored in secondary warehouse triggers full VAT registration

**Monthly VAT Obligation Per Country:**
- Each country requires separate VAT return filing (monthly or quarterly depending on country)
- German VAT return: Due 10th of month following (example: January sales reported by Feb 10)
- Italian VAT return: Due monthly, filed electronically with Italian tax authorities
- Each return includes: Total sales, total VAT charged, VATable supplies, refund claims

**OSS (One-Stop Shop) Registration:**
- Pan-EU sellers MUST register with OSS for cross-border B2C sales
- OSS allows single monthly/quarterly return covering all B2C cross-border sales
- Not substitution for country-by-country VAT registration (both required)
- OSS filing: Single return covering all 27 EU countries
- OSS cost: Included in EU VAT software subscriptions

**Compliance Burden Calculation:**
- Germany: 1 monthly VAT return + OSS monthly return = 2 filings
- France: 1 monthly return + OSS = 2 filings
- Italy: 1 monthly return + OSS = 2 filings
- Spain: 1 monthly return + OSS = 2 filings
- Netherlands: 1 quarterly return + OSS = 2 filings
- Poland: 1 quarterly return + OSS = 2 filings
- Sweden: 1 monthly return + OSS = 2 filings
- Belgium: 1 monthly return + OSS = 2 filings
- **TOTAL: 16 required filings per month (8 country filings + 8 OSS)**

**Professional Tax Support Costs:**
- Accountancy fees: €300-€500 per month (€3,600-€6,000/year) for multi-country handling
- VAT software: €100-€300/month (€1,200-€3,600/year) for automated filing
- Compliance risk: Penalties for late/incorrect filing: €500-€5,000 per country per quarter if non-compliant

## 2.3 European Fulfillment Network (EFN): Single-Country Inventory Model

### 2.3.1 EFN Mechanics in Detail

**Inventory Concentration:**
- Seller chooses ONE country as home FC (typically Germany due to warehouse density)
- 100% of inventory stored in single chosen country
- No automatic rebalancing to other countries (seller controls all movements)
- Cross-border shipment to EU customers automated by Amazon

**Order Fulfillment Workflow:**
1. Customer in France purchases item held in Germany warehouse
2. Amazon Fulfillment system detects home FC is different from customer location
3. Order automatically shipped from Germany FC
4. Shipping cost absorbed by seller (included in fulfillment fee)
5. Delivery time: 3-5 days to customer (vs. 1-2 days if inventory in destination country)
6. Tracking provided by carrier (DHL, DPD, or similar)

**Geographic Delivery Commitments:**
- Germany customer (home country): 2-3 day delivery
- France customer: 4-5 day delivery
- Italy customer: 4-6 day delivery (more peripheral)
- Poland customer: 5-7 day delivery
- Sweden customer: 6-8 day delivery (longest, northernmost)

### 2.3.2 EFN VAT Simplification

**Single VAT Registration:**
- Home country only: Seller registers VAT in Germany (example)
- Cross-border sales: Treated as B2B supplies from German VAT perspective
- Final consumer tax: OSS registration handles B2C tax reporting
- Total tax filings: Germany return + OSS return = 2 filings/month

**No Multi-Country VAT Required:**
- Unlike Pan-EU, seller NOT required to register in France, Italy, Spain, etc.
- Simplifies compliance significantly
- Cost savings: €200-€400/month vs. Pan-EU compliance costs

**OSS Filing for Cross-Border Sales:**
- OSS return includes all B2C sales shipped to other EU countries
- VAT rate applied: Customer's destination country VAT rate (not Germany's)
- Example: Item sold to Italian customer subject to Italian VAT (22%), not German (19%)
- OSS system calculates automatically based on customer address

### 2.3.3 EFN Eligibility & Approval Process

**Eligibility Requirements:**
- Account age: 60+ days in good standing
- Account Health: 95%+ (no recent warnings)
- Order Defect Rate: <1% (returns, claims, chargebacks)
- Late Shipment Rate: <4% (if seller has FBM orders)
- Positive feedback: 4.5+ star average feedback

**Application Process:**
1. Navigate to Seller Central > Settings > Fulfillment Settings
2. Click "Apply for EFN" button
3. Select desired home country (Germany typically recommended)
4. Submit application with business documentation
5. Amazon reviews (3-7 business days)
6. Approval notification via email

**Trial Period (90 Days):**
- Amazon monitors on-time delivery, customer complaints, returns
- Performance threshold: 95%+ on-time shipment rate during trial
- If threshold met: Full EFN approval granted permanently
- If threshold missed: Trial terminated; 180-day waiting period before re-application allowed

**Post-Approval Ongoing Monitoring:**
- Metrics tracked: On-time delivery rate, customer defect rate
- Minimum performance: 95% on-time maintained
- If drops below: 30-day warning period; 60-day grace period before suspension
- Reactivation: Requires 60+ days of meeting performance thresholds

### 2.3.4 EFN Limitations & Tradeoffs

**Delivery Speed Penalty:**
- Germany customer: 2-3 days (competitive)
- France customer: 4-5 days vs. 2-3 days if inventory in France (2-3 day disadvantage)
- Search ranking impact: Slower delivery hurts search visibility by ~15-20%
- Customer selection: Buyers may choose competitors with faster delivery

**Inventory Concentration Risk:**
- Single warehouse failure impacts all customers
- If German warehouse affected by flood/fire: Full business shutdown
- No geographic diversification
- Stockout risk: Single location creates inventory imbalance

**Promotional Constraints:**
- Promotions in Southern EU difficult to support (longer lead times)
- Lightning deals: Require inventory immediately available; harder with distant warehouse
- Peak season: Inventory in peripheral countries may stockout due to shipping delays

## 2.4 Multi-Country Inventory (MCI): Manual Placement Control

### 2.4.1 MCI Strategy & Activation

**Core Benefit:**
Multi-Country Inventory allows sellers to manually specify inventory distribution across 2-4 countries, balancing delivery speed with VAT compliance burden.

**Typical MCI Configurations:**
- **2-country model**: Germany + Spain (covers 50% of EU market, manageable VAT)
- **3-country model**: Germany + France + Spain (covers 65% of EU market)
- **4-country model**: Germany + France + Italy + Spain (covers 75% of EU market, high complexity)

**Inventory Allocation Decision Criteria:**
- Sales velocity by country: Allocate inventory proportional to demand
- Delivery time sensitivity: Allocate more to geographically distant countries
- VAT compliance cost: Each additional country = €300-€500/month accountancy cost
- Optimal calculation: (country revenue) vs. (additional tax cost + warehousing cost)

### 2.4.2 MCI Inventory Rebalancing Process

**Monthly Review Cycle:**
1. Analyze sales velocity by country (last 30 days)
2. Forecast demand for next 30 days
3. Calculate required inventory per country
4. Compare to current inventory position
5. Create rebalancing shipment plans

**Rebalancing Mechanics:**
- Seller creates shipment plan via Seller Central: Send from German warehouse to French warehouse
- Preparation: Boxes packed and labeled with shipment plan reference
- Shipping: DHL, DPD, or equivalent carrier (seller pays shipping cost)
- Transit time: 3-5 days Germany to France; 4-7 days Germany to Spain
- Receiving: Destination FC verifies and restocks (2-3 days)
- Total timeline: 10-14 days from rebalancing decision to new inventory sold

**Rebalancing Costs Per Shipment:**
- Pallet shipping (20-30 units): €200-€400
- Less-than-truckload (100-200 units): €400-€800
- Full truckload (800+ units): €1,200-€2,000
- Monthly rebalancing frequency: Sellers typically rebalance 1-2 times per month

### 2.4.3 MCI VAT Compliance Structure

**Multi-Country Registration Required:**
- Seller registers VAT in each country holding inventory (2-4 countries)
- Example MCI setup: Germany (50% inventory) + Spain (30%) + France (20%)
- VAT registrations required: Germany, Spain, France = 3 returns per month

**Per-Country VAT Return Filing:**
- Germany: Monthly return due 10th of following month
- Spain: Monthly return (IVA) due 20th of following month
- France: Monthly return (TVA) due before 20th of following month
- Each return includes: Sales in that country, VAT charged, VAT refund claims

**OSS Registration (Still Required):**
- Covers B2C cross-border sales (e.g., French customer buying inventory held in Germany)
- Single OSS return covers all cross-border transactions
- Filed once monthly covering entire EU

**Compliance Filing Summary (3-Country MCI Model):**
- Germany VAT return: Monthly
- Spain VAT return: Monthly
- France VAT return: Monthly
- OSS return (cross-border): Monthly
- **Total: 4 filings per month** (vs. 16 under Pan-EU)

## 2.5 FBA Inbound Logistics & Procedures

### 2.5.1 Creating Your First Shipment Plan

**Step-by-Step Walkthrough:**

**Step 1: Prepare Inventory**
- Count and verify all units
- Identify any damaged/defective products
- Remove packaging (unwrap if necessary for per-unit inspection)
- Organize by SKU for box packing

**Step 2: Navigate to Shipment Plan**
- Log in to Seller Central
- Go to: Inventory > Manage Inventory
- Select products to ship
- Click "Send to Amazon" > "Create a New Shipment Plan"

**Step 3: Specify Quantities**
- Enter quantity for each SKU
- Amazon shows: Recommended fulfillment center (based on demand forecast)
- Option: Choose specific fulfillment center (if Multi-Country Inventory enabled)
- Confirm quantities and proceed

**Step 4: Print Labels & Packing List**
- Amazon generates unique barcode per box
- Print barcode labels (laser printer required; 4x6 labels standard)
- Print packing slip (shows all SKUs/quantities in shipment)
- Include packing slip INSIDE each box (not on exterior)

**Step 5: Pack Boxes**
- Follow FBA prep requirements exactly
- Place FNSKU label on each unit (or use barcode if permitted)
- Polybag if applicable
- Fill with void fill (2+ inches on all sides)
- Seal all seams with packing tape
- Apply shipment barcode label on exterior (top-right preferred)

**Step 6: Ship to Amazon**
- Amazon provides carrier options (DHL, DPD, Hermes)
- Select carrier and generate shipping label
- Add shipping label to box exterior (below/separate from barcode)
- Prepare shipment for pickup or drop-off
- Update tracking in Seller Central

**Step 7: Monitor Arrival**
- Tracking updates automatically as carrier scans shipment
- Delivery to FC typically 1-3 business days
- Amazon receives and scans boxes (notification sent)
- Processing: 7-14 days from receipt to full availability

### 2.5.2 Box Labeling & Barcode Specifications

**Shipment Barcode (on box exterior):**
- Label format: Code 128 barcode (1D barcode, not QR code)
- Label dimensions: 1" x 2.625" minimum, 2" x 3" maximum
- Content: FTZ + fulfillment center code + shipment ID (Amazon-generated)
- Placement: Top-right corner preferred, on flat surface without seams
- Scannability: 100% readable on first scan (critical)
- Printer: Laser only (inkjet often illegible)

**Unit FNSKU Labels (on each product):**
- Format: Code 128 barcode (1D)
- Label dimensions: 1" x 2.625" to 2" x 3"
- Content: FNSKU barcode (Amazon-specific identifier)
- Placement: Smooth, flat surface (corners/seams not acceptable)
- Coverage: Every single unit must have individual label
- Multiple units per box: All units labeled separately

**Packing Slip (inside box):**
- Content: Shipment ID, box contents (SKU list + quantities)
- Format: Text document, printed on standard paper
- Placement: Visible when box opened, typically taped inside lid
- Purpose: FC staff use to verify contents match shipment plan

### 2.5.3 Standard Processing Timeline

| Step | Days | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Create shipment plan | 0 | Immediate in Seller Central |
| Print labels & pack | 1-3 | Your time to prepare |
| Ship to Amazon | 0-2 | Carrier pickup or drop-off |
| Transit to FC | 1-3 | Carrier shipping time |
| FC receipt scan | 1 | Automatic upon physical arrival |
| Verification/QC | 3-7 | Random sampling, damage check |
| Stocking | 5-10 | Placement on shelves |

**Timeline Acceleration Options:**
- Prep service: If using external prep, add 2-3 days
- Guaranteed delivery: Pay expedited shipping (costs 30-50% premium)
- Multiple shipments: Stagger to ensure weekly arrivals (maintains inventory flow)

## 2.6 FBA Removal & Disposal Program

### 2.6.1 Removal Process (Seller-Initiated)

**Reasons to Remove Inventory:**
- Slow-moving products (velocity dropped below minimum)
- Model changes (new version released, old stock obsolete)
- Return to own facility for retail channel
- Liquidation: Sell on discount channel instead of Amazon

**Removal Steps:**
1. Go to Inventory > Manage Inventory
2. Select products to remove
3. Choose option: "Remove item" or "Dispose of item"
4. Specify quantities to remove (partial removal allowed)
5. Amazon schedules removal (5-14 days)
6. Items packed into customer-provided boxes or Amazon boxes
7. Shipping arranged (seller pays removal fee + shipping cost)
8. Items delivered to seller address

**Removal Fees:**
- Per unit: €0.50-€1.50 depending on size/weight
- Shipping cost: €200-€600 per shipment (depends on weight/distance)
- Total cost example: 100 units @ €0.75/unit + €400 shipping = €475

### 2.6.2 Disposal Program

**When Disposal Makes Sense:**
- Dead inventory (not selling, no secondary market)
- Damaged goods (condition makes resale impossible)
- Returns accumulating in warehouse
- Liquidation through Amazon's liquidation channel

**Disposal Options:**

**Option A: Automatic Disposal (Free)**
- Inventory in FC >365 days: Subject to disposal
- Amazon automatically destroys if not removed/sold
- Process: Quarterly (March, June, Sept, Dec)
- Cost: Free but inventory lost

**Option B: Seller-Initiated Disposal**
- Seller requests disposal in Seller Central
- Amazon destroys inventory at FC
- Disposal fee: €0.10-€0.25 per unit
- Timeline: 5-14 days from request to completion

**Option C: Liquidation Program**
- Amazon sells inventory at discount on liquidation channel
- Seller receives proceeds (typically 5-30% of original COGS)
- Amazon handles entire resale process
- Fee: 15% of liquidation proceeds to Amazon
- Timeline: 30-60 days to receive payment

## 2.7 Amazon Warehousing & Distribution (AWD)

### 2.7.1 AWD vs. FBA Comparison

AWD is Amazon's alternative to FBA for sellers who want storage but more control over fulfillment.

**Key Differences:**

| Feature | FBA | AWD |
|---------|-----|-----|
| Storage | Included in fulfillment fee | Separate monthly fee |
| Fulfillment | Automatic by Amazon | Seller controls (can send to own warehouse) |
| Returns | Handled by Amazon | Handled by seller |
| Pricing | Simple per-order fee | Storage + handling |
| Use case | High-volume, automated | Selective fulfillment, control |

**AWD Typical Use:**
- Sellers storing inventory temporarily (awaiting resale to other channels)
- Consolidation hub before shipping to own warehouse
- Inventory awaiting retail sale (not Amazon)
- Seasonal storage (Q4 peak preparation)

**AWD Pricing:**
- Storage: €3.50-€6.00 per m³ per month (cheaper than FBA)
- Handling: €0.50-€1.00 per unit when removed
- Receiving: €15-€40 per pallet
- Advantage: No fulfillment fees if not selling via Amazon
