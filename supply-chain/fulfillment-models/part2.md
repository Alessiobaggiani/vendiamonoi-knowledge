**Scoring System:** 1-5 points per criterion

| Criterion | Weight | Huboo | ShipBob | Byrd | Your Target | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|---------|------|-------------|-------|
| **Geographic Coverage** | 20% | 2/5 | 5/5 | 4/5 | ___ | Number of countries served |

### 3.2.2 Qualitative Evaluation Questions

**Geography & Logistics:**
- Which of your top 5 markets are served? (Need minimum 3 of top 5)
- Average delivery time to your key markets? (Should be <3 days for 95% of orders)
- Warehouse capacity available for 12-month contract? (Need flexibility for growth)
- Multi-warehouse inventory balancing: Automatic or manual? (Automatic preferred)

**Integration & Technology:**
- Which sales channels integrated natively? (All your active channels needed)
- API documentation quality? (Critical for custom integrations)
- Real-time inventory sync? (15-30 min delay acceptable; >1 hour problematic)
- Reporting/analytics: What KPIs available? (Order accuracy, ship-on-time, cost per order essential)

**Pricing & Terms:**
- Full cost per order formula? (Storage + pick/pack + shipping combined)
- Volume discounts available? (At 500, 1000, 2500 orders/month? By what %?)
- Contract length: Minimum term? (Prefer 12 months; avoid 24+ month locks)
- Minimum monthly volume commitment? (Understand penalty if you miss)

**Returns & Customer Service:**
- Return processing time? (<10 days acceptable)
- Return rate tolerance? (How high % returns before fees escalate?)
- Customer support availability? (Hours of operation, response SLAs)
- Onboarding timeline? (Should be <30 days from contract to live fulfillment)

## 3.3 Multi-3PL Strategy for EU Coverage

### 3.3.1 Why Multi-3PL is Necessary

**Single Warehouse Limitation:**
- From Germany warehouse: Spain delivery 4-6 days (too slow for competitive market)
- From Spain warehouse: Poland delivery 5-7 days (peripheral market suffering)
- Solution: Use 2+ warehouses for <3 day delivery across EU

**Optimal 2-3PL Network Design:**

**Configuration A: Germany + Spain (Covers 50% of EU)**
- Germany warehouse: Inventory 60% (serves Germany, France, Benelux, Central EU)
- Spain warehouse: Inventory 40% (serves Spain, Italy, Portugal, Greece)
- Delivery: <3 days to all major markets
- Cost: €2,200-€3,000/month (storage + fees)
- Recommended providers: Byrd (Germany) + Huboo (Spain)

**Configuration B: Germany + Spain + France (Covers 65% of EU)**
- Germany: 45% inventory (serves German-speaking countries + North)
- Spain: 25% inventory (serves Southern Europe)
- France: 30% inventory (serves France + Benelux cross-border)
- Delivery: <3 days to 90% of EU population
- Cost: €3,000-€4,500/month
- Recommended providers: ShipBob (all 3 locations)

### 3.3.2 Inventory Allocation Formula

**Demand-Based Allocation:**
- Calculate monthly revenue by country
- Allocate inventory proportional to country revenue (with adjustments)

**Example Calculation:**
- Germany revenue: €8,000/month (35% of total €22,900)
- France revenue: €5,000/month (22% of total)
- Spain revenue: €4,500/month (20% of total)
- Italy revenue: €3,000/month (13% of total)
- Other countries: €2,400/month (10% of total)

**Allocation Decision (assuming 3 warehouses: Germany, Spain, France):**
- Germany warehouse: 45% (serves Germany, Austria, Central EU, Benelux)
- Spain warehouse: 25% (serves Spain, Italy, Portugal, Greece)
- France warehouse: 30% (serves France, Belgium, Benelux, Scandinavia)

**Inventory Distribution by warehouse:**
- Germany (45% of 1,000 units) = 450 units
- Spain (25% of 1,000 units) = 250 units
- France (30% of 1,000 units) = 300 units
- Total = 1,000 units across 3 locations

## 4.1 Warehouse Layout Optimization for E-Commerce

### 4.1.1 Standard E-Commerce Warehouse Layout (2,500-10,000 m²)

**Zone-Based Layout Design:**

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│  RECEIVING DOCK (North Wall)        │
│  ├─ Unloading area (3-4 dock doors)│
│  ├─ Inspection/QC (50 m²)          │
│  └─ Quarantine staging (100 m²)    │
├─────────────────────────────────────┤
│  BULK STORAGE ZONE                  │
│  ├─ Pallet racking (40% of space)  │
│  ├─ Floor storage (high-volume SKUs)│
│  └─ Climate control (if needed)    │
├─────────────────────────────────────┤
│  PICK ZONE (Middle)                 │
│  ├─ Flow racks (fast-moving SKUs)  │
│  ├─ Bins/shelving (slow-moving)    │
│  ├─ High-density storage            │
│  └─ SKU arrangement (A-B-C analysis)│
├─────────────────────────────────────┤
│  PACKING STATION ZONE               │
│  ├─ 4-8 packing tables             │
│  ├─ Barcode scanners               │
│  ├─ Packaging materials storage    │
│  └─ Quality control (10 m²)        │
├─────────────────────────────────────┤
│  SHIPPING ZONE (South Wall)         │
│  ├─ Staging area (200 m²)          │
│  ├─ Carrier dock (2-3 dock doors)  │
│  └─ Sorting by carrier/route       │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Zone Sizing Guidelines (for 200 orders/day facility):**
- Receiving: 75-100 m² (1-2 teams unloading)
- Bulk storage: 1,000-1,500 m² (40-50% of facility)
- Pick zone: 400-600 m² (working area for pickers)
- Packing: 200-300 m² (4-8 packing stations)
- Shipping: 150-200 m² (staging before carrier pickup)
- Office/other: 200-300 m² (administrative space)
- **Total facility: 2,000-2,900 m²** for 200 orders/day operation

### 4.1.2 Pick Zone Optimization

**SKU Organization (A-B-C Analysis):**

| Tier | Definition | % of SKUs | % of Volume | Location | Picking Method |
|------|-----------|----------|------------|----------|------------------|
| A | High velocity | 10% | 70% | Waist-height flow racks | Single-order pick |
| B | Medium velocity | 30% | 25% | Lower shelves | Batch pick (10-20 orders) |
| C | Low velocity | 60% | 5% | High/low shelves | Wave pick (consolidate) |

**Space Allocation in Pick Zone:**
- A-tier SKUs: 10% of zone (high turnover, compact)
- B-tier SKUs: 30% of zone (medium space)
- C-tier SKUs: 60% of zone (spread out, less-used)

**Example Pick Zone Layout (500 m²):**
- A-tier (50 m²): 8 flow racks, 200 SKUs, restocked 2x daily
- B-tier (150 m²): Shelving system, 800 SKUs, restocked daily
- C-tier (300 m²): Bulk bins, 2,000 SKUs, restocked weekly

### 4.1.3 Receiving Zone Design

**Dock Configuration:**
- Dock doors: 1 per 50,000 units/month of throughput
- Typical: 200 orders/day facility = ~30,000 units/month = 1 dock door
- Higher volume: 2 dock doors recommended for efficient inbound

**Receiving Process Layout:**
1. Truck arrives at dock
2. Unload to receiving table (inspect labels)
3. Scan inbound barcode to confirm shipment
4. QC check: Open 10% of boxes, verify contents
5. Move to staging area (temporary holding)
6. Route to storage zone (high-velocity items fast-tracked)

**Staging Area Capacity:**
- Hold 2-3 days of inbound volume
- Example: 50 units/day inbound = need 100-150 m² staging space
- Longer holds = larger staging area needed

## 4.2 Picking Methods & Efficiency

### 4.2.1 Four Core Picking Methods

**Method 1: Single Order Pick**
- One worker picks items for single customer order
- Process: Walk zone, pick all items, move to packing
- Efficiency: 120-175 picks/hour (industry standard)
- Best for: Small order volume (<100/day) or high-velocity zones
- Cost: ~€15-€20 per order (labor only)
- Technology: Barcode scanner, printedpick list

**Method 2: Batch Pick**
- One worker picks same items for 10-20 customer orders simultaneously
- Process: Walk zone once, pick 10 units of Item A for 10 orders, etc.
- Efficiency: 250-350 picks/hour (faster due to consolidation)
- Best for: Medium order volume with repeat SKUs
- Cost: ~€8-€12 per order
- Technology: RF scanner, batch picking software

**Method 3: Wave Pick**
- Multiple workers pick different items in parallel across zones
- Organized by: Zone (each worker owns zone), then sorted by order
- Process: All pickers pick simultaneously, then consolidate to packing
- Efficiency: 400-600 picks/hour (parallelization benefit)
- Best for: High order volume (500+/day) with multiple zones
- Cost: ~€6-€10 per order (distributed labor)
- Technology: Conveyor system, WMS-driven wave assignments

**Method 4: Zone Pick**
- Facility divided into zones; worker picks only items in their zone
- Process: Each order split across zones; routed through zones sequentially
- Efficiency: 300-400 picks/hour (less walking than single-order, no consolidation delay)
- Best for: Large facilities with natural zone separation
- Cost: ~€8-€12 per order
- Technology: Sortation conveyor, zone-based WMS

### 4.2.2 Efficiency Benchmarks by Method

| Method | Picks/Hour | Cost/Order | Best For | Downside |
|--------|-----------|-----------|----------|----------|
| Single Order | 150 | €18 | <100 orders/day | Inefficient walking |
| Batch | 300 | €10 | 100-300 orders/day | Consolidation delays |
| Wave | 500 | €7 | 300+ orders/day | Complex setup |
| Zone | 350 | €9 | Multi-zone facilities | Requires routing system |

## 4.4 Barcode & Labeling Standards

### 4.4.1 Barcode Formats

**EAN-13 (European Standard):**
- 13 digits (country code + company code + product code + check digit)
- Used for: Retail products (GTIN standard)
- Scannable by: Standard barcode readers
- Example: 9 780595 265053 (ISBN converted to EAN)

**UPC-A (North American Standard):**
- 12 digits
- Mostly used in US; acceptable in EU but not primary
- Scannable: Standard barcode readers

**FNSKU (Amazon Fulfillment Network SKU):**
- Amazon-generated unique barcode
- Format: FTZ + 10-digit alphanumeric code
- Used only for: FBA fulfillment
- Replaces manufacturer barcode in FBA system

**SSCC (Serial Shipping Container Code):**
- 18-digit code for cartons/pallets
- Used for: Inbound shipment tracking
- Format: GS1 barcode format

### 4.4.2 Labeling Best Practices

**Multiple Barcode Scenarios:**

| Scenario | Barcode | Placement | Notes |
|----------|---------|-----------|-------|
| Retail with EAN | EAN-13 | Front of package | Standard retail |
| FBA sale | FNSKU | Every unit + box | Replaces EAN for FBA |
| 3PL fulfillment | EAN-13 + internal SKU | Product unit | Maintain for traceability |
| Pallet shipment | SSCC (18-digit) | Box exterior | For shipping/receiving |
| Multi-unit bundle | Individual FNSKU per unit | Each unit in bundle | All units labeled separately |
