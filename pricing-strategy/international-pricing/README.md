# International Pricing Strategy - Complete Reference

**Version 1.0** | Last Updated: April 2, 2026 | Status: Production Reference Document

## Overview

This is a comprehensive guide to international pricing strategy for European marketplace sellers. It covers pricing governance across major European marketplaces, purchasing power parity analysis, tariff impact calculations, Brexit considerations, and cross-border arbitrage strategies.

## Key Statistics

- **EU27 GDP per Capita Range**: €24,400 (Romania) to €96,000 (Luxembourg)
- **PPP Index Variance**: 61% (Romania) to 239% (Luxembourg), with Germany as 100% reference
- **Key Marketplaces Covered**: Amazon (6 countries), eBay, Kaufland, Bol.com, Cdiscount, Allegro
- **Tariff Rates**: 0% (Electronics, Toys) to 95% (Some food products); Anti-dumping duties up to 85%

### Pricing Decision Tree

**Question 1: Is your product lightweight and high-value?**
- Yes → Differentiate pricing by country (arbitrage risk)
- No → Consider uniform pricing (shipping cost barrier)

**Question 2: Are you selling >500 units/month?**
- Yes → Implement differentiated pricing (ROI justifies complexity)
- No → Uniform pricing acceptable (scale too small)

**Question 3: What is your product's PPP variance across target markets?**
- >120% range → Must differentiate
- 90-120% range → Consider regional zones
- <90% range → Uniform pricing acceptable
