## Clearance & Inventory Liquidation

### Inventory Aging Triggers

**Decision Framework**: Markdown strategy depends on how long inventory has been in stock.

#### 0-30 Days in Stock
- **Status**: Fresh inventory, early maturity phase
- **Action**: No discount; optimize for full-price sales
- **Tactics**:
  - Use for bundling (pair with complementary items or slow-movers)
  - Position as loss leader only if strategic (e.g., new marketplace entry)
  - Invest in advertising (PPC, brand visibility)
  - Create FAQ and detailed content (reduce return risk)
- **Margin Target**: 100% of normal margin
- **Velocity Target**: 3-5 units/day (healthy baseline)

#### 30-60 Days in Stock
- **Status**: Normal lifecycle; slight slowdown emerging
- **Action**: Light discount testing (3-5%)
- **Tactics**:
  - Clip coupon (Amazon) or limited-time promo code (email)
  - Flash sale (24-hour) to test demand at lower price
  - Monitor sell-through velocity
  - A/B test: 3% discount vs. 5% discount vs. no discount (small sample)
- **Margin Target**: 95-100% of normal margin
- **Velocity Target**: 4-7 units/day

#### 60-90 Days in Stock
- **Status**: Early stagnation; velocity declining
- **Action**: Moderate discounts (8-12%)
- **Tactics**:
  - Weekly flash sales (2-3 per week)
  - Heavy bundling (pair with faster-moving items)
  - Email campaigns (abandoned browsers, past purchasers)
  - Category reset (move to new collection, re-list with fresh photos)
- **Margin Target**: 85-95% of normal margin
- **Velocity Target**: 5-8 units/day (maintain from previous phase)

#### 90-180 Days in Stock
- **Status**: Problematic stagnation; high carry cost
- **Action**: Heavy discounts (15-25%)
- **Tactics**:
  - Daily flash sales (high-visibility push)
  - Deep bundling discounts (€10-20 off bundles)
  - Liquidation pricing begins (cost + minimal margin)
  - Evaluate FBA removal cost vs. continued carrying cost
- **Margin Target**: 60-80% of normal margin
- **Velocity Target**: 6-10 units/day (acceleration required)

#### 180+ Days in Stock
- **Status**: Critical deadstock; urgent liquidation needed
- **Action**: Liquidation pricing (30-50% off)
- **Tactics**:
  - Evaluate FBA removal cost vs. one-time fire sale
  - Consider donation for tax writeoff (if removal cost > residual value)
  - Discontinuation (stop restocking entirely)
  - Batch liquidate across product line (reduce per-SKU overhead)
- **Margin Target**: 0-40% of normal margin (or negative for donation)
- **Velocity Target**: All units out within 30 days

### FBA Removal vs. Liquidation Cost Analysis

**Scenario**: 100 units stuck in FBA warehouse, €50 per unit cost basis.

**Option A: Amazon FBA Removal**
- Removal fee: €0.50/unit (standard)
- Total cost: 100 × €0.50 = €50
- Timeline: 10 business days
- Result: Inventory removed; no further carrying cost
- Liquidity: €0 (no revenue)

**Option B: Liquidate at 40% Discount**
- Selling price: €50 × 0.60 = €30
- Revenue: 100 × €30 = €3,000
- COGS: 100 × €50 = €5,000
- Loss: €2,000
- FBA carrying cost (3 months): €150 (est.)
- Total cost: €2,000 loss + €150 carrying = €2,150

**Option C: Donate for Tax Writeoff**
- Donation value: Fair Market Value (FMV) €50 × 100 = €5,000
- Tax benefit (40% rate): €5,000 × 0.40 = €2,000 tax savings
- Removal cost: €50
- Net benefit: €2,000 - €50 - carrying cost (€150) = €1,800

**Option D: Liquidate at 50% Discount + Extended Timeline**
- Selling price: €50 × 0.50 = €25
- Revenue: 100 × €25 = €2,500
- COGS: 100 × €50 = €5,000
- Loss: €2,500
- FBA carrying cost (6 months, extended): €300
- Total cost: €2,500 loss + €300 carrying = €2,800

**Decision Matrix**:

| Option | Revenue | Cost | Net | Timeline | Risk |
|--------|---------|------|-----|----------|------|
| Removal | €0 | €50 | -€50 | 10 days | Low |
| Liquidate 40% | €3,000 | €2,150 | €850 loss | 30-90 days | Medium |
| Donate | €0 | -€1,800 (tax benefit) | €1,800 gain | 30 days | Low |
| Liquidate 50% | €2,500 | €2,800 | €300 loss | 60-180 days | High |

**Recommendation**: Donate if tax-advantaged entity (40%+ tax rate); otherwise liquidate at 40% discount if 30-day clearance possible, else remove.

**Key Events**:
- **Winter Sales (Soldes/Rebajas/Saldi)**: Strictly regulated in FR, ES, IT (legal mandate)
- **New Year Resolution**: Health, fitness, productivity categories peak
- **Budget Balance**: Corporate spend from prior year

- **France**: January 2nd Wednesday through 5 weeks (typically Jan 12 - Feb 15)

## Promotional Lift Measurement & ROI

### Defining the Right Metrics

**Mistake**: Confusing Revenue Lift with Profit Lift

**Revenue Lift**: Total sales increase during promotional period
- Formula: (Promo Period Sales) - (Baseline Period Sales)
- **Problem**: Ignores margin reduction from discount

**Profit Lift**: Margin-adjusted revenue increase accounting for discount cost
- Formula: ((Promo Sales Margin) - (Baseline Sales Margin)) + (Baseline Period Profit × 0% because promo cannibalizes)
- **Correct approach**: Use this for ROI decision-making

**Example** (€100 product, 40% baseline margin):
- Baseline: 100 units @ €100 = €10,000 revenue, €4,000 profit (40%)
- Promotion: 150 units @ €80 (20% discount) = €12,000 revenue, €3,000 profit (25%)
- **Revenue lift**: €2,000 (20% increase) ✓
- **Profit lift**: -€1,000 (25% DECREASE) ✗

**Correct Interpretation**: Despite 20% revenue increase, profit dropped 25% due to margin compression. Promotion is unprofitable if the 50 incremental units are NOT incremental (if 30 came from pull-forward effect).

### Incremental Sales & Pull-Forward Effect

**Definition**: 
- **Incremental Sales**: Truly new demand (customer wouldn't have bought without promotion)
- **Pull-Forward Effect**: Customer moves purchase earlier (same customer who would have bought later)
- **Cannibalization**: Customer substitutes higher-margin product for promotional product

**Calculation Method**:

1. **Establish Baseline**: Measure non-promotional period (same season, previous year)
   - Past October (non-promotional) = 100 units/day × 30 days = 3,000 units

2. **Run Promotion**: Flash sale at 20% discount
   - October (promotional) = 250 units/day × 30 days = 7,500 units
   - Gross lift = 4,500 units

3. **Measure Post-Promotion**: November (immediately after promotion)
   - November (post-promotional) = 60 units/day × 30 days = 1,800 units
   - Baseline November = 100 units/day × 30 days = 3,000 units
   - Pull-forward loss = 1,200 units

4. **Calculate Incremental**:
   - Gross lift: 4,500 units
   - Pull-forward (lost in next month): 1,200 units
   - True incremental: 4,500 - 1,200 = 3,300 units
   - Incremental %: 3,300 / 4,500 = 73% of observed lift is truly incremental

**Profit Calculation** (€50 product, 35% margin, 20% discount):

| Metric | Value | Calculation |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Baseline Oct Profit | €3,150 | 3,000 units × €50 × 35% |
| Baseline Nov Profit | €3,150 | 3,000 units × €50 × 35% |
| **Baseline 2-Month Total** | **€6,300** | — |
| Promo Oct Revenue | €7,500 | 7,500 units × €40 (20% discount) |
| Promo Oct Margin % | 28% | 35% - 7% (discount impact) |
| Promo Oct Profit | €2,100 | €7,500 × 28% |
| Promo Nov Profit | €1,260 | 1,800 units × €50 × 35% |
| **Promo 2-Month Total** | **€3,360** | — |
| **Profit Loss** | **-€2,940** | €6,300 - €3,360 |

**Interpretation**: Despite 250% units lift in October (3,000 → 7,500), total 2-month profit DECREASED by €2,940 because:
1. Margin compressed 28% (from 35%) due to discount
2. Pull-forward effect stole 40% of incremental sales from November

**Conclusion**: Promotion is unprofitable unless something else justified it (market share gain, new customer acquisition, clearance deadline).

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) vs. Lifetime Value (LTV) Analysis

**Framework**: Promotional customers have different LTV profiles than organic customers.

**Scenario**: New customer via discount promotion vs. organic discovery

**Promotional Customer Path**:
- Acquired via flash sale (€50 product, 20% discount = €40 first purchase)
- Initial margin: 28% (€11.20)
- CAC: €5 (advertising cost to drive to flash sale)
- **Net CAC**: €5 - €11.20 = -€6.20 (profitable on first purchase)

**LTV Calculation** (Assumptions: 25% repeat purchase rate, €50 avg order value, 3 years horizon):

| Month | Repeat Rate | Orders | Revenue | Margin (35%) | Margin Dollars | Cumulative |
|-------|------------|--------|---------|-------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Month 1 | 0% | 0 | €0 | €0 | €0 | €0 |
| Month 3 | 25% | 1 | €50 | 35% | €17.50 | €17.50 |
| Month 6 | 15% of repeat | 1 | €50 | 35% | €17.50 | €35 |
| Month 12 | 10% of repeat | 2 | €100 | 35% | €35 | €70 |
| Year 2-3 | 8% annual | 3 | €150 | 35% | €52.50 | €122.50 |
| **LTV** | — | **~7 orders** | **€350** | **35% avg** | **€122.50** | — |

**LTV vs. CAC**:
- LTV: €122.50 (3-year margin)
- CAC: €5
- **LTV:CAC Ratio**: 24.5:1 (healthy; >3:1 is breakeven)

**Organic Customer Path** (Discovered via search, no promo):
- First purchase: €50 product at full margin (35% = €17.50)
- CAC: €0 (organic search, no advertising)
- **Net CAC**: €0 - €17.50 = -€17.50 (more profitable on first purchase)

**LTV Calculation** (Higher repeat rate due to premium-tier organic acquisition):

| Metric | Promotional | Organic | Difference |
|--------|------------|---------|-----------|
| Repeat Rate (Year 1) | 25% | 35% | +10pp |
| Avg LTV (3 years) | €122.50 | €145 | +€22.50 |
| CAC | €5 | €0 | -€5 |
| LTV:CAC Ratio | 24.5:1 | Infinite* | — |

*Organic CAC = 0, so ratio is infinite (organic is most profitable)

**Strategic Implication**: Promotional customers have similar LTV to organic customers (€122.50 vs €145, only 18% difference), suggesting promotions are valuable for volume but don't generate more valuable customers long-term.

### Multi-Touch Attribution Methodology

**Challenge**: Customer may see promotion across multiple channels before purchase.

**Scenario**: Customer journey across 4 weeks:
1. Week 1: Sees Instagram ad (influencer code)
2. Week 2: Email newsletter arrives with promo code
3. Week 3: Sees Google search ad (retargeting)
4. Week 4: Completes purchase via email link from newsletter

**Attribution Models**:

#### Last-Touch Attribution (Simplest)
- Attribution: 100% to email (Week 4)
- **Limitation**: Ignores all prior touchpoints that built consideration

#### First-Touch Attribution
- Attribution: 100% to Instagram (Week 1)
- **Limitation**: Ignores email and search that drove final conversion

#### Linear Attribution (Fair Share)
- Attribution: 25% each (Instagram, Email #1, Search, Email #2)
- **Limitation**: Treats all touchpoints equally (earlier ones less valuable)

#### Time Decay Attribution (Realistic)
- Assign increasing weight closer to purchase:
  - Week 1 (Instagram): 15%
  - Week 2 (Email #1): 20%
  - Week 3 (Search): 25%
  - Week 4 (Email #2): 40%
- **Advantage**: Acknowledges final touchpoints more valuable in conversion path

**Implementation**:

Track each promotion channel separately and assign credit based on time decay:

| Channel | Week 1 | Week 2 | Week 3 | Week 4 | Attribution Credit | Orders Attributed | ROI |
|---------|--------|--------|--------|--------|-------------------|------------------|-----|
| Instagram | 15% | — | — | — | 15% | 45 orders | +300% |
| Email (Newsletter) | — | 20% | — | 40% | 60% | 180 orders | +450% |
| Google Search | — | — | 25% | — | 25% | 75 orders | +200% |
| **Total** | — | — | — | — | **100%** | **300 orders** | **+350% avg** |

### ROI Calculation by Promotion Type

**Formula**: 
```
ROI = (Revenue Increase × Margin %) - Direct Costs / Direct Costs × 100%
```

**Example: Amazon Flash Sale**

- **Setup**: 100 units, €50 ASP, 30% discount (€35 sale price), 35% normal margin
- **Costs**:
  - Platform fee: €0 (Amazon absorbs)
  - Advertising (push): €50 (internal email blast)
  - Margin loss: (€35 × 25% margin) vs. (€50 × 35% margin) = -€5 per unit
  - Total margin loss: 100 units × €5 = €500
  - **Total cost**: €50 + €500 = €550

- **Revenue**:
  - Flash sale: 100 units × €35 = €3,500
  - Halo effect (30 additional post-flash sales at full price): 30 × €50 = €1,500
  - **Total revenue**: €5,000

- **Profit**:
  - Flash sale margin: 100 units × €35 × 25% = €875
  - Halo effect margin: 30 units × €50 × 35% = €525
  - Total margin: €1,400
  - Net profit: €1,400 - €550 = €850

- **ROI**: (€850 / €550) × 100% = 154% ROI

**2-Month Horizon (Accounting for Pull-Forward)**:
- Baseline 2 months: 200 units × €50 × 35% = €3,500 profit
- Flash sale month: €875 margin (flash) + €525 (halo) = €1,400
- Post-flash month: 70 units (30 less pull-forward) × €50 × 35% = €1,225
- **Total flash sale 2-month**: €1,400 + €1,225 = €2,625
- **Loss vs. baseline**: €3,500 - €2,625 = €875 opportunity cost

**Corrected ROI**: (€850 - €875) / €550 = -4.5% (NEGATIVE after accounting for pull-forward)

**Conclusion**: Flash sale appears profitable at +154% if ignoring pull-forward, but becomes unprofitable (-4.5%) when accounting for customer timing shift.

## Conclusion: Promotional Strategy Framework

### Decision Tree: Should We Discount?

```
START: Margin >40%?
├─ YES → Discount up to 15%; focus on velocity
│   └─ ASP > €30? → Clip coupon strategy
│   └─ ASP < €30? → Flash sale or bundle strategy
├─ NO → Margin 20-40%?
    ├─ YES → Discount up to 10%; focus on incremental
    │   └─ Inventory aging >60 days? → Yes, discount
    │   └─ Inventory aging <60 days? → No, reposition
    ├─ NO → Margin <20%?
        ├─ YES → Clearance only; liquidate aggressively
        └─ NO → Don't discount; consider discontinuation
```
