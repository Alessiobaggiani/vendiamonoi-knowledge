## Legal Requirements by Country

### France
- **Soldes Dates**: Strictly regulated (January: 2nd Wednesday + 5 weeks; Summer: 4th Wednesday June + 5 weeks)
- **Discount Floor**: Must reduce from reference price established in preceding 30 days
- **Labeling**: Mandatory % discount display on shelf or digital listing
- **Budget Impact**: 20-25% of annual promotional budget (official budget week)

### Spain
- **Rebajas Dates**: Strictly regulated (January: 1st-31st; July: 1st-31st)
- **Signage**: Must prominently display discount % or amount
- **Price Comparison**: Must show "from X€ to Y€" format
- **Budget Impact**: 15-20% of annual promotional budget

### Italy
- **Saldi Dates**: Strictly regulated (Winter: 2nd Saturday January + 60 days; Summer: 1st Saturday July + 60 days)
- **Discount Floor**: Legal minimum 10% discount from regular price
- **Display**: Physical store signage rules strictly enforced; digital must match
- **Budget Impact**: 18-22% of annual promotional budget

### Germany
- **Flexible Dates**: No legal restrictions on clearance sales
- **Consumer Rights**: 14-day return period applies to sale items
- **Advertising**: "Was/Now" pricing requires 4-week reference period
- **Budget Impact**: 12-15% of annual promotional budget (discretionary timing)

### United Kingdom
- **Flexible Dates**: No legal restrictions on clearance sales
- **Consumer Rights**: No "final sale" clauses allowed; 30-day return rights
- **Price Labeling**: Must show original price clearly for 28+ days before sale
- **Budget Impact**: 12-15% of annual promotional budget

### Netherlands
- **Flexible Dates**: No legal restrictions; can use "uitverkoop" year-round
- **Advertising**: Must show reference price from preceding 30 days
- **Consumer Rights**: Return policies apply to sale items
- **Budget Impact**: 10-12% of annual promotional budget

### Belgium
- **Flexible Dates**: No legal restrictions on promotional timing
- **Labeling**: Must clearly show discount % on digital and physical listings
- **Price Comparison**: Must link reduction to established reference price
- **Budget Impact**: 10-12% of annual promotional budget

## Critical Decision Thresholds

### Coupon Use
- **ASP < €15**: Avoid clip coupons (fee negates margin). Use flash sales, bundles, or Subscribe & Save instead.
- **ASP €15-30**: Clip coupons viable at 5-8% discount; test Newsletter Promo Codes.
- **ASP > €30**: Clip coupons effective; consider tiered coupons (€5 off €30+, €10 off €75+).

### Discount Depth
- **Margin > 40%**: Can sustain 10-15% discounts while maintaining profit.
- **Margin 20-40%**: Limit to 5-10% discounts; pair with bundles or flash sales.
- **Margin < 20%**: Clearance only; use markdown schedules, avoid ongoing discounts.

### Inventory Aging
- **< 30 days in stock**: Hold pricing, use for bundling or loss leaders.
- **30-60 days**: Light discounts (3-5%), test flash sales, push on high-visibility.
- **60-90 days**: Moderate discounts (8-12%), weekly flash sales, bundle heavily.
- **90-180 days**: Heavy discounts (15-25%), daily flash sales, consider FBA removal cost vs. sell-through.
- **> 180 days**: Liquidation pricing (30-50%), FBA removal cost analysis, donation writeoff evaluation.

### ROI Thresholds
- **>5:1 ROAS**: Expand budget and frequency
- **3-5:1 ROAS**: Maintain current spend; test incremental volume
- **2-3:1 ROAS**: Reduce discount depth; shift to bundles or limited flash sales
- **<2:1 ROAS**: Stop ongoing promotion; move to clearance-only strategy or FBA removal
