## SECTION 5: EVIDENCE COLLECTION & DOCUMENTATION

### 5.1 What to Document (Complete Checklist)

**Pre-Shipment Documentation:**
```
□ Item photos (4 angles minimum)
□ QC inspection form (signed, dated)
□ Serial number/batch number recorded
□ Packaging integrity check
□ Weight & dimensions verification
□ Condition comparison to listing
□ Defect notation (if any)
□ Storage condition log (temperature/humidity)
```

**Shipment Documentation:**
```
□ Packing slip (itemized, signed)
□ Packaging material photos (bubble wrap, boxes)
□ Sealed package photo (before carrier pickup)
□ Carrier tracking number (recorded)
□ Proof of pickup from warehouse
□ Shipping label photo (legible)
□ Carrier selection rationale (speed/insurance)
□ Insurance documentation (if applicable)
```

**Delivery Documentation:**
```
□ Tracking updates (screenshot, timestamp)
□ Delivery confirmation (photo + signature)
□ Photo of package at delivery location
□ Recipient name verification
□ Timestamp correlation (delivery time vs. claim)
□ Return label (if applicable)
□ Carrier signature requirement (enforced)
```

**Communication Documentation:**
```
□ All buyer messages (screenshot or export)
□ Seller responses (time-stamped)
□ Pre-delivery notifications (automated)
□ Post-delivery follow-ups
□ Response to complaints (archived)
□ Offer/resolution proposals (dated)
□ Declining offers (buyer's choice, not seller)
```

## SECTION 6: ESCALATION MANAGEMENT

### 6.1 Internal Decision Matrix

**When to Respond vs. Refund:**

| Claim Type | Amount | Evidence | Decision |
|-----------|--------|----------|----------|
| Non-receipt | <€50 | No tracking | Refund |
| Non-receipt | €50-200 | Signed delivery | Defend (contest) |
| Non-receipt | >€200 | Signed delivery | Escalate to legal |
| SNAD | <€75 | Photo poor | Partial refund (10-15%) |
| SNAD | €75-300 | Photo clear | Defend + offer 5% |
| SNAD | >€300 | Photo clear | Legal review |
| Defect | <€100 | Internal QC log | Partial refund (20%) |
| Defect | €100-400 | QC + warranty | Defend + warranty claim |
| Defect | >€400 | Unknown | Escalate (legal) |

**Decision Protocol:**
1. **Immediate (within 24 hours):** All Amazon A-to-Z claims
2. **Quick (within 48 hours):** eBay escalations, PayPal chargebacks
3. **Review (2-3 days):** Bol.com, Cdiscount, Fnac
4. **Legal (5+ days):** >€500 claims, repeated buyer patterns, counterfeit allegations

### 6.2 Marketplace Arbitration vs. Civil Court

**Marketplace Arbitration Process:**

| Stage | Marketplace | Timeline | Cost | Appeal |
|-------|-------------|----------|------|--------|
| Submission | Amazon/eBay/PayPal | Immediate | €0 | Limited |
| Investigation | Marketplace | 5-30 days | €0 | Once, within 30 days |
| Decision | Automated/Manual | Day 30-60 | €0 | No appeal possible |
| Enforcement | Buyer/Seller follow through | Ongoing | Variable | N/A |

**When to Escalate to Civil Court:**

```
Escalate if:
✓ Claim >€1,000 AND dispute decision unfavorable
✓ Repeated pattern of disputes from same buyer (fraud suspicion)
✓ Counterfeit allegations (IP rights involved)
✓ Damages exceed marketplace limits
✓ Chargeback + marketplace loss (double-dipping)
✗ Do NOT escalate if claim <€500 (legal costs exceed recovery)
```

### 6.3 EU Small Claims Procedure

**EU Small Claims (up to €5,000):**

**Advantages:**
- No legal representation required
- Simplified procedures
- Faster resolution (4-8 months vs. 2-3 years)
- Lower costs (€20-100 filing)
- Cross-border enforcement

**Timeline:**
```
Day 0: File claim (online or paper form)
Day 30-60: Defendant responds
Day 90-120: Court decision (paper-based, no hearing)
Day 150+: Enforcement (varies by country)
```

**Evidence Requirements (Same as marketplace):**
- Tracking confirmation
- Communication logs
- Photos/video
- Warranty/specification documents

**Cost-Benefit:**
```
Claim: €800 (defective item, lost)
Legal cost: €200 (filing + admin)
Success probability: 70% (with strong evidence)
Expected recovery: €560 (€800 × 70%)
Net: €360 gain

NOT VIABLE: Claims <€300 (legal cost exceeds recovery)
MARGINAL: Claims €300-600 (1:1 cost-benefit)
VIABLE: Claims >€600 (clear cost advantage)
```

### 6.4 Involving Legal Counsel

**When to Involve Legal:**

**Immediate Legal Review:**
- Counterfeit accusations (IP infringement risk)
- Fraud pattern (repeat disputes, chargeback fraud)
- Criminal accusations (items harmful, illegal)
- Regulatory compliance issues (product safety)

**Legal Department Actions:**
1. Case file review (5-10 hours)
2. Evidence assessment (2-5 hours)
3. Decision recommendation
4. Court filing preparation (if needed)
5. Representation (if escalated to court)

**Cost Structure:**
```
Small case review: €200-400 (30-min consultation)
Full defense preparation: €800-1,500 (5-10 hours)
Civil court representation: €2,000-5,000+ (contingency or hourly)
```

**Justification Threshold:**
- Involve legal only if claim >€1,000 OR pattern fraud OR IP issue
- Routine disputes: Handled by seller operations team

## SECTION 7: PREVENTION STRATEGIES

### 7.1 Proactive Communication Framework

**Pre-Purchase Communication:**

```
Within product listing:
□ Detailed measurements (L×W×H cm)
□ Weight specification (exact kg)
□ Color/material variations shown (all angles)
□ Known limitations disclosed ("may have minor surface marks")
□ Return policy clearly stated (14 days, buyer pays return shipping)
□ Warranty period specified (12 months, covers manufacturing defects only)
□ Assembly required? Clearly indicated
```

## SECTION 8: SELLER METRICS & ACCOUNT HEALTH

### 8.1 ODR (Order Defect Rate) — Amazon

**ODR Formula:**
```
ODR % = (Negative feedback + A-to-Z + Chargeback + Returns) / Total Orders (90 days) × 100
Target: <1% (Amazon threshold)
Danger: >1.5% (suspension risk)
```

**Calculation Example:**
```
Scenario: 5,000 orders in 90 days
- A-to-Z claims: 8 (4 lost, 4 won)
- Chargebacks: 3
- Negative feedback: 2
- Disputed returns: 1

ODR = (8 + 3 + 2 + 1) / 5,000 = 14 / 5,000 = 0.28%
Status: EXCELLENT (well below 1% threshold)
```

**Status Breakdown:**
```
<0.5%: Excellent (no consequences, incentive programs eligible)
0.5-0.8%: Good (amber warning possible, monitor closely)
0.8-1.0%: At Risk (performance improvement plan required)
1.0-1.5%: Critical (buy box suspension, limited to 1-3 days)
>1.5%: Account Suspension (14-30 day pause, possible closing)
```

**Recovery Path (from 1.2% to <1%):**
```
Current: 1,000 orders, 12 defects = 1.2% (suspended)

Month 1: 900 orders, 6 defects (0.67%)
Month 2: 900 orders, 5 defects (0.56%)
Month 3: 900 orders, 4 defects (0.44%)

After 90-day period: Average ~0.56% = SAFE
Timeline to recovery: 90+ days
```

### 8.2 Defect Rate — eBay

**eBay Defect Rate Calculation:**
```
Defect Rate % = Cases closed in buyer's favor / Total transactions (90 days) × 100
Target: <1% for Top Rated status
Critical: >2% (Top Rated loss, fee increase)
```

**Top Rated Benefits (requires <1%):**
- Final value fee reduction: **20% discount** (major financial impact)
- Search prominence increase: +25-40% visibility
- "Top Rated Plus" badge (buyer confidence signal)
- Buyer confidence → higher conversion rate

**Cost of Losing Top Rated Status:**
```

- Top Rated FVF: 9.2% = €4,600/month
- Monthly savings: €1,850

Loss of Top Rated = €1,850 × 12 = €22,200/year
```

**Defect Rate Reduction Strategy:**


**Success Rate:** 65-75% approval rate with strong appeal.

**If Appeal Denied:**

