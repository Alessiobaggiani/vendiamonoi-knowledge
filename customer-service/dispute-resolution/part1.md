## SECTION 1: AMAZON A-TO-Z GUARANTEE CLAIMS

### 1.1 Overview & Triggers

The Amazon A-to-Z Guarantee protects buyers from non-receipt or item defect. As seller, you have **3 calendar days** to respond from claim opening.

**Common Triggers:**
- Item not received (despite delivery confirmation)
- Item not as described (quality, color, damage)
- Item significantly smaller than expected
- Missing accessories/components
- Expired or counterfeit products
- Major defects affecting functionality

**Claim Rate Benchmark:** Industry average 0.3-0.8% of orders. Target: <0.5% for account health.

### 1.2 Timeline & Response Requirements

| Timeline | Action | Owner | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|-------|
| Day 0 | Buyer opens claim | Buyer | 60-day window from order date |
| Day 0-1 | Amazon notifies seller | Amazon | Email to registered account |
| Day 1-3 | Seller response window | Seller | **CRITICAL: 3-day deadline** |
| Day 4 | Auto-escalation to case | Amazon | If no seller response |
| Day 4-7 | Resolution attempt | Amazon/Buyer | Buyer can provide evidence |
| Day 8-20 | Amazon investigates | Amazon | Contact seller for evidence |
| Day 21 | Final determination | Amazon | Refund or appeal decision |

**Response Strategy:**
- Day 1: Acknowledge claim within 24 hours
- Day 2: Submit full evidence package
- Day 3: Follow up if no Amazon response

### 1.3 Evidence Required (Winning Approach)

**For Non-Receipt Claims:**
```
✓ Carrier tracking (FedEx/DHL/PostNL tracking number)
✓ Proof of delivery (photo, signature, timestamp)
✓ Customs clearance documents (if international)
✓ Return label if item marked undeliverable
✓ Communication logs showing carrier updates
✓ Signature confirmation from delivery
```

**For Item Not As Described:**
```
✓ Product listing screenshots (exact wording, images)
✓ Product condition photos from warehouse
✓ QC report (internal documentation)
✓ Batch/serial number verification
✓ Manufacturing specifications
✓ Communication with buyer showing issue
✓ Response to buyer's specific complaint
```

**For Defect/Damage Claims:**
```
✓ Packaging photos (pre-shipment condition)
✓ Tracking photos at each stage
✓ Final delivery condition photos
✓ Warehouse QC records
✓ Product specifications vs. buyer's report
✓ Technical analysis if defect claimed
✓ Buyer's photographic evidence assessment
```

### 1.4 Win Strategy by Claim Type

**Non-Receipt (60% of cases):**
- Proof of delivery + signature = near-certain win
- Track.trace.id + carrier confirmation = 85% win rate
- Carrier liability shift = Amazon releases funds to seller

**Item Not As Described (25% of cases):**
- Listing accuracy is KEY
- Screenshot original listing with exact claims
- Photo documentation from warehouse crucial
- Buyer's photos often show poor handling (your advantage)
- Win rate: 65-75% with strong photo documentation

**Damaged Goods (10% of cases):**
- Internal packaging standards documentation
- Photos of item before shipment = 90% win rate
- Professional packaging = credibility boost
- Carrier damage claims can be filed simultaneously

**Defective/Counterfeit (5% of cases):**
- Authenticity certificates (for brands)
- Manufacturing specifications
- Technical testing results
- Batch verification from supplier
- Win rate: 40-60% (most difficult)

### 1.5 Appeal Process

**When to Appeal:**
- Factual errors in Amazon decision
- Missing evidence in original response
- New carrier evidence (delivery proof)
- Buyer's evidence contradicts their own photos

**Appeal Submission:**
1. Navigate to "Your Seller Account" > "Performance" > "A-to-Z Guarantee Cases"
2. Select case > "Appeal Decision"
3. Upload NEW evidence only (previously submitted evidence rarely changes outcome)
4. Focus on 1-2 strongest points (don't rehash)
5. Keep appeal to <500 words

**Appeal Success Rate:** 25-40% (very low—appeals rarely overturn)

**Better Strategy:** Request Amazon review within 30 days if:
- Carrier later confirms delivery to buyer
- Buyer acknowledges receipt in feedback
- New technical evidence surfaces

### 1.6 ODR (Order Defect Rate) Impact

**ODR Calculation:**
```
ODR = (A-to-Z + Chargeback + Credit Card Reversals) / Total Orders in 90 days
Target: <1% (Amazon policy ceiling: 1%)
Danger Zone: >1.5% = account suspension risk
```

**A-to-Z Weight:** Each claim counts as 1 defect, regardless of outcome.

**Impact Threshold:**
- <0.5%: Green status (no consequences)
- 0.5-0.8%: Yellow flag (performance warnings)
- 0.8-1.0%: Red alert (buy box restrictions likely)
- >1.0%: Account suspension, forced closing

**Example:** 1,000 orders, 12 A-to-Z claims = 1.2% ODR = immediate action required

## SECTION 2: EBAY MONEY BACK GUARANTEE

### 2.1 Resolution Process Overview

eBay's Money Back Guarantee covers:
- Item not received (confirmed by tracking)
- Item not as described
- Significantly not as described (SNAD)

**Process Flow:**
```
Buyer opens case → 48-hour resolution option → Escalation → eBay investigation
```

**Critical Difference from Amazon:** eBay gives 48 hours for RESOLUTION, not just response.

### 2.2 Buyer Cases — Managing Resolution

**Stage 1: Resolution (48 hours)**

Seller actions:
1. Refund + return (most effective)
2. Refund without return (for small items <€25)
3. Replacement item
4. Partial refund (offer, buyer accepts or escalates)

**Win Strategy:** Offer partial refund (10-20%) immediately
- 65% of cases close without escalation
- Saves time, preserves metrics
- Buyer satisfaction: High

**Never Ignore:** Auto-escalation to eBay case management occurs after 48 hours.

### 2.3 Escalation & eBay Case Management

**Escalation Timeline:**
| Stage | Duration | Seller Action |
|-------|----------|---------------|
| Resolution | 48 hours | Offer resolution |
| Escalation | 24 hours | Submit evidence |
| Investigation | 5-10 days | Await decision |
| Final | Immediate | Implement decision |

**Evidence Submission (Escalation):**
- Tracking confirmation + delivery proof
- Photo documentation of item condition
- Communication logs (itemized)
- Response to specific buyer allegations
- Marketplace message history

**eBay Decision Factors:**
- Tracking > refunds offered (if tracking shows delivery, seller usually wins)
- Communication quality (responsive sellers: +20% win advantage)
- Account history (PowerSellers: slight preference, ~5%)
- Case specificity (detailed claims > vague complaints)

### 2.4 Defect Rate & Top Rated Seller Impact

**eBay Defect Rate Calculation:**
```
Defect Rate = Cases closed in buyer's favor / Total transactions (90 days)
Target: <1% (eBay requirement for top rated status)
Critical: >2% = demoted status, losing top rated benefits
```

**Top Rated Benefits (requires <1% defect):**
- 20% final value fee reduction (major cost benefit)
- Search prominence +25-40%
- "Top Rated Plus" badge (buyer confidence)
- Access to advance seller tools

**Cost of Losing Top Rated:**
- 20% fee increase on all items = €2,000-5,000/month for medium seller
- Lost search visibility = 15-20% sales decline
- Buyer confidence impact = lower conversion

### 2.5 Defect Prevention & Top Rated Protection

**Proactive Measures:**
1. **Detailed Item Descriptions**
   - Exact dimensions (L x W x H in cm)
   - Condition clarity (New, Like New, Good, Fair)
   - Known flaws disclosed upfront
   - Return policy crystal clear

2. **High-Quality Photos**
   - 8-12 photos minimum
   - Zoom capability (close-ups)
   - Damage/wear clearly shown
   - Size comparison (coin, hand reference)

3. **Expedited Shipping**
   - 2-3 day delivery = fewer loss claims
   - Tracking update frequency improves confidence
   - Carrier insurance recommended

4. **Buyer Communication**
   - Post-purchase message within 24 hours
   - Proactive tracking notification
   - "Any questions?" offer

## SECTION 3: PAYPAL & PAYMENT DISPUTES

### 3.1 Chargeback Process & Timeline

**Chargeback Definition:** Buyer disputes credit card charge with their bank, not PayPal.

**Timeline:**
| Day | Event | Seller Window |
|-----|-------|---------------|
| 0 | Buyer initiates chargeback with bank | None (no notification) |
| 0-45 | Bank investigation period | No involvement |
| 45-60 | PayPal notified of chargeback | 10 days to respond |
| 55-65 | Seller evidence window | **CRITICAL: 10-day deadline** |
| 65-180 | Bank final decision | Waiting period |
| 180+ | Settlement | Funds deducted from PayPal balance |

**Key Risk:** Chargebacks bypass PayPal's system entirely—direct bank dispute.

### 3.2 Section 75 Protection (UK/EU)

**Section 75 (UK Consumer Credit Act):**
- Credit card issuer is equally liable with merchant
- Maximum claim: £20,000
- Applies when item cost £100-20,000
- Time limit: 6 years
- EU equivalent: Consumer Rights Directive Art. 74-77

**How Buyers Weaponize This:**
```
Buyer buys €500 item via credit card
→ Files chargeback claiming "not received"
→ Gets refund from bank
→ Files PayPal dispute (double-dipping)
→ Seller loses twice
```

**Protection Strategy:**
- Use PayPal Goods & Services (seller protection)
- Signature-required delivery mandatory (€10-20 cost, worth it)
- Proof of delivery is non-negotiable for >€100 orders

### 3.3 Chargeback Evidence & Representment

**Winning Evidence Package:**

```
For "Unauthorized Transaction":
✓ Transaction ID + timestamp
✓ Cardholder IP verification
✓ Buyer's eBay/PayPal purchase history
✓ Buyer's previous payment methods
✓ Delivery confirmation (signed)
✓ Invoice matching buyer address
Win Rate: 85%+ (very defendable)

For "Item Not Received":
✓ Signed delivery proof (non-negotiable)
✓ Photo of delivery location
✓ Carrier confirmation + tracking
✓ Insurance documentation
✓ Signature release form
Win Rate: 90%+ (strongest defense)

For "Item Not As Described":
✓ Transaction record
✓ Listing screenshot (exact wording)
✓ Item photos from warehouse
✓ Communication with buyer
✓ Buyer's photographic evidence
✓ Technical specifications
Win Rate: 45-60% (weakest, banks favor buyer)
```

**Representment Timing:**
- PayPal notifies: Day 55-60
- Seller submits evidence: Day 60-65
- Bank receives: Day 70
- Decision: Day 120-180

**Winning Strategy:**
1. **Signed delivery is absolute requirement** (€10 cost prevents €500+ loss)
2. **Respond within 72 hours** of PayPal notification
3. **Provide 3-5 strongest pieces** (not 50 files)
4. **Focus on signature + photo** for non-receipt
5. **Address buyer's specific claim** with counter-evidence

### 3.4 Chargeback Costs & Prevention

**Direct Costs:**
- Chargeback fee: €20-50 per case (PayPal)
- Lost merchandise: Full item cost
- Shipping already paid: Non-recoverable
- Refund processing: Included

**Indirect Costs:**
- Account flag: Chargeback count tracked
- Processing delays: 60-180 days (cash flow impact)
- Psychological toll: 2-4 hours per case investigation
- Risk escalation: Multiple chargebacks = account review

**Annual Impact Example (Medium Seller):**
```
100 chargebacks/year × €35 average cost = €3,500
+ Lost merchandise value: €25,000
+ Staff time (200 hours × €15) = €3,000
= €31,500 annual chargeback cost

Signature requirement investment: €3,000/year (100 chargebacks × €30 signature)
= €28,500 net savings (91% reduction)
```

**Prevention Hierarchy:**
1. **Signature required** (reduces chargebacks 85%)
2. **Insurance on high-value** (€100+ orders)
3. **Communication logs** (proactive messaging)
4. **Photo documentation** (pre-shipment, post-shipment)
5. **Delivery alerts** (automated buyer updates)

## SECTION 4: MARKETPLACE-SPECIFIC DISPUTE SYSTEMS

### 4.1 Bol.com (Netherlands/Belgium/France)

**Dispute System:** "Return & Refund" + "Buyer Complaint"

**Timeline:**
- Buyer opens complaint: Up to 30 days post-delivery
- Seller response: 2 business days
- Bol.com investigation: 10 business days
- Final decision: Immediate

**Winning Evidence:**
```
✓ Tracking (PostNL, DPD timestamps)
✓ Photo of product condition
✓ Video unboxing (if available from customer)
✓ Communication logs (Bol messaging only)
✓ Batch/serial numbers for defects
```

**Bol.com Advantage:** Values photo documentation heavily
**Bol.com Disadvantage:** Slow response to appeals

**Defect Rate Impact:** >2% = suspension risk. Bol.com tolerates higher dispute rates (up to 2.5%) than Amazon.

### 4.2 Kaufland (Germany/Romania/Czech Republic)

**Dispute System:** "Return Request" + "Complaint"

**Process:**
- Returns handled through Kaufland, not direct
- Kaufland inspects returned item
- Seller can only respond to complaint details
- No direct evidence submission possible

**Strategy:** 
- Respond quickly to complaint description
- Request Kaufland's inspection findings
- Appeal only if Kaufland didn't follow inspection protocol

**Evidence Submission:** Only through written response; no photo upload capability.

**Advantage:** Kaufland inspects before refund = fewer false claims win
**Disadvantage:** Seller has minimal control over evidence

### 4.3 Cdiscount (France)

**Dispute System:** "Resolution" + "Escalation"

**Timeline:**
- Buyer opens: 30 days post-order
- Seller responds: 48 hours (critical deadline)
- Investigation: 15 days
- Decision: 5 days after investigation

**Winning Strategy:**
```
Within 48 hours, offer:
1. Full refund (if genuine defect)
2. Partial refund 15-20% (if SNAD claim)
3. Replacement (if item is replaceable)
= 70% resolution rate without escalation
```

**Escalation Evidence:**
- Tracking + signature confirmation
- Product photos from warehouse
- Communication showing offer made

**Cdiscount Behavior:** Strongly encourages settlement. Escalations rarely reviewed favorably.

### 4.4 Allegro (Poland)

**Dispute System:** "Buyer Protection" claim

**Process:**
- Buyer files claim within 14 days of order
- Seller has 7 days to respond/resolve
- Allegro mediation: 30 days
- Appeal to Allegro: 14 days

**Evidence Required:**
```
✓ Tracking with timestamp
✓ Photos (marketplace messaging only)
✓ Transaction record
✓ Response message (Allegro system only)
```

**Critical Rule:** All communication must be in Allegro system. External emails not recognized.

**Win Rate:** 60% with tracking + timely response. Allegro less protective of sellers than Amazon.

### 4.5 ManoMano (France/Spain/Germany)

**Dispute System:** "Non-Conformity Claim"

**Timeline:**
- Claim period: 30 days from delivery
- Seller response: 5 business days
- Resolution: 30 days

**Special Rule:** ManoMano handles logistics for certain sellers—if using ManoMano warehouse:
- ManoMano is responsible for condition
- Seller liability limited to accuracy of description
- Photos mandatory for all SNAD claims

**Evidence:**
```
✓ Photos of item unboxing
✓ Specification sheet
✓ Communication log
✓ Installation/setup confirmation (if applicable)
```

**Advantage:** ManoMano photo requirement cuts false claims significantly.

### 4.6 Fnac-Darty (France)

**Dispute System:** "Complaint & Resolution"

**Timeline:**
- Claim: 30 days
- Seller response: 3 days
- Investigation: 14 days

**Fnac Specificity:** Electronics/media retailer—defect claims more likely
- Requires manufacturer warranty proof
- Technical specifications comparison critical
- Buyer's test photos heavily weighted

**Winning Evidence:**
```
✓ Warranty documentation
✓ Specifications vs. claim
✓ QC test results
✓ Shipping condition photos
✓ Technical analysis of claimed defect
```
