### 6.2 EU Consumer Protection Language Rules

**DIRECTIVE 2011/83/EU - Consumer Rights Directive:**
- Return policy MUST be in consumer's language (not marketplace default)
- Refund timeline must be explicit (typically 14 days for purchases, 30 for returns)
- Costs of return shipping: Must specify who pays
- Language: No exemption for "technical translations"

**REGULATION (EU) 2011/1169 - Food Safety:**
- If selling food products: Labels MUST be in local language
- Allergen information: Local language mandatory
- Nutritional content: Local language (numerical values only acceptable if English)

**GDPR ARTICLE 12 - Data Privacy:**
- Privacy notice must be "concise, transparent, intelligible and easily accessible"
- Language: End-user's language (customer's browser language presumed)
- Written copy: Available in local language on request

**DIRECTIVE 2013/11/EU - Alternative Dispute Resolution:**
- Consumer complaint form: Local language mandatory
- Dispute resolution options: Must include local ADR body information
- Communication: All ADR notices in consumer's language

### 6.3 Country-Specific Legal Requirements

**Germany (BfG - Bundesdatenschutzgesetz + AGG):**
- All communications in German or English (German preferred)
- AGB (Terms) must be clearly visible in German
- Widerrufsrecht (withdrawal right) 14 days (MUST include template)
- Versand/Rückversand costs must be specified upfront

**France (CNIL + Code du Consommation):**
- All customer-facing docs in French
- Right to withdrawal: 14 days (French legal template required)
- Price transparency: TTC (all-in) mandatory
- CGV (Terms of Sale) must include complaint contact

**Poland (UOKIK - Office of Consumer Competition):**
- Polish language mandatory for all customer comms
- Warranty period: 24 months (product-dependent variations)
- Reklamacja (complaint process): Must include formal complaint form
- Return procedure: Clear timeline in Polish

**Netherlands (NVWA + Autoriteit Consument & Markt):**
- Dutch language required for consumer comms
- Return window: 14 days minimum (statutory)
- Transport costs: Seller typically pays for returns
- Openbare Orde (public order): No hidden terms allowed

**Spain (OCU + AEPD):**
- Spanish mandatory for all communications
- Derecho de desistimiento: 14 days (detailed explanation required)
- Factura (Invoice): Spanish language on request
- AEPD privacy compliance: Spanish-language notices

## 7. Quality Assurance for Translations

### 7.1 BLEU Score & Metrics

**BLEU Score Interpretation:**
- 50+: Excellent (professional quality)
- 40-49: Very good (acceptable for publication)
- 30-39: Good (requires human review)
- 20-29: Fair (significant post-editing needed)
- <20: Poor (not acceptable)

**Target BLEU Scores by Use Case:**
| Use Case | Target BLEU | Tools | Post-Edit % |
|---|---|---|---|
| Product Descriptions | 35-40 | DeepL + review | 20-25% |
| Legal Documents | 38-42 | DeepL + legal reviewer | 30-40% |
| Customer Emails | 32-37 | Google/DeepL + native | 25-35% |
| Technical Specs | 40-45 | DeepL (specialized) | 15-20% |
| Marketing Copy | 33-38 | DeepL + copywriter | 30-35% |

### 7.2 Human Evaluation Framework

**Evaluation Criteria (5-point scale):**

1. **Accuracy** (5 = perfect terminology match)
   - No words lost in translation
   - All proper nouns correctly handled
   - Numbers/dates/codes unchanged

2. **Fluency** (5 = native speaker quality)
   - Natural phrasing in target language
   - Proper grammar and syntax
   - Reads like original content

3. **Adequacy** (5 = complete meaning transfer)
   - All source information preserved
   - Context preserved
   - No ambiguity introduced

4. **Style Consistency** (5 = perfect consistency)
   - Matches glossary/approved terminology
   - Formal level appropriate (Sie/vous/Pan)
   - Brand voice maintained

5. **Readability** (5 = optimal for target market)
   - Appropriate for marketplace audience
   - No cultural inappropriateness
   - Persuasive for commercial context

**Passing Score:** 4.0+ average across 5 criteria

### 7.3 Native Speaker Review Process

**Reviewer Requirements:**
- Native speaker of target language
- Subject-matter knowledge (e-commerce, product category)
- Experience with target marketplace (Amazon.de, Allegro, etc.)
- Minimum 2 years translation experience

**Review Checklist:**
1. Read full translated text (no source language reference)
2. Evaluate fluency/naturalness (5-point scale)
3. Check glossary consistency (100% required)
4. Verify formatting/special characters
5. Flag any ambiguities or unclear passages
6. Provide inline comments for post-editor
7. Approve or request revisions

**Timeline:** 24 hours per 5,000 words

### 7.4 A/B Testing for Translations

**Methodology:**
- Create 2 variants (alternative translations) of same product
- Split traffic 50/50 on marketplace A/B testing feature (if available)
- Measure: Click-through rate (CTR), conversion rate (CR), bounce rate
- Duration: Minimum 2 weeks, 1,000+ impressions per variant
- Statistically significant: p < 0.05

**Example Test (Amazon.de):**
- **Variant A:** "Wasserdichtes Smartphone-Armband" (Literal)
- **Variant B:** "Smartphone-Armband mit IP67 Wasserschutz" (Feature-focused)
- **Result:** Variant B = +3.2% CTR, -2.1% cart abandonment
- **Action:** Use Variant B across all German marketplace descriptions

### 8.4 Cost Optimization Strategies

**1. Leverage Translation Memory**
- 1st project: 100% translation
- 2nd project: 60% TM reuse = 40% cost reduction
- 3rd project: 75% TM reuse = 75% cost reduction

**2. Batch Translations**
- Monthly batch processing vs. daily: -10% cost
- Consolidate updates: Translate monthly instead of weekly

**3. Strategic Outsourcing**
- High-volume languages (FR/DE): DeepL + junior reviewer (€0.08/word)
- Niche languages (PL): Hybrid human-led approach (€0.11/word)

**4. Automation Wherever Possible**
- Technical specs: 100% MT acceptable (BLEU >40)
- Product dimensions: CSV templates with variable substitution
- Legal docs: Template-based with variable injection

**5. Internal Development**
- Year 1-2: Outsource all translation
- Year 3+: Hire 1 in-house bilingual (DE/FR) editor (€2,500/mo)
- Reduces review costs by 30-40% long-term

## Key Takeaways

1. **Language Localization is non-negotiable** for EU marketplace compliance (legal + commercial)
2. **DeepL + human review (20-25% post-edit)** is optimal for cost/quality balance
3. **Translation Memory leverage** reduces Year 2+ costs by 50-60%
4. **Localization ≠ translation**: Requires cultural adaptation (formality, units, payment preferences)
5. **Hybrid customer service** (automation + BPO) balances cost (€1,200-2,000/month) and quality
6. **3-year ROI: 564% additional net revenue** justifies initial €15K+ investment
7. **Legal requirements mandate local language** for returns, warranty, privacy, complaints
8. **Budget €46K annually** for ongoing localization + customer service (mature state)
