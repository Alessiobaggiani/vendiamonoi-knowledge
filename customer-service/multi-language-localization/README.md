### Critical Success Factors

1. **Formality Levels Matter:** German "Sie", French "vous", Polish "Pan/Pani" — not interchangeable with casual forms
2. **Translation Memory Leverage:** After 2-3 projects, 60-75% of content reuses previous translations, cutting costs dramatically
3. **Legal Documentation:** Non-negotiable — must use local language for returns, warranty, privacy (EU law)
4. **Native Review is Essential:** Machine translation alone (even DeepL) produces 28-32 BLEU score; human review lifts to 38-42
5. **Marketplace-Specific Glossaries:** What Amazon.de calls "Wasserdicht" differs from technical specs on ManoMano

### Avoid Common Pitfalls

- Do NOT rely 100% on machine translation (quality drops 30-40%)
- Do NOT skip glossary development (leads to inconsistent terminology)
- Do NOT ignore cultural adaptation (units, dates, formality = cart abandonment)
- Do NOT hire monolingual customer service agents (local language mandatory for complaints)
- Do NOT forget GDPR: Privacy notices must be in end-user's language
