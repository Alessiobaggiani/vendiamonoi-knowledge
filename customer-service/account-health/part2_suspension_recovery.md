## 1. Suspension Prevention — Proactive Measures

### 1.1 Early Warning Indicators

**Amazon Specific Warnings:**
- ODR reaches 1.5% (alert before 2% suspension)
- LSR reaches 3.5% (alert before 4% suspension)
- PFCR reaches 2.0% (alert before 2.5% suspension)
- Feedback score dips below 95% positive
- Unusual A-to-Z claim spike (3+ claims same day)
- Negative review spike (5+ 1-star reviews in 24 hours)

**eBay Specific Warnings:**
- TDR reaches 1.5% (alert before 2.0% Top Rated)
- DSR average drops below 4.5 on any dimension
- Returns exceed 1% of orders

**Bol.com Specific Warnings:**
- Cancellation rate exceeds 2% (before 3% threshold)
- Response time > 8 hours (before 24-hour SLA)
- Track & Trace missing on > 5 orders in single day
- On-time delivery drops below 90%

## 2. Suspension Recovery

### 2.1 Immediate Response Protocol

**Upon Suspension Notice:**

1. **Screenshot Everything**
   - Suspension email + timestamp
   - Account dashboard
   - Metrics at suspension time
   - Recent orders, complaints

2. **Gather Documentation**
   - Last 3 months supplier invoices
   - Shipping records
   - Customer returns/refunds
   - Quality logs
   - Communication records

3. **Identify Root Cause**
   - Review suspension reason
   - Correlate with metrics
   - Check false positives
   - Identify correctable issues

4. **Inform Stakeholders**
   - Executive team
   - Finance (revenue impact)
   - Customer service
   - Recovery team

5. **Stop Business-as-Usual**
   - Pause new listings
   - Hold new shipments
   - Centralize communications

### 2.2 Plan of Action (POA) Template

**Structure:**

```
AMAZON PLAN OF ACTION — [COMPANY NAME]
Submitted: [DATE]
Account: [SELLER ID]
Reason for Suspension: [REASON]

1. ACKNOWLEDGMENT OF ISSUE
[Demonstrate understanding. Be specific. Take responsibility.]

2. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
[Explain what caused the issue. Use data/evidence.]

3. CORRECTIVE ACTIONS TAKEN
[Document steps completed. Be specific with dates.]

4. PREVENTIVE MEASURES
[Describe systems/processes to prevent recurrence.]

5. MONITORING & METRICS
[How will you track ongoing performance?]

6. SUPPORTING DOCUMENTATION
[Invoices, logs, contracts, policies, timelines]
```

**Sample ODR POA:**

```
1. ACKNOWLEDGMENT
ODR reached 2.3% (exceeding 1% policy). This indicates customer
dissatisfaction and increases buyer risk. We take full responsibility.

2. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
- 34 cases (59%): Carrier damage (FedEx shipments Nov 12-19)
- 16 cases (28%): Packaging error (manual packing, no verification)
- 8 cases (13%): Wrong item shipped (no barcode verification)

3. CORRECTIVE ACTIONS TAKEN (as of 11/27/2025)

a) Pre-shipment quality inspection:
   - Every order verified before packing
   - Photo documentation
   - Barcode scan verification
   - Implemented: 11/25/2025, Owner: [Name]

b) Carrier switch (FedEx → DHL):
   - 99.2% on-time record
   - €500 insurance per package
   - Implemented: 11/26/2025, Owner: [Name]

c) Updated packaging:
   - Reinforced boxes for fragile items
   - Void fill for electronics
   - Bubble wrap for breakables
   - Implemented: 11/25/2025, Owner: [Name]

4. PREVENTIVE MEASURES

a) Daily monitoring (automated):
   - Real-time complaint tracking
   - Alert if ODR > 1.5% weekly
   - Owner: [Name]

b) Supplier quality:
   - SLA: 0.5% defect rate
   - Monthly scorecard
   - Quarterly reviews
   - Owner: [Name]

c) Team training:
   - Fulfillment training: 12/1/2025
   - Carrier review process
   - Owner: [Name]

d) Technology:
   - Barcode scanning at every step
   - Automated carrier API
   - Proactive notifications
   - Implemented: 12/5/2025, Owner: [Name]

5. MONITORING & METRICS

Daily: Complaint count, carrier damage, reject rate
Weekly: ODR trend, complaint analysis, carrier performance
Monthly: Root cause analysis, supplier performance

Targets:
- ODR: < 0.7% (Dec-Jan)
- LSR: < 2% (maintain)
- PFCR: < 1% (maintain)

6. SUPPORTING DOCUMENTATION
- Supplier invoices (Sept-Nov)
- DHL contract
- Process documentation (11/25/2025)
- Quality checklist
- Training schedule
- Complaint logs
- Carrier comparison

Submitted: 11/27/2025
```

### 2.3 eBay Appeal Process

**Structure:**
- Appeals via Resolution Center
- Limited to 2 attempts
- Response deadline: 7-14 days

**Template:**
```
eBay SELLER APPEAL — [NAME]
Account: [ID]
Submission Date: [DATE]

VIOLATION OVERVIEW
[State the violation cited]

EXPLANATION OF CIRCUMSTANCES
[Timeline, events, misunderstandings]

STEPS TAKEN TO PREVENT RECURRENCE
[Specific actions with dates]

REQUEST
[Explicitly request reinstatement]
```

### 2.4 Bol.com Reinstatement Request

**Process:**
- Mandatory cooling-off: 14 days
- Reinstatement application
- Review by compliance: 7-10 days
- Decision: Reinstatement or permanent ban

**Structure:**
- Root cause analysis
- Corrective measures + timeline
- Compliance certification
- Supporting documentation

### 2.5 Recovery Timeline Expectations

**Amazon:**
- Suspension → POA submission: 24-48 hours
- POA review: 5-14 business days
- First response: 10 days average
- Appeals: 2 additional (14 days each)
- Total: 14-90 days (can extend to 120+)

**eBay:**
- Suspension → Data accessible: 24 hours
- Appeal deadline: 7-14 days
- Appeal review: 5-10 days
- Decision: Within 14 days
- Total: 14-28 days typical

**Bol.com:**
- Cooling period: 14 days (mandatory)
- Reinstatement window: Day 14+
- Review: 7-14 business days
- Decision: Within 21 days
- Total: 21-40 days typical

### 2.6 Reinstatement Success Factors

**What Works:**
- Genuine understanding of issue
- Root cause with data
- Specific, measurable actions
- Evidence of implementation
- Realistic metrics
- Professional tone

**What Doesn't Work:**
- Generic templates
- Blaming external factors
- Perfection promises
- Vague actions
- No documentation
- Defensive tone

## 3. Multi-Account Strategy

### 3.1 When Multi-Accounting is Legitimate

**Valid Scenarios:**
- Different business units (B2C vs. B2B)
- Different product categories
- Geographic expansion
- Brand separation

**Invalid Scenarios:**
- Evading suspension (forbidden, results in all banned)
- Manipulating metrics
- Circumventing restrictions

### 3.2 Platform Policies

**Amazon:**
- Multiple allowed with pre-approval
- Disclose all related accounts
- Separate tax ID per account
- Cannot share inventory/policies
- Violation: All accounts banned

**eBay:**
- Multiple allowed
- Separate registration
- Cannot circumvent restrictions

**Bol.com:**
- Multiple allowed for multi-brand
- Explicit approval required
- Business justification needed
- Violation: Account termination

### 3.3 Risk Mitigation

**If Operating Multiple Accounts:**
1. Completely separate operations
2. Disclose all related accounts
3. Use separate business entities
4. Separate financial records
5. Never share customer data
6. Never share inventory
