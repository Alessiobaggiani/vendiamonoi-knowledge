## Section 2: Ticket Routing & Prioritization

### Priority Scoring Algorithm (0-100 Scale)

Vendiamonoi must process 10,000 tickets daily. Manual prioritization is impossible. Automated scoring ensures critical issues reach senior agents in <15 minutes.

**Scoring Formula**:
```
Priority Score = 
  (Category Factor × 0.20) +
  (Sentiment Factor × 0.15) +
  (Wait Time Factor × 0.15) +
  (Customer Value Factor × 0.20) +
  (SLA Threshold Factor × 0.15) +
  (Order Value Factor × 0.15)
```

### Factor 1: Category Factor (0-100, weight 20%)

| Category | Score | SLA | Examples |
|----------|-------|-----|----------|
| Critical/Urgent | 100 | 15 min | Account compromise, payment fraud, major defect |
| High Priority | 80 | 30 min | Refund dispute, lost package, wrong item |
| Medium Priority | 50 | 2 hours | Shipping delay, minor defect, billing question |
| Low Priority | 20 | 8 hours | General inquiry, feedback, compliment |

**Implementation**: Use Zendesk condition-based scoring:
```
IF subject contains ["fraud", "compromised", "stolen"] THEN Category = 100
IF subject contains ["refund", "lost", "wrong item"] THEN Category = 80
IF subject contains ["shipping", "delay"] THEN Category = 50
ELSE Category = 20
```

### Factor 2: Sentiment Factor (0-100, weight 15%)

**Implementation**: OpenAI GPT-4 sentiment analysis on ticket body.

| Sentiment | Score | Detection Signals |
|-----------|-------|-------------------|
| Extremely angry (1.0) | 100 | CAPS LOCK, repeated punctuation (!!!), curse words, threats |
| Very angry (0.75) | 75 | Strong negative language, frustration, urgency markers |
| Neutral (0.5) | 40 | Standard inquiry, no emotional language |
| Positive (0.25) | 20 | Polite tone, thank you, compliment |

**Escalation Logic**:
IF Sentiment >= 0.75 THEN escalate to senior agent, notify supervisor

### Factor 3: Wait Time Factor (0-100, weight 15%)

```
Wait Time Factor = MIN(100, (Hours Waiting / 4) × 100)
```

**Examples**:
- 0 min wait: Factor = 0
- 2 hours wait: Factor = 50
- 4+ hours wait: Factor = 100

**Logic**: Age-based escalation ensures no ticket waits >8 hours.

### Factor 4: Customer Value Factor (0-100, weight 20%)

**CLV Calculation**:
```
CLV = (Annual Revenue per Customer) + (Gross Margin %) + (Repeat Purchase Likelihood)
```

| Customer Segment | Annual Revenue | Score | SLA Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| VIP (€10k+/year) | €10,000+ | 100 | 15 min |
| Platinum (€5k-10k) | €5,000-€10,000 | 80 | 30 min |
| Gold (€1k-5k) | €1,000-€5,000 | 50 | 2 hours |
| Standard (<€1k) | <€1,000 | 20 | 8 hours |

**Implementation**: Integrate with Zendesk CRM to pull customer CLV on ticket creation.

### Factor 5: SLA Threshold Factor (0-100, weight 15%)

```
SLA Factor = (Minutes Until SLA Breach / Total SLA Minutes) × 100
```

**Examples**:
- SLA 2 hours, 1 hour 45 min remaining: (15/120) × 100 = 12
- SLA 2 hours, 30 min remaining: (30/120) × 100 = 25
- SLA 2 hours, 5 min remaining: (5/120) × 100 = 4

**Impact**: Automatic escalation when <15 minutes remain.

### Factor 6: Order Value Factor (0-100, weight 15%)

| Order Value | Score | Context |
|---|---|---|
| €500+ | 100 | High-value orders receive immediate attention |
| €200-500 | 60 | Medium-value orders |
| €50-200 | 30 | Standard orders |
| <€50 | 10 | Small orders (often auto-resolved) |

### Routing Priority Tiers

**Tier 1: Critical (Score 80-100)**
- **Response time**: 15 minutes
- **Assignee**: Senior agent (5+ years experience) or supervisor
- **Notification**: SMS + Slack + email
- **Escalation**: After 10 min wait, auto-escalate to supervisor
- **Examples**: Fraud, account compromise, major defect, VIP customer issue

**Tier 2: High (Score 60-79)**
- **Response time**: 30 minutes
- **Assignee**: Experienced agent (2+ years)
- **Notification**: Slack + email
- **Escalation**: After 25 min wait, bump to supervisor queue
- **Examples**: Refund disputes, lost packages, wrong items

**Tier 3: Medium (Score 40-59)**
- **Response time**: 2 hours
- **Assignee**: Standard agent or junior agent with supervision
- **Notification**: Email digest (15-min batch)
- **Escalation**: After 1.5 hours, auto-escalate
- **Examples**: Shipping delays, minor defects, billing questions

**Tier 4: Low (Score 0-39)**
- **Response time**: 8 hours
- **Assignee**: Junior agent, chatbot, or automation
- **Notification**: Daily email digest
- **Escalation**: Manual review if >4 hours old
- **Examples**: General inquiries, feedback, non-urgent questions

### Skill-Based Routing

Beyond priority, tickets route to agents with matching skill tags.

**Specialization Queues**:

1. **Amazon Specialist Queue** (18 agents)
   - Required skills: Amazon Seller Central, marketplace policies
   - Handles: Amazon-specific issues, A-to-Z disputes, policy questions
   - Avg resolution: 45 minutes
   - SLA: 30-60 min

2. **Returns & Refunds Queue** (12 agents)
   - Required skills: Return policies, refund processing, accounting integration
   - Handles: Returns, refunds, prepaid labels, dispute resolution
   - Avg resolution: 20 minutes
   - SLA: 30 min

3. **Shipping & Logistics Queue** (15 agents)
   - Required skills: Logistics, carrier integrations, tracking
   - Handles: Shipping issues, tracking, delivery exceptions
   - Avg resolution: 30 minutes
   - SLA: 30-120 min (depends on urgency)

4. **Technical Support Queue** (7 agents)
   - Required skills: Product issues, technical troubleshooting
   - Handles: Product defects, technical issues, account problems
   - Avg resolution: 60 minutes
   - SLA: 30-120 min

5. **Billing & Disputes Queue** (5 agents)
   - Required skills: Accounting, payment systems, chargeback handling
   - Handles: Payment issues, billing questions, chargeback disputes
   - Avg resolution: 45 minutes
   - SLA: 30-120 min

6. **General Queue** (30 agents)
   - Required skills: All basic competencies
   - Handles: First-line, simple issues, escalations
   - Avg resolution: 25 minutes
   - SLA: 30 min-4 hours

**Routing Logic**:
```
IF ticket.category IN ["Returns", "Refund"] THEN route to Returns Queue
ELSE IF ticket.order.channel = "Amazon" THEN route to Amazon Queue
ELSE IF ticket.category = "Shipping" THEN route to Shipping Queue
ELSE IF ticket.requires_skill = "Technical" THEN route to Technical Queue
ELSE IF ticket.category IN ["Payment", "Billing", "Chargeback"] THEN route to Billing Queue
ELSE route to General Queue (round-robin based on capacity)
```

## Section 3: Chatbot & AI Integration

### Three-Tier AI Architecture

**Tier 1: Rule-Based Chatbot (Months 1-3, 15% automation)**
- Simple if/then rules for common questions
- Pattern matching on keywords
- Canned response library (150 responses)
- No machine learning, 100% deterministic
- Used for: Returns inquiries, shipping tracking, account access

**Tier 2: Intent-Based Classifier (Months 4-6, 30% automation)**
- Supervised ML model classifies ticket intent
- Routes to appropriate queue automatically
- Extracts key entities (order ID, tracking #, issue type)
- Begins fine-tuning on company data
- Used for: Triage, queue routing, categorization

**Tier 3: Generative AI with GPT-4 (Months 7+, 50%+ automation)**
- Fine-tuned GPT-4 model on 12-month historical tickets
- Generates natural, personalized responses
- Maintains conversation context over multi-turn interactions
- Automatic resolution suggestions
- Escalation logic for complex issues
- Used for: Response generation, proactive offers, complex problem-solving

## Section 4: Make.com Workflow Automation

### Workflow 1: Automated Return Label Generation

**Trigger**: Customer initiates return in self-service portal or via email.

**Step-by-step pseudocode**:

```
ON trigger: Return initiation
  STEP 1: Extract order ID from ticket/form
  STEP 2: Query order API (Shopify/Amazon/eBay)
    - Verify order exists
    - Verify order is eligible for return (30 days, not digital)
    - Get shipping address
    - Get return reason code
  
  STEP 3: IF order not eligible THEN
    - Send ineligible email
    - Create ticket for human review
    - END
  
  STEP 4: Call EasyPost API to generate return label
    - Carrier: Use customer's original carrier preference
    - Weight: Auto-estimate from order
    - Return address: Vendor warehouse location
  
  STEP 5: Create return record in Zendesk
    - Link to original order ticket
    - Set RMA code (auto-generated)
    - Flag for monitoring (expect return within 14 days)
  
  STEP 6: Send customer email with:
    - Return label (PDF attached or link)
    - RMA code (print on package)
    - Refund timeline (3-5 business days after receipt)
    - Return instructions (specific to carrier)
  
  STEP 7: Create Slack notification to Logistics team
    - RMA code
    - Expected return date
    - Return warehouse location
  
  STEP 8: AUTO-CLOSE ticket or set to "awaiting return"
  
  Impact: 90% of return requests completed without agent intervention
  Time saved: 8 min per request (manual process) → 15 sec automated
```

**Expected volume**: 1,200 return requests/month = 160 hours saved/month

### Workflow 2: Automated Refund Processing

**Trigger**: Return package received and verified at warehouse.

**Refund processing logic**:

```
ON trigger: Return received (Zendesk ticket status updated by warehouse)
  
  STEP 1: Query order details
    - Order value: €X
    - Original payment method
    - Return reason: reason_code
    - Customer CLV: €Y/year
  
  STEP 2: Risk assessment
    IF return reason = "defective" AND CLV < €500 THEN
      Auto-refund (no supervisor needed)
      Path = A (automated)
    ELSE IF return reason = "changed mind" AND order_value < €100 THEN
      Auto-refund (no supervisor)
      Path = A (automated)
    ELSE IF return reason = "wrong item" (clearly vendor error) THEN
      Auto-refund (no supervisor)
      Path = A (automated)
    ELSE
      Mark for supervisor review
      Path = B (manual)
  
  STEP 3A: Auto-refund path (85% of returns)
    - Call payment processor API (Stripe/PayPal)
    - Initiate refund to original payment method
    - Set refund processing time: 3-5 business days
    - Create Zendesk note: "Auto-refunded via workflow"
    - Send customer email: "Your refund has been processed"
    - Mark ticket status: "Resolved - Auto-refunded"
    - Log refund in accounting system
  
  STEP 3B: Supervisor review path (15% of returns)
    - Create Slack message to Billing Supervisor:
      * Order ID
      * Order value
      * Return reason
      * Customer CLV
      * Recommendation (approve/deny)
    - Supervisor responds: approve or deny
    - IF approve: Execute refund via Step 3A
    - IF deny: Send customer explanation email with next steps
    - Mark ticket status accordingly
  
  STEP 4: Post-refund follow-up
    - Schedule email 6 days later: "Refund received? Let us know!"
    - Request feedback on return process (survey link)
    - Offer 10% discount on next order (CLV < €500 only)
  
  Impact: 85% of refunds processed without manual intervention
  Processing time: 3 days → 6 hours (automated)
  Cost savings: €50/refund review (supervisor time) × 1,000 refunds/month = €50k/month saved
```

### Workflow 3: Proactive Escalation Notifications

**Trigger**: High-priority tickets waiting >10 minutes without response.

**Timeline-based notifications**:

```
ON trigger: Ticket created with priority >= HIGH (score 60+)
  
  T+0 min: Ticket assigned to agent in specialized queue
  
  T+10 min: IF ticket still unread THEN
    - Send Slack message to agent: "[High-priority ticket - respond within 5 min]"
    - Sound notification (ping)
  
  T+15 min: IF still unresponded AND priority = CRITICAL THEN
    - Send SMS to agent: "Critical ticket needs response (Order: #12345)"
    - Notify supervisor: "Escalate to available senior agent"
  
  T+20 min: IF still unresponded THEN
```

### Workflow 5: Automated Proactive Resolution Offers

**Trigger**: Delivery date approaching and package not yet in transit.

**Proactive outreach logic**:

```
ON trigger: Every 6 hours (automated job)
  
  STEP 1: Query orders with status = "processing" AND created >= 3 days ago
  STEP 2: Query marketplace APIs for actual shipment status
  
  FOR EACH order not yet shipped:
    IF estimated_delivery_date < TODAY + 2 days THEN
      CONDITION 1: Order at risk of late delivery
      
      STEP 3: Assess customer communication history
        - How many times has customer been contacted about this order?
        - Is there an open ticket?
        - What is customer sentiment (if known)?
      
      STEP 4: Generate proactive offer
        IF customer_sentiment = "neutral" or "positive" THEN
          Offer type: Discount or partial refund
          Discount amount: 10% of order value
          Message: "Your order may arrive late. Please accept this 10% discount as our apology."
          Call to action: Click to accept
        ELSE IF customer_sentiment = "negative" THEN
          Offer type: Expedited replacement
          Message: "We'll expedite a replacement at no cost. Would you like us to ship today?"
          Call to action: Reply "Yes" or "No"
      
      STEP 5: Send proactive email via Make.com
        TO: customer_email
        SUBJECT: "Your order [#12345] - We're offering [discount/replacement]"
        BODY: Generated from template with personalization
        TRACKING: Append UTM parameters to track if offer accepted
      
      STEP 6: Create Zendesk ticket
        Status: "Proactive outreach - awaiting response"
        Tag: "proactive-offer"
        Set follow-up date: +3 days
      
      STEP 7: Log offer in customer profile
        Record: Date, offer type, amount, acceptance status
      
      STEP 8: Monitor response
        IF customer accepts offer within 24 hours THEN
          Process discount/replacement
          Mark ticket: "Resolved - accepted offer"
        ELSE IF no response after 3 days THEN
          Send follow-up: "Still interested in our offer?"
        ELSE
          Escalate to human agent for phone follow-up
  
  Impact: 28% reduction in complaint tickets (proactive resolution)
  Customer sentiment: +8% CSAT improvement
  Offer acceptance rate: 92% of customers accept the offer
```

**ROI**: 1,000 offers/month × 92% acceptance × 10% discount avg = €46,000 revenue impact, but eliminates 280 complaint tickets (€14,000 in handling costs saved) = €32,000 net monthly benefit.
