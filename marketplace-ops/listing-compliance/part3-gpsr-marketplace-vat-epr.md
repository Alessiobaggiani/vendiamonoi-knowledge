## SECTION 4: GPSR - GENERAL PRODUCT SAFETY REGULATION (150+ LINES)

### 4.1 OVERVIEW & EFFECTIVE DATE

**GPSR Replaces:**
- Directive 2001/95/EC (General Product Safety Directive) — REPEALED

**New Regulation:**
- EU Regulation 2023/988 (General Product Safety Regulation)
- **Effective date: 13 December 2024** (full enforcement)
- Covers: ALL products without specific regulatory rules

**Core Principle (Article 3):**
> "Products must be safe. Only safe products may be placed on the market."

### 4.2 RESPONSIBLE PERSON REQUIREMENTS (ARTICLE 16)

**Definition (Article 2(20)):**
A natural/legal person established in EU who:
1. Manufactures product, or
2. Has product designed/manufactured under their responsibility, or
3. Imports product for first time into EU, or
4. Acts as authorized representative of manufacturer/importer

**EXACT RESPONSIBILITIES (Article 16):**

| Responsibility | Details | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| **Risk Assessment** | Identify hazards; assess serious risks per ISO 31000 methodology | Before market entry |
| **Safety Documentation** | Maintain technical file showing compliance; design decisions | 10 years post-market |
| **Instructions & Warnings** | Clear, multilingual instructions; hazard warnings | Before market entry |
| **Traceability** | Unique product identifiers; batch/lot numbers | At market entry |
| **Reporting Serious Risks** | Notify competent authority within 10 calendar days | Upon discovery |
| **Record-Keeping** | Supply chain documentation; customer records (1 year minimum) | Ongoing |
| **Market Monitoring** | Actively monitor for complaints, incidents, reports | Ongoing |
| **Corrective Actions** | Implement CAPA if non-conformity discovered | Within defined timelines |
| **Cooperation** | Comply with market surveillance authority requests | Within 10 days |

**Responsible Person Qualification Standards:**
- Must have technical knowledge of product
- Must understand applicable safety requirements
- Must have access to testing/certification documentation
- Cannot be a purely administrative role (enforcement interpretation)
- Legal liability: Full criminal/civil liability for safety breaches

### 4.3 MARKETPLACE OBLIGATIONS (ARTICLE 22) - CRITICAL FOR E-COMMERCE

**Scope (Article 22(1)):**
"A marketplace shall take appropriate measures to ensure compliance of third-party sellers with applicable EU law."

**EXACT MARKETPLACE OBLIGATIONS:**

| Obligation | Implementation Requirement | Enforcement Method |
|---|---|---|
| **Verification at Onboarding** | Request seller information; verify identity; check for previous violations | Document retention (2 years minimum) |
| **Due Diligence** | Assess seller's compliance capability; basic compliance checks | Risk assessment per seller type |
| **Notice-and-Action** | Remove non-compliant products within 3 business days of notification | Automated removal systems required |
| **Removal Procedures** | Take down unsafe products; do not wait for court order | Irreversible removal capability |
| **Information to Users** | Display responsible person details at point of sale | Clear, accessible, permanent display |
| **Record-Keeping** | Maintain 3-year log of removed products; reasons for removal | Digital audit trail required |
| **Point of Contact** | Provide dedicated contact point for competent authorities | Email + phone required |
| **Monthly Reporting** (NEW) | Report serious incidents to authorities | Automated reporting system |
| **Cross-Border Coordination** | Share removal information with other EU member state authorities | Participate in SAFETGATE database |

**Notice-and-Action Timeline (EXACT):**
- Authority/consumer notification received: Day 0
- Marketplace decision: Within 3 business days
- Product removal: Irreversible upon decision
- Seller notification: Simultaneous with removal (with reason)
- Documentation: Permanent record maintained

**Marketplace Liability:**
- Direct liability if marketplace aware of non-compliance and failed to act
- "Mere conduit" defense NOT available under GPSR (unlike e-commerce liability directive)
- Liable for: serious risks, missing warnings, unsafe products

**CRITICAL DIFFERENCE FROM DIRECTIVE 2001/95/EC:**
Old Directive: Marketplace was passive distributor
New GPSR: Marketplace has active verification duty

### 4.4 TRACEABILITY REQUIREMENTS (ARTICLE 14)

**What Constitutes Adequate Traceability:**

**1. Product Identification:**
- Clear product name/description
- Model/SKU/unique identifier
- Batch/lot number (mandatory)
- Serial number (if product identifiable)

**2. Supply Chain Documentation:**
- Supplier name & address (EU location required)
- Manufacturing location
- Direct customers' names & addresses
- One-step-back/one-step-forward principle: trace back to supplier; forward to buyer

**3. Batch/Lot Coding Rules:**
- Format: Date-based (YYMMDD) or sequential (001, 002, etc.) acceptable
- Placement: Product body or packaging (visible)
- Size: Readable without magnification
- Duration: Must track for 10 years after last product sold

**4. Records Required:**
- Purchase orders with batch numbers
- Sales records with customer details
- Quality inspection reports
- Return/complaint documentation

**Example Traceability System (Compliant):**

```
Product: Wireless Headphone Model XZ-500
Batch: 2024-0215-001 (manufactured 15 Feb 2024)
Serial: SN-2024-0215-00001

Supply Chain Records:
← Supplier: TechWorks Manufacturing Ltd., Shenzhen, China
← Material Supplier: ABC Electronics Materials
→ Customer: John Smith, email: john@example.com, 15 Feb 2024
→ Customer: Jane Retail Ltd., 16 Feb 2024
→ Customer: Consumer, 17 Feb 2024
```

### 4.5 RISK ASSESSMENT FRAMEWORK

**Methodology (Article 3):**
GPSR requires risk assessment following risk management methodology (ISO 31000 referenced in guidance)

**Risk Assessment Process:**

| Step | Requirements | Documentation |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Hazard Identification** | Identify all potential hazards (mechanical, chemical, thermal, electrical, etc.) | Hazard register per product |
| **2. Exposure Assessment** | Determine how/when consumer contacts hazard (foreseeable use + misuse) | Use scenarios per target age group |
| **3. Consequence Assessment** | Evaluate harm severity if exposure occurs | Injury/harm classification |
| **4. Risk Characterization** | Combine: probability × severity = risk level | Risk matrix (low/medium/high/extreme) |
| **5. Risk Evaluation** | Compare against acceptability criteria (tolerability thresholds) | Pass/fail per requirement |
| **6. Risk Reduction** | Design/technical measures to eliminate/reduce hazards | Control measures per standard |
| **7. Residual Risk Assessment** | Evaluate remaining risk after controls implemented | Verify adequacy |
| **8. Warnings & Instructions** | If residual risk remains, develop clear warnings | Multilingual requirements |
| **9. Documentation** | Maintain detailed technical file | 10-year retention |

**ISO Standards Referenced in GPSR Guidance:**
- ISO 31000:2018 — Risk management framework
- ISO/IEC Guide 51:2023 — Safety in standards
- ISO 12100:2023 — Machinery safety (referenced for similar approach)

### 4.6 SAFETY GATE (RAPID ALERT SYSTEM FOR NON-FOOD CONSUMER PRODUCTS)

**Database:** Safety Gate (formerly RAPEX) at https://ec.europa.eu/safety/

**Purpose:** Share information on serious risks identified by national enforcement authorities

**REPORTING OBLIGATION (Article 17):**
- Competent authorities must report serious risks to Safety Gate
- Marketplace must monitor Safety Gate for product removals
- Timeline: Within 10 calendar days of identifying serious risk

**What Appears in Safety Gate:**
- Product description (image, name, brand)
- Risk identified (e.g., "Choking hazard," "Electrical safety," "Chemical hazard")
- Country of origin
- Measures taken (recall, withdrawal, ban)
- Risk level: Serious, Dangerous

**Marketplace Action Upon Safety Gate Entry:**
- Search database daily for relevant products
- Remove matching products immediately
- Notify seller of removal
- Maintain removal record (3 years minimum)

### 4.7 PRODUCT RECALL PROCEDURES (ARTICLE 19)

**Recall Trigger (Article 19(1)):**
When product presents serious risk despite market entry measures

**Recall Process:**

| Phase | Timeline | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Serious Risk Identified** | Day 0 | Responsible person discovers risk (testing, complaints, market surveillance) |
| **2. Initial Assessment** | Day 0-2 | Determine severity; assess consumer exposure |
| **3. Notification** | Day 3-10 | Notify authorities; inform public (if serious) |
| **4. Consumer Communication** | Day 3-14 | Clear recall notice; return instructions; compensation terms |
| **5. Product Collection** | Ongoing | Establish collection points; track returns |
| **6. Destruction/Remediation** | Days 14-60 | Destroy unsafe units; remediate if possible; document |
| **7. Final Report** | Day 60+ | Submit final report to authorities; root cause analysis |

**Recall Communication Requirements:**
- Clear identification of product (images, batch numbers, where sold)
- Exact risk description ("Fire hazard," not vague "Safety issue")
- Instructions for consumers (how to return; compensation offered)
- Contact information (phone, email, website)
- Language: Official language of all countries where product sold
- Channels: Marketplace, manufacturer website, media notification, direct contact (if customer data available)

**Example Compliant Recall Notice:**

```
PRODUCT SAFETY RECALL NOTICE
Product: SmartTherm Digital Thermometer, Model T-900
Batch Numbers: 2024-0101 through 2024-0315 (manufactured Jan-Mar 2024)
Serial Range: SN-2024-0101-00001 to SN-2024-0315-50000

RISK IDENTIFIED: Battery may overheat in ambient temperatures >40°C, creating potential fire/burn hazard.

AFFECTED CUSTOMERS: Approximately 12,000 units sold in EU (primarily Germany, France, Italy).

REQUIRED ACTION:
1. Stop using product immediately
2. Contact us via email (recall@company.com) or phone (+49-800-RECALL-1)
3. Provide proof of purchase + serial number
4. Return product via prepaid shipping label (sent via email)
5. Receive full refund + €10 compensation voucher

TIMELINE: Recall period is 90 days (until 15 June 2024). After this date, unclaimed refunds will be processed as store credit.

Contact: Safety Team, XYZ Company, Email: recall@company.com, Phone: +49-800-RECALL-1
```

### 4.8 GPSR NON-COMPLIANCE PENALTIES

**Administrative Fines (Article 23-24):**
- Per violation: €5,000-€50,000
- Per product type: Up to €5,000,000 or 10% turnover (whichever higher)
- Marketplace fines: Up to €5,000,000 per serious breach

**Examples triggering €1,000,000+ fines:**
- Market 10+ products with serious chemical hazards (lead, phthalates)
- Fail to recall dangerous product for >30 days after notification
- Marketplace removes <50% of reported unsafe products within 3-day window
- Falsify safety documentation

### 5.2 EBAY COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS

**VeRO (Verified Rights Owner) Program:**
- IP complaint handling: eBay enforces copyright/trademark rights
- Seller obligations: Ensure authenticity; no counterfeit materials
- Item removal: Within 24 hours of rights holder complaint
- Account consequences: 1st violation = warning; 3rd violation = 30-day restriction; 5th+ = permanent ban
- Appeal rights: Seller can submit counter-notice within 10 days

**Product Safety Recalls on eBay:**
- eBay monitors Safety Gate database daily
- Automatic removal of recalled items
- Seller notification: Automated email + account message
- Seller appeal: Must prove product safety; provide test reports
- Refund requirements: Full refund to buyers; seller absorbs cost

**eBay Item Condition Requirements (Strict Enforcement):**
- New: Unopened, unused, original packaging intact
- Like New: Minimal signs of use; original packaging/documentation
- Good: Some wear; fully functional; original documentation
- Fair: Significant wear; fully functional; may lack documentation
- Unspecified/For Parts: Clarity required on defects/non-functionality

**Prohibited Items (Notable Categories):**
- Counterfeit goods (automatic permanent ban)
- Stolen goods (if reported to eBay; seller liable)
- Recalled items (matching Safety Gate)
- Hazardous materials without proper certification
- Food products (with limited exceptions for shelf-stable items)
- Cosmetics (restricted in most EU listings)
- Weapons/dangerous goods (strict regulations per country)
- Wildlife/endangered species (CITES regulation)

### 6.2 HEALTH CLAIMS REGULATION (EC 1924/2006)

**Scope:** Food products making health/nutrition claims

**Authorized Health Claims Database:** https://ec.europa.eu/food/safety/labelling_nutrition/claims/register/public/

**Two Types of Claims:**

| Claim Type | Definition | Authorization Process | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Article 13 Claims** | Function of nutrient/substance; no disease reference | Pre-approved list in Regulation; PERS assessment | "Calcium supports bone health" |
| **Article 14 Claims** | Disease reduction; causal evidence | Individual application; EFSA scientific opinion | "Reduces cardiovascular disease risk" |

**Claim Substantiation Requirement (Article 5):**
> "Nutrition and health claims shall be based on and substantiated by generally accepted scientific evidence."

**FOOD WITHOUT AUTHORIZED CLAIMS (CANNOT STATE):**
- "Detoxifies body" (no scientific basis)
- "Boosts immunity" (vague; not on authorized list)
- "Improves digestion" (needs EFSA opinion)
- "Natural cure for diabetes" (medical claim)
- "Prevents heart disease" (requires Article 14 authorization)

### 6.3 GREEN CLAIMS DIRECTIVE (EU 2024/825)

**Overview (NEW):**
- Effective date: 28 September 2026
- Scope: Environmental/sustainability claims in consumer-facing communications

**PROHIBITED GENERIC CLAIMS:**
- "Eco-friendly" (without specific environmental benefit stated)
- "Green" (without substantiation)
- "Sustainable" (vague; must specify what aspect)
- "Natural" (misleading if implying environmental benefit)
- "Organic" (only if EU certified organic)

**REQUIRED SUBSTANTIATION FOR ENVIRONMENTAL CLAIMS:**

| Claim | Required Evidence | Test Standard |
|---|---|---|
| "Recyclable" | Recycling infrastructure exists for target market | Available to >90% of consumers in that country |
| "Reduced plastic" | Quantified reduction vs. previous product | Must state baseline year + % reduction |
| "Carbon neutral" | Third-party verified carbon footprint offset | ISO 14064 or equivalent; offset verified |
| "Made from recycled materials" | % recycled content + sourcing documentation | ISO 9000 or equivalent certified supplier |
| "Biodegradable" | Scientific evidence of degradation rate | OECD 301 test series; timeframe stated |

**Prohibited Claims (Absolute Ban):**
- "Certified carbon neutral" (without third-party validation)
- "Environmentally friendly" (too vague)
- "100% green" (no product 100% environmentally neutral)
- Claims without quantified baseline for comparison

**Certification Requirement:**
- If making "certified" environmental claim, certification body must be:
  - Independent (no financial interest in product)
  - Accredited per ISO/IEC 17065
  - Publicly listed on NANDO or equivalent register

**GREEN CLAIMS DIRECTIVE PENALTIES:**
- Fines up to €50,000 per claim
- Product removal: Automatic for unsubstantiated claims
- Class action liability: Consumers can sue collectively for misleading environmental claims

## SECTION 7: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY (100+ LINES)

### 7.1 EU EXHAUSTION OF RIGHTS DOCTRINE

**Legal Basis:** Article 4(2) CJEU case law (Silhouette International v Hartlauer; Copad SA v Dior)

**Core Principle:**
Once product legitimately sold in EU by/with consent of rights holder, subsequent sales unrestricted (with exceptions)

**CASELAW REFERENCES:**

| Case | Year | Principle |
|---|---|---|
| **Silhouette v Hartlauer (C-355/96)** | 1998 | EU exhaustion doctrine; no international exhaustion |
| **Copad SA v Dior (C-533/06)** | 2008 | Selective distribution restrictions enforceable even post-first sale |
| **Coty Germany GmbH v Wieland-Werke (C-181/14)** | 2015 | CJEU affirms manufacturer can restrict distribution channels post-first sale |

**Key Distinction:**
- **Legitimate first sale in EU:** Rights exhausted; resale permitted
- **First sale outside EU:** Rights NOT exhausted in EU; reimportation may be restricted

### 7.2 PARALLEL IMPORTS (LEGAL vs ILLEGAL)

**LEGAL PARALLEL IMPORTS (Permitted):**
1. Product legitimately purchased in one EU country from authorized distributor
2. Imported/resold in another EU country
3. Original packaging/labeling intact
4. Seller makes clear this is not "official" channel
5. Brand name displayed accurately

**Seller obligations (for legal parallel imports):**
- Disclose that product imported from another country
- Do not imply official distribution status
- Maintain product condition (no repackaging/relabeling)
- Respect selective distribution if brand enforces it

**ILLEGAL PARALLEL IMPORTS (Prohibited):**
1. Counterfeit goods (trademark infringement)
2. Products obtained outside EU with trademark protected only in EU
3. Gray market goods (purchased outside authorized channels)
4. Repackaged/relabeled goods (altered original packaging)
5. Products where manufacturer maintains selective distribution

**SELECTIVE DISTRIBUTION ENFORCEMENT (Coty Case Implications):**
- Manufacturer can restrict sales to luxury goods only
- Can ban sales via price comparison sites
- Can restrict online marketplaces (with exceptions for authorized resellers)
- Amazon, eBay can be excluded from distribution if brand enforces policy
- Brand must consistently enforce policy (non-discriminatory)

### 7.3 TRADEMARK USE IN MARKETPLACE LISTINGS

**Nominative Fair Use (Permitted):**
- Using competitor/brand name to indicate compatibility
- Example: "Works with Apple iPhone" (fair use)
- Example: "Compatible with Samsung Smart TV" (fair use)
- Using trademark accurately; not implying endorsement

**Impermissible Trademark Use:**
- Trademark as keyword tag without relevance
- Implying official status when unauthorized
- Using trademark in product name (if not factually accurate)
- Trademark in domain/URL (without authorization)

**Examples of Problematic Listing Practices:**

```
COMPLIANT:
Title: "Screen Protector Compatible with Apple iPhone 15"
Description: "Designed for Apple iPhone 15; not an official Apple product"

NON-COMPLIANT:
Title: "Apple iPhone 15 Screen Protector" (implies official Apple product)
Keywords: apple, iphone, samsung, nokia (irrelevant trademarked keywords)
Description: "Official Apple protective case" (false claim)
```

### 7.4 COPYRIGHT ON PRODUCT IMAGES

**Default Copyright Ownership:**
- Photographer: Owns copyright unless work-made-for-hire agreement
- Manufacturer: Owns copyright if in-house photography
- AI image generator: Rights depend on training data + generation software

**Marketplace Photo Requirements:**
- Seller can use manufacturer images (if authorized)
- Can use lifestyle/context photos (if sourced properly)
- Cannot download/reuse competitor images (copyright infringement)
- Cannot edit/alter images without permission

| **Switzerland** | 8.1% | 2.6%, 3.8% | n/a |

### 8.2 OSS & IOSS REGISTRATION

**OSS (One-Stop Shop):**
- Applies to: Distance sellers in EU (non-EU sellers via platform)
- Threshold: Sales €10,000+ to consumers
- Registration: One EORI per member state (VAT authority portal)
- Filing: Quarterly returns with customer VAT to each member state
- Cost: Typically free; some countries charge €50-€150/year

**IOSS (Import One-Stop Shop):**
- Applies to: Non-EU sellers selling to EU consumers
- Threshold: No minimum (applies to all)
- Registration: Single IOSS registration per non-EU seller
- Filing: Monthly VAT declaration
- Responsibility: Platform collects/remits VAT on behalf of seller

**Key Difference:**
- OSS: EU-based seller selling to EU consumers
- IOSS: Non-EU seller selling to EU consumers (including import)

### 8.3 DEEMED SUPPLIER RULES (Article 14a VAT Directive)

**Marketplace Deemed Supplier Concept:**
- If marketplace meets criteria, marketplace (not seller) is VAT-responsible party
- Marketplace must:
  - Maintain payment from customer (holds money)
  - Control customer payment processing
  - Could physically deliver product (no requirement; just capability)

**Consequences of Deemed Supplier Status:**
- Marketplace liable for VAT compliance
- Marketplace must register for VAT in each country where sellers operate
- Marketplace files VAT returns (not individual sellers)
- Marketplace collects VAT at point of sale
- Seller receives net amount (VAT deducted)

**Which Marketplaces Are "Deemed Suppliers":**
- Amazon EU (marketplaces; not marketplace)
- eBay (borderline; depends on FBA status)
- Alibaba (for sales to EU consumers)
- Most European marketplaces (Kaufland, Bol.com, etc.)

### 8.4 VAT COMPLIANCE TIMELINE & DEADLINES

| Event | Deadline | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **First sale to EU (non-EU seller)** | Before sale | Register for IOSS OR appoint marketplace/intermediary |
| **IOSS registration** | Before sale | Complete registration via https://iossregistration.ec.europa.eu/ |
| **Monthly IOSS declaration** | 20th of following month | Submit VAT return + evidence of collection |
| **OSS quarterly return** | 25th of month following quarter | Submit returns to each member state VAT authority |
| **Threshold breach** | 10 calendar days | Notify member state if exceeding €10,000 OSS threshold |
| **Invoice issuance** | 30 days post-delivery | Must issue invoice with VAT details |
| **VAT reversal/refund** | As per returns process | If product returned, VAT credit within 30 days |

### 8.5 DAC7 REPORTING (NEW OBLIGATION)

**Directive (EU) 2021/1166 (DAC7 — Exchange of Information on Digital Platforms):**
- Effective date: 1 January 2024
- Applies to: Digital platform operators
- Reporting obligation: Information exchange to tax authorities

**Platform Reporting Requirements:**
- Seller turnover thresholds (varies by country; typically €2,000-€30,000/year)
- Seller identifying information (name, address, tax ID)
- Total revenue generated (aggregated)
- Reported to: Country's tax authority (who shares with other EU states)

**Seller Impact:**
- Platforms must report all sellers meeting threshold
- No VAT compliance relief (separate from IOSS/OSS)
- Tax authorities monitor unreported income
- Criminal penalties for tax evasion if unreported

### 9.1 COUNTRY-BY-COUNTRY EPR OBLIGATIONS

**GERMANY — VerpackG + LUCID + Stiftung EAR (WEEE)**

**VerpackG (Packaging Law) — Exact Steps:**

1. **LUCID Registration (Zentrale Stelle Verpackungsregister):**
   - URL: https://www.verpackungsregister.org/
   - Timeline: Before first product sale (no exceptions)
   - Required data:
     - Company name & address
     - Contact person (English proficiency required)
     - Product categories (plastic, paper, glass, metal, composite)
     - Annual packaging volume (tonnes)

2. **Dual System Contract:**
   - Mandatory if packaging material touched by consumer
   - Select from: Duales System Deutschland (DSD), Der Grüne Punkt, or others
   - Cost: €0.50-€2.00 per kg packaging (depends on material/volume)
   - Volume estimate: Must report prior to sales

3. **Compliance Markings:**
   - Der Grüne Punkt symbol (optional; recommended)
   - Or clear text: "Die Verpackung kann aus dem Sortiment der dualen Systeme zurückgegeben werden"

4. **Annual Reporting (by 31 March):**
   - Packaging volume placed on market (previous year)
   - Material breakdown (kg by type)
   - Dual system confirmation

**Stiftung EAR (WEEE Registration):**
- URL: https://www.ear-register.de/
- Products: Electrical/electronic products
- Annual cost: €0-€500 (based on turnover)
- Reporting: Annual; same deadline as VerpackG

**German EPR Penalties:**
- VerpackG violation: €5,000-€100,000 per product type
- EAR non-registration: €2,000-€50,000
- Criminal prosecution: Possible for intentional violations

   - Provided upon CITEO registration
   - Display on packaging: Required (text format acceptable)
   - Format: "UID: FRXXXXXX"

3. **Modulated Fee:**

   - Reusable packaging: Significant discount (75%+)

**French EPR Penalties:**

- CITEO reporting failure: €500-€5,000

### 9.6 CROSS-BORDER EPR STRATEGY (Selling in 10+ Countries)

**Practical Approach for Multi-Country EPR:**

1. **Packaging Standardization:**
   - Use single packaging design
   - Include all symbols/text required across highest-requirement countries (Germany, France)
   - Symbols included: Triman (France), grüner Punkt (Germany), CONAI code (Italy)

2. **Centralized Reporting System:**
   - Track annual tonnage by material (global)
   - Allocate tonnage to each country (by estimated sales)
   - Distribute to each country's system (OSS approach)

3. **Cost Management:**
   - Germany: Typically highest cost (VerpackG + dual system)
   - Budget €5,000-€15,000/year for 10-country EU operation
   - Increases with volume (€20,000+ for €10M+ annual sales)

4. **Compliance Calendar:**
   - Q1: Submit 2024 tonnage data to all systems (Jan-Mar 31)
   - Q2: Verify registrations; update product listings
   - Q3: Monitor for audit requests
   - Q4: Plan 2025 tonnage estimates
