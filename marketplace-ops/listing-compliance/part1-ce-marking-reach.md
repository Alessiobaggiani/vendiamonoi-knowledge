## SECTION 1: CE MARKING REQUIREMENTS (200+ LINES)

### 1.1 OVERVIEW & LEGAL BASIS

CE marking is mandatory under the **New Legislative Framework (NLF)** established by:
- **Decision No 768/2008/EC** – Common framework for marketing products
- **Regulation (EU) 2019/1020** – Market Surveillance Regulation (MSR)
- **Regulation (EU) 2023/988** – General Product Safety Regulation (GPSR)

The CE marking is a declaration of conformity certifying that a product meets all applicable EU legislation requirements. It is NOT a quality mark but a regulatory requirement.

**CRITICAL PRINCIPLE:** CE marking is the responsibility of the **manufacturer** or **authorized representative**. It cannot be delegated to distributors, importers, or marketplace operators.

### 1.3 DECLARATION OF CONFORMITY (DoC) EXACT REQUIREMENTS

**Legal Basis:** Decision 768/2008/EC, Annex IV

The Declaration of Conformity must contain EXACTLY the following information:

1. **Product Identification:**
   - Product name and model/version (if applicable)
   - Commercial reference number
   - Lot/batch number (where applicable)
   - Unique product identifier (UPI/UDI for medical devices)

2. **Manufacturer/Authorized Representative Information:**
   - Full legal name
   - Registered address (must be in EU for authorized representative)
   - Contact person name and email
   - Signature authority (name, title, date)

3. **Declaration Statement (EXACT WORDING REQUIRED):**
   > "The manufacturer (or authorized representative with registered address in [EU Member State]) hereby declares that the product [product name] is in conformity with the essential requirements of [list all applicable EU Directives/Regulations]."

4. **Applicable Harmonized Standards:**
   - List all EN standards applied (e.g., EN 60950-1:2005+A1:2009+A2:2013)
   - Notified Body number (if applicable, format: [Country Code][4-digit number])
   - Conformity assessment module used (per Decision 768/2008/EC Annex I)

5. **Reference to Technical File:**
   - Location/storage method of technical file
   - Validity period (minimum 10 years from market entry)

6. **Restricted Substances Declaration (where applicable):**
   - RoHS 2011/65/EU compliance statement
   - "The product is in conformity with Directive 2011/65/EU as amended by (EU) 2021/696"

7. **Date and Location:**
   - Date of declaration
   - Place of signature (EU territory reference)

**CRITICAL:** The DoC must be:
- Available in the official language(s) of the country where product is marketed
- Provided FREE of charge within 10 business days of request (per GPSR Article 14)
- Kept for 10 years after last product placed on market
- Signed by authorized representative (legal authority required)

**Non-Compliant DoC Examples that trigger enforcement action:**
- Blank/missing manufacturer address
- DoC with no technical file reference
- Undated or unsigned DoC
- DoC lacking applicable directive reference
- Using template text without specific product data

### 1.4 TECHNICAL FILE (TECHNICAL DOCUMENTATION) CONTENTS

**Legal Basis:** Decision 768/2008/EC, Annex VI

The Technical File must enable assessment of conformity. Exact contents vary by product type:

**MINIMUM CONTENTS (All products):**
1. General description with photos/drawings
2. Design specifications and risk analysis
3. List of all applicable standards used
4. Description of conformity assessment method
5. Results of conformity testing/verification
6. Quality system documentation (if applicable)
7. Inspection and test reports
8. Material safety data sheets (where hazardous materials present)
9. Declaration of Conformity
10. EU importer/authorized representative contact details

**ELECTRICAL PRODUCTS (LVD 2014/35/EU) must additionally include:**
- Circuit diagrams with component values
- PCB layout drawings
- Bill of Materials (BoM) with part numbers and safety-relevant components
- Schematic diagrams showing safety circuits
- Test reports for short-circuit capacity
- Thermal analysis per EN 60950-1 section 3.4.2
- Leakage current measurements
- Earth continuity testing results
- Clearance and creepage distance calculations per Table 2.1 (EN 60950-1)
- Protective earth conductor sizing per EN 60950-1 section 4.4.4

**RADIO EQUIPMENT (RED 2014/53/EU) must additionally include:**
- Frequency bands and power levels used
- Radio conformity testing per EN 301 489-1 (EMC)
- SAR testing (if body-worn) per EN 62209-1, ≤2.0 W/kg limit
- Radio module type-examination certificate (if using notified body)
- Conformity assessment module description (A1, B, B+C, or H per Annex VII)

**TOYS (Toy Safety Regulation 2024/1688) must additionally include:**
- Chemical testing per EN 71-3 (heavy metals in accessible materials):
  - Cadmium ≤75 mg/kg
  - Lead ≤160 mg/kg (300 mg/kg for painted/coated surfaces)
  - Mercury ≤560 mg/kg
  - Chromium ≤60 mg/kg
  - Aluminum ≤15,400 mg/kg
- Physical/mechanical testing per EN 71-1
- Flammability testing per EN 71-2
- Small parts testing results (choking hazard)
- Age warning justification

**COSMETICS (EC 1223/2009) must additionally include:**
- Product Information File (PIF) per Annex I (Section 3-6):
  - Substance data sheets for all ingredients
  - Stability/compatibility testing summary
  - Microbiological quality assurance data
  - Stability test summary (temperature, light, humidity)
  - Safety assessment report per Article 27
- CPNP notification proof
- Responsible Person designation letter

**FOOD (EU 1169/2011) must additionally include:**
- Product formulation/recipe
- Allergen assessment per Article 9 (identification of all 14 allergens)
- Nutritional analysis per Article 30 (5 mandatory values minimum):
  1. Energy value (kJ and kcal)
  2. Fat (g)
  3. Saturated fat (g)
  4. Carbohydrates (g)
  5. Sugars (g)
  6. Protein (g)
  7. Salt (g)
- Testing laboratory reports
- Origin documentation

**STORAGE REQUIREMENTS:**
- Physical Technical File: fireproof storage, EU location preferred
- Digital Technical File: encrypted backup, 10-year retention minimum
- Immediately accessible to:
  - Market surveillance authorities
  - Competent national authorities
  - Notified bodies (upon request)
- Average size: 50-500 pages depending on product complexity

### 1.5 NOTIFIED BODIES: WHEN REQUIRED VS SELF-DECLARATION

**Notified Bodies are MANDATORY for:**

| Regulation | Product Category | Module | Notified Body Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| RED 2014/53/EU | Class 2 radio equipment | B+C or H | Type examination; system audit |
| PPE 2016/425 | Category II PPE | B+C | Design type examination; surveillance |
| PPE 2016/425 | Category III PPE | B+C or H | Design exam; notified body surveillance |
| MDR 2017/745 | Class IIa medical devices | B+C | Type exam; surveillance audit |
| MDR 2017/745 | Class IIb medical devices | B+C | Mandatory notified body approval |
| MDR 2017/745 | Class III medical devices | B+C or H | Mandatory approval; full documentation |
| Pressure Equipment Directive 2014/68/EU | Category II vessels | B or B+C | Design verification; surveillance |
| PED 2014/68/EU | Category III vessels | B+C | Full type examination required |
| ATEX 2014/34/EU | Group II equipment | B+C | Type examination mandatory |
| Construction Products Regulation (CPR) 2011/89/EU | Critical building products | B+C | System 1 or System 2+ assessment |

**Self-Declaration PERMITTED for:**
- Low Voltage Directive (2014/35/EU) — manufacturer performs conformity assessment
- EMC Directive (2014/30/EU) — in-house testing allowed if technically qualified
- RoHS Directive (2011/65/EU) — no notified body requirement
- GPSR (EU 2023/988) — all products
- Most consumer products without specific NB requirement
- Most electrical appliances <1kW

**Finding a Notified Body:**
- EU NANDO database (New Approach Notified and Designated Organisations): https://nando.ec.europa.eu/
- Search by regulation, country, activity code
- Each notified body has specific scope (e.g., "PPE for head protection" vs all PPE)
- Notified body number format: [2-letter country code] + [4 digits] (e.g., DE 0123)
- Cost range: €2,000-€50,000 depending on complexity

**Notified Body Responsibilities (EXACT Legal Definition):**
Per Article 2(27) Decision 768/2008/EC:
> "A notified body is a body designated by a Member State to carry out conformity assessment activities defined in the relevant Union harmonisation legislation."

Key obligations:
1. Maintain expertise in assigned scope
2. Issue certificates within published timescales (typically 4-8 weeks)
3. Report to competent authority annually
4. Subject to accreditation per EN ISO/IEC 17065 or EN ISO/IEC 17021
5. Maintain independence (no ownership interest in manufacturer)
6. Carry professional liability insurance (minimum €2 million per occurrence)

### 1.6 ECONOMIC OPERATORS: MANUFACTURER vs IMPORTER vs DISTRIBUTOR vs AUTHORIZED REPRESENTATIVE

**CRITICAL DISTINCTION (Per Decision 768/2008/EC, Article 1 & GPSR Article 2):**

| Role | Legal Definition | CE Marking Responsibility | Technical File Obligation | DoC Requirement | Liability for Non-Compliance |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Manufacturer** | Any natural/legal person manufacturing a product or having it designed/manufactured under their responsibility | MUST affix | MUST keep (10 years) | MUST issue | FULL LIABILITY |
| **Authorized Representative** | A natural/legal person with registered address in EU authorized by manufacturer to act on their behalf | Can affix (if delegated) | MUST keep (manufacturer responsibility) | Can issue (on behalf of manufacturer) | Joint liability with manufacturer |
| **Importer** | Any natural/legal person established in EU placing non-EU manufactured product on EU market | CANNOT affix (unless authorized) | MUST keep (if aware of non-conformity) | MUST ensure availability | LIMITED LIABILITY (verify CE marking) |
| **Distributor** | Any natural/legal person in supply chain not manufacturer/importer | CANNOT affix | NO obligation | NO obligation | MINIMAL (verify CE presence) |

**SPECIFIC OBLIGATIONS PER ROLE:**

**MANUFACTURER Obligations (Decision 768/2008/EC):**
- Design product per applicable EU laws
- Conduct risk assessment per ISO 14971 (medical devices), ISO 12100 (machinery)
- Affix CE marking before market entry
- Prepare Technical File before CE marking affixing
- Issue Declaration of Conformity (signed, dated)
- Keep DoC + Technical File for 10 years (from last product manufactured)
- Provide DoC free within 10 days on request
- Cooperate with market surveillance authorities
- Implement corrective/preventive actions (CAPA)
- Withdraw/recall products if non-compliant (per GPSR Chapter VIII)
- Register product on relevant databases (CPNP for cosmetics, EUDAMED for medical devices, etc.)

**IMPORTER Obligations (GPSR Article 10, Decision 768/2008/EC):**
- Verify CE marking presence before accepting product
- Verify DoC availability
- Ensure product labeling in destination language
- Ensure technical documentation can be obtained
- Check for recall notices in Safety Gate database
- Maintain records of supplies (1 year minimum)
- Maintain records of customers (1 year minimum)
- Establish relationship with manufacturer or authorized representative
- Monitor market for complaints
- If non-conformity discovered: notify competent authority, withdraw from market
- Keep received DoC for 10 years

**AUTHORIZED REPRESENTATIVE Obligations (Article 3(c) Decision 768/2008/EC):**
- MUST have registered office in EU (not just "establishment")
- Must be expressly authorized by manufacturer (written agreement required)
- Can issue DoC on behalf of manufacturer
- Must keep Technical File with access to manufacturer's records
- Must cooperate with competent authorities
- Represent manufacturer in market surveillance procedures
- Cannot act as distributor simultaneously
- Signature authority for DoC

**DISTRIBUTOR Obligations (GPSR Article 11):**
- Check for CE marking (visual inspection)
- Not responsible for affixing CE marking
- Monitor for complaints/incidents
- Report serious incidents to manufacturer/importer
- Facilitate information access (e.g., contact details for consumer inquiries)
- Not liable for technical compliance IF:
  - Product has CE marking
  - Packaging is undamaged
  - Distributor has no reason to believe non-conformity

**DROP-SHIPPING SCENARIO (Common e-commerce model):**
Legal analysis: Drop-shipper (seller on marketplace) is typically **importer** under EU law because:
1. They place order with supplier
2. They assume commercial/financial risk
3. They control sales to end customers
4. They direct product delivery

Drop-shipper obligations:
- Verify CE marking on product images before listing
- Request Technical File/DoC from supplier
- Maintain 1-year records of supplier identity
- Monitor for recalls; delist products if notified
- Liable for misleading product descriptions (GPSR Article 4)

### 1.8 CE MARKING PLACEMENT, SIZE & VISIBILITY REQUIREMENTS

**Legal Basis:** Decision 768/2008/EC, Annex II (Specifications for CE Marking)

**EXACT DIMENSIONS:**
- Minimum height: 5 mm (except products where technical/economic impossibility evident)
- Proportions: width:height = 1:1 (square format required)
- For products <40 mm width: minimum 3 mm height acceptable
- Line thickness (strokes): proportional to height
- Must be readily visible on product itself

**LOGO DESIGN (EXACT SPECIFICATIONS):**
- Font: no specific requirement (typically sans-serif)
- Letters: "C" and "E" must have equal prominence
- Color: blue on white background acceptable; inverse acceptable
- No background pattern/graphic allowed
- Must be solid color (no transparency, no gradient)

**PLACEMENT RULES:**
1. **Affixing location:** Product body preferred; if impossible, packaging
2. **Visibility:** Clearly visible to consumer without disassembly
3. **Protected areas:** Protected from damage during normal use
4. **Multiple products:** Each product requires separate CE marking
5. **Product without surface suitable for marking:** Packaging must bear CE marking

**EXAMPLES OF COMPLIANT PLACEMENT:**
- Electrical appliance: rear panel, white background
- Radio equipment: bottom or back, 5mm minimum height
- Toy: packaging box, clearly visible
- Machinery: nameplate, easily visible to operator

**EXAMPLES OF NON-COMPLIANT PLACEMENT:**
- CE marking ≤2mm height (undersized)
- Hidden under label/sticker (not readily visible)
- CE marking on removable/consumable component (wrong location)
- Marking in document-only format (must be physical mark)
- CE on product interior without exterior backup

**PENALTY FOR INCORRECT PLACEMENT:**
- Not considered non-compliance IF:
  - Marking present on correct surface
  - Marking meets size/visibility requirements
  - Misleading placement corrected upon request
- Becomes non-compliance IF:
  - Manufacturer intentionally hid CE marking
  - Marking incorrectly applied to suggest non-applicable directives

### 1.9 NOTIFIED BODY SELECTION & ACCREDITATION

**Regulatory basis:** Regulation (EC) 765/2008 (Accreditation Regulation)

**ACCREDITATION REQUIREMENTS:**
- ISO/IEC 17065 (product certification bodies) OR
- ISO/IEC 17021-1 (management system certification) OR
- ISO/IEC 17020 (inspection bodies)
- Notified bodies must be accredited by:
  - Competent Accreditation Body (e.g., DAkkS in Germany, ILAC members)
  - Signatory to ILAC MRA (International Laboratory Accreditation Cooperation)

**FINDING NOTIFIED BODY IN NANDO:**
1. Visit https://nando.ec.europa.eu/
2. Select "Notified bodies" tab
3. Filter by:
   - Directive/Regulation
   - Product category
   - Member State
   - Activity (design review, audit, testing)
4. Review scope and contact details
5. Verify current accreditation status
6. Compare quotations (typical range: €3,000-€50,000)

**NOTIFIED BODY CERTIFICATE VALIDITY:**
- Product certificates: typically 3-5 years
- Quality system certificates: typically 3 years
- Audit frequency: minimum annual surveillance audits
- Renewal: full re-assessment required every 3 years

## SECTION 2: REACH REGULATION (150+ LINES)

### 2.1 OVERVIEW & LEGAL BASIS

**REACH** = **Registration, Evaluation, Authorisation and Restriction of Chemicals**

**Regulation:** EC 1907/2006 (as amended by Regulation (EU) 2018/1119 and subsequent amendments)

**Effective Date:** 1 June 2007 (phased approach)

**Enforcing Authority:** European Chemicals Agency (ECHA) based in Helsinki

**REACH applies to:**
- Manufacturers of chemical substances (≥1 tonne/year)
- Importers of substances in articles
- Suppliers of articles containing chemicals
- E-commerce sellers (if they supply articles with hazardous substances)

**KEY PRINCIPLE:** "No data, no market" — substances cannot be placed on market without registration

### 2.2 SVHC (SUBSTANCES OF VERY HIGH CONCERN) NOTIFICATION PROCESS

**Definition (Article 3(39)):** Substances meeting one of these criteria:
1. Carcinogenic (Category 1A/1B per GHS)
2. Mutagenic (Category 1A/1B per GHS)
3. Reproductive toxicant (Category 1A/1B per GHS)
4. Persistent, bioaccumulative, toxic (PBT)
5. Very persistent, very bioaccumulative (vPvB)
6. Endocrine disruptor (Article 57(f))
7. Environmental hazard comparable to CMR/PBT
8. Hazardous to environment (aquatic toxicity Cat 1A/1B)

**CANDIDATE LIST (Updated January & July Each Year):**
As of April 2026: **313 SVHCs** (constantly growing)

**KEY SVHCs IN CONSUMER PRODUCTS:**
- Lead (Pb) — jewelry, paint, ammunition
- Cadmium (Cd) — batteries, pigments, coatings
- Hexavalent chromium — metal coatings, leather tanning
- Phthalates (DEHP, DBP, BBP, DIBP) — PVC, adhesives, coatings
- Bisphenol A (BPA) — epoxy resins, polycarbonate
- Perfluorooctane sulfonic acid (PFOS) — protective textiles, water-resistant paper
- 4,4'-diaminodiphenylmethane (DDM) — polyurethane, adhesives
- Dibutyl phthalate (DBP) — inks, adhesives
- Diisobutyl phthalate (DIBP) — adhesives, coatings

### 2.3 NOTIFICATION THRESHOLD & OBLIGATION

**NOTIFICATION REQUIRED IF:**
1. Article contains SVHC
2. Concentration ≥0.1% w/w (weight/weight)
3. Annual quantity ≥1 tonne per SVHC
4. Seller places article on market in EU

**EXACT LEGAL REQUIREMENT (Article 33):**
> "A supplier of an article shall notify the European Chemicals Agency without delay and in any case within six months of the first placing on the market of the article, if the article as such or as part of another article contains a substance meeting the criteria in Article 57 and the substance is present in those articles in concentrations above 0.1% w/w."

**NOTIFICATION PROCESS STEP-BY-STEP:**

1. **Access ECHA Portal:**
   - https://echa.europa.eu/ → Information provided by suppliers
   - Register company account
   - Verify identity (EORI number required)

2. **Prepare Notification Package:**
   - Article name/description
   - SVHC identity (CAS number, EC number, chemical name)
   - Concentration (%) in article
   - Safe use instructions (if available)
   - Supplier contact details
   - Recipient contact details (downstream user)

3. **Submit via ECHA Portal:**
   - Each SVHC = separate notification
   - Multiple notifications allowed simultaneously
   - Receipt confirmation within 48 hours

4. **ECHA Processing:**
   - Entry into SCIP database (within 6 months)
   - Public disclosure (non-confidential data)
   - Notification to relevant enforcement authorities

**NON-COMPLIANCE PENALTIES:**
- First instance: €10,000-€100,000 (warning)
- Repeated breach: €100,000-€1,000,000
- Criminal penalties: up to 3 years imprisonment (serious cases)
- Mandatory product recall (GPSR Article 19)

### 2.4 SCIP DATABASE OBLIGATIONS

**SCIP** = **Substances of Concern In articles as such or in Products**

**Legal Basis:** REACH Article 69(5a), Decision 2022/2067/EU

**Effective Date:** 5 January 2021 (MANDATORY since this date)

**SCIP Registration REQUIRED for:**
- Articles containing SVHC ≥0.1% w/w
- Articles where manufacturer/importer is aware of substance presence
- Even if not notified to end users (yet)

**SCIP DATABASE ENTRY MUST CONTAIN:**
1. Identification code (auto-generated)
2. Article description
3. SVHC(s) present
4. Concentration (range acceptable: 0.1-1%, 1-10%, >10%)
5. Registrant (company contact)
6. Article usage description
7. Hazards/risks (free-text)

**PUBLIC ACCESS:**
- SCIP database searchable by article type
- https://echa.europa.eu/scip
- Supplier names HIDDEN (confidential)
- Allows consumers/authorities to identify articles with SVHCs

**PENALTIES FOR SCIP NON-COMPLIANCE:**
- €5,000-€50,000 per article not registered
- Mandatory registration within 30 days (written notice)
- Public naming and shaming (enforcement authority discretion)

### 2.5 REACH REGISTRATION REQUIREMENTS FOR IMPORTERS

**WHO MUST REGISTER:**
- Manufacturers of substances (any quantity)
- Importers of substances ≥1 tonne/year
- Importers of articles containing substances ≥1 tonne/year (rare)

**REGISTRATION THRESHOLDS:**

| Annual Quantity | Registration Required | Data Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| <1 tonne | NO | None |
| 1-10 tonnes | YES | Basic hazard summary |
| 10-100 tonnes | YES | Standard package per Annex VI |
| >100 tonnes | YES | Complete dossier per Annex VII-XI |
| >1000 tonnes | YES | Full hazard/PBT assessment |

**REGISTRATION PROCESS:**
1. Determine tonnage band
2. Prepare Safety Data Sheet (SDS) per Annex II
3. Compile technical dossier per applicable Annex
4. Submit via ECHA REACH-IT system
5. Receive dossier number
6. Pay fee: €500-€2,300 (depending on tonnage)
7. Participate in substance information exchange (SIEFs)

**SIEFs (Substance Information Exchange Forums):**
- Legal requirement to share test data
- Cost-sharing obligation
- Aims to avoid duplicate testing
- Typical cost: €10,000-€100,000 per substance (shared)

### Key Product Category Restrictions:

| Substance/Product | Restriction Details | Thresholds | Penalties |
|---|---|---|---|

**MEASUREMENT/TESTING STANDARDS:**
- Lead in jewelry: EN 71-3 (toy safety standard used for compliance)

**AUTHORIZATION PROCESS:**
1. Manufacturer/importer applies to ECHA
2. Demonstrates socio-economic benefit outweighs risk
3. Shows no alternatives available
4. ECHA review: typically 18-24 months
5. European Commission decision: accepts/rejects/modifies conditions
6. Authorization granted for defined use only
7. Periodic review every 5-7 years

**IMPACT ON E-COMMERCE SELLERS:**
- Most Annex XIV substances already phased out
- If selling article with Annex XIV substance:
  - Verify authorization is in place
  - Document authorization holder (supplier)
  - Cannot use product after sunset date without explicit authorization
  - Distribute SDS to downstream users
  - Maintain supply chain documentation

### 2.10 TESTING REQUIREMENTS & COST ESTIMATES

**THIRD-PARTY TESTING LABORATORIES:**
- Must be ISO/IEC 17025 accredited
- Average cost per substance: €500-€2,000
- Full product testing (10+ substances): €8,000-€30,000
- Turnaround time: 10-20 working days

**COST BREAKDOWN FOR TYPICAL CONSUMER PRODUCT (e.g., children's toy):**
- Lead testing: €400
- Cadmium testing: €400
- Mercury testing: €400
- Phthalate testing (4 substances): €800
- Chromium testing: €400
- Additional SVHC screening (10 substances): €3,000
- **Total estimated cost: €5,400-€6,000**

**TESTING FREQUENCY RECOMMENDATIONS:**
- First production batch: comprehensive testing
- Annual spot checks: 1 per 50 SKUs
- When supplier changes: full testing
- When formulation changes: relevant substance testing
