### 3.1 RoHS DIRECTIVE 2011/65/EU (RESTRICTION OF HAZARDOUS SUBSTANCES)

**Overview:**
- Effective date: 1 July 2006 (amended 2015, 2021)
- Latest amendment: EU 2021/696 (effective 4 July 2023)
- Scope: Electrical and electronic equipment (EEE) categories 1-10
- Core principle: Restrict use of 10 hazardous substances in EEE

**SUBSTANCE LIMITS TABLE (Exact thresholds):**

| Substance | Maximum Concentration (by weight) | Exceptions |
|---|---|---|
| Lead (Pb) | 0.1% w/w | Lead in high melting temperature solders (≥85% lead); lead in certain applications with high solderability; aviation/aerospace connectors |
| Cadmium (Cd) | 0.01% w/w | Cadmium in plating solutions (closed systems); nickel-cadmium emergency lighting batteries; cadmium in photovoltaic products; aviation applications |
| Mercury (Hg) | 0.1% w/w | Mercury in compact fluorescent lamps (CFLs); cold cathode fluorescent lamps (CCFLs); certain medical devices; measuring instruments |
| Hexavalent Chromium (Cr VI) | 0.1% w/w | Chromium trioxide in closed-loop systems; corrosion protection of carbon steel components; aerospace/defense applications |
| Polybrominated Biphenyls (PBB) | 0.1% w/w | None (complete ban) |
| Polybrominated Diphenyl Ethers (PBDE) | 0.1% w/w | None (complete ban) |
| Bis(2-ethylhexyl) phthalate (DEHP) | 0.1% w/w | Medical devices; high-temperature applications |
| Dibutyl phthalate (DBP) | 0.1% w/w | Medical devices; high-temperature applications |
| Benzyl butyl phthalate (BBP) | 0.1% w/w | Medical devices; high-temperature applications |
| Diisobutyl phthalate (DIBP) | 0.1% w/w | Medical devices; high-temperature applications |

**RoHS 2.0 vs RoHS 3.0 (EU 2021/696):**
- RoHS 2.0 (2011/65/EU): 6 substances (Pb, Cd, Hg, Cr VI, PBB, PBDE)
- RoHS 3.0 (as amended 2021): 10 substances (added 4 phthalates)
- Phase-in for phthalates: 22 July 2023 (medical device exemptions extended)

**RoHS MARKING REQUIREMENT:**
- CE marking required (not separate RoHS mark)
- Optional: RoHS logo (green checkmark + "RoHS" text)
- DoC must explicitly state: "Product complies with Directive 2011/65/EU as amended by (EU) 2021/696"

**SCOPE OF RoHS (10 Categories):**

| Category | Examples | Exemptions |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Large appliances** | Refrigerators, washing machines, dishwashers | Non-electric functionality; rare exemptions |
| **2. Small appliances** | Microwaves, toasters, kettles, hair dryers | Non-electric parts |
| **3. IT & telecommunications** | Computers, servers, laptops, printers | Spare parts; professional-only equipment |
| **4. Consumer equipment** | Cameras, DVD players, keyboards, mice | Some professional/industrial items |
| **5. Lighting equipment** | LED bulbs, fluorescent tubes, light fixtures | High-powered lighting (>25W, certain types) |
| **6. Electrical & electronic tools** | Power drills, saws, vacuum cleaners (electric) | Corded-only industrial tools |
| **7. Toys & leisure** | Electronic toys, drones, gaming devices | Non-electrical components |
| **8. Medical devices** | Hearing aids, electronic thermometers, monitors | Class III medical devices (some exemptions) |
| **9. Monitoring & control equipment** | Smoke detectors, thermostats, security systems | Industrial-only equipment |
| **10. Automatic dispensers** | Vending machines, ticketing machines | Large industrial dispensers (exceptions) |

**EXEMPTIONS UNDER RoHS:**
- Current exemptions list: Annex III-IV (20+ specific exemptions)
- Examples: Aircraft equipment, radioactive equipment, certain lead solder for high-reliability applications
- Exemptions reviewed every 4 years (next review: 2027)
- Manufacturers must justify continued use (cost-benefit analysis)

**RoHS TESTING & VERIFICATION:**
- Testing per EN IEC 62321 series (6 parts covering different substance groups)
- Testing labs: ISO/IEC 17025 accreditation required
- Typical cost: €1,500-€5,000 per product
- Desktop analysis: XRF (X-ray fluorescence) screening, typically €500-€1,000
- Confirmation testing: ICP-OES, typically €2,000-€4,000

**RoHS NON-COMPLIANCE PENALTIES:**
- Germany: €5,000-€100,000 per product type
- France: €3,000-€75,000 per violation
- Italy: €2,000-€50,000 per product
- Netherlands: €100,000+ fines + criminal prosecution
- UK: Fines up to £5,000 per article + seizure
- Product seizure and destruction: automatic if non-compliant

**WEEE CATEGORY CLASSIFICATION:**
Products must declare which category they belong to:
1. **Category 1:** Temperature exchange equipment (refrigerators, A/C, heat pumps)
2. **Category 2:** Screens & monitors (TV, computer monitors >100cm²)
3. **Category 3:** Lamps (fluorescent tubes, LED bulbs, high-intensity discharge)
4. **Category 4:** Large appliances (>25 kg weight, washing machines, ovens, dishwashers)
5. **Category 5:** Small appliances (<25 kg weight, microwaves, toasters, kettles, hair dryers)
6. **Category 6:** Small IT & telecommunications (laptops, tablets, mobile phones, keyboards, mice)
7. **Category 7:** Other equipment (cable, power supplies, circuit boards, consumer electronics)

**WEEE REPORTING OBLIGATIONS:**
- Annual reporting: quantity placed on market (kg) per category
- Deadline: 31 March (for previous calendar year)
- Reporting format: WEEE online portal (varies by country)
- Data required:
  - Product name/model
  - Quantity (units and weight in kg)
  - Category classification
  - For each model: average weight (kg)

**WEEE COLLECTION TARGETS (Producer Responsibility):**

| Year | Collection Target | Calculation Method |
|---|---|---|
| 2020-2024 | 65% of average WEEE collected | Based on 3-year average collection in member state |
| 2025-2029 | 70% of average WEEE collected | Revised calculation |
| 2030+ | 80% of average WEEE collected | Further increase |

**Producer means:**
- Arranging free take-back at point of sale
- OR paying into collective Producer Responsibility Organization (PRO)
- OR providing certified waste management proof

**WEEE MARKING REQUIREMENTS:**
- Crossed-out wheelie bin symbol (WEEE symbol) on product
- Size: ≥10% of product width OR minimum 8mm width
- Placement: clearly visible on product or packaging
- Format: black symbol on white/contrasting background

**WEEE NON-COMPLIANCE PENALTIES:**
- Germany: €5,000-€100,000 per product type
- France: €3,000-€75,000
- Italy: €2,000-€50,000
- Spain: €300-€50,000 (graduated scale)
- Netherlands: €100,000+ + seizure
- Criminal prosecution possible in serious cases

### 3.3 BATTERIES REGULATION (EU 2023/1542) - NEW OBLIGATIONS

**Overview:**
- Effective date: Provisions phased in 2024-2027
- Replaces: Directive 2006/66/EC (Batteries Directive)
- Core principles: Circular economy, recycled content requirements, carbon footprint

**KEY NEW OBLIGATIONS (as of 2026):**

**1. RECYCLED CONTENT REQUIREMENTS (New):**

| Battery Type | Recycled Content Targets |
|---|---|
| **Portable batteries (AA, AAA, button)** | 12% cobalt (2025), 4% lithium (2025), 4% lead (2025) |
| **Automotive batteries** | 12% cobalt (2025), 4% lithium (2025), 4% lead (2025) |
| **Industrial batteries** | Same targets as above |
| **Single-use batteries** | Phase-out by 2027 (limited exemptions for safety-critical applications) |

**2. CARBON FOOTPRINT DECLARATION (Mandatory):**

| Battery Type | Requirement | Deadline | Threshold |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Electric vehicle (EV) batteries** | Product carbon footprint (PCF) per kWh | 1 Jan 2025 | Mandatory labeling if ≥10 kWh capacity |
| **Rechargeable industrial batteries** | PCF per kWh threshold: 90 gCO₂eq/kWh | 1 Jan 2026 | Must declare if exceeds |
| **Batteries in portable electronics** | PCF per kWh (informational) | 1 Jan 2027 | Voluntary initially |

**Calculation methodology:** Cradle-to-gate per ISO 14040/14044 standards; must include:
- Mining and mineral processing
- Material refining
- Battery manufacturing
- Transportation to plant gate

**3. QR CODE & DIGITAL BATTERY PASSPORT:**

Mandatory for all batteries ≥2 kWh (as of 2027):
- Unique identifier (UUID or barcode)
- Links to battery information:
  - Battery identification (chemistry, capacity)
  - Recycled content percentage
  - Carbon footprint
  - Safety certifications
  - Recycling instructions
  - Warnings (hazard classification)

**4. DUE DILIGENCE REQUIREMENTS (Supply Chain):**
- Mandatory for all battery manufacturers >500 tonnes/year annual production
- Risk assessment required per Article 75 (supply chain due diligence)
- Covers: conflict minerals, child labor, environmental damage
- Documentation: supply chain mapping, audit reports
- Audit frequency: annual minimum
- Third-party verification by 1 Jan 2026

**5. LABELING & WARNINGS:**

**Mandatory Markings:**
- Chemistry type symbol (Li, Ni-Cd, Pb) on product face
- Capacity (mAh or Wh)
- Waste symbol (crossed-out wheelie bin)
- Chemical content declaration (if hazardous substances)
- Recycling instructions
- QR code (for large batteries)

**Example for lithium-ion battery:**
```
[symbol] Li 2500 mAh
[WEEE symbol]
[QR code]
CONTAINS COBALT & NICKEL — DO NOT INCINERATE
```

**6. COLLECTION & RECYCLING TARGETS:**

| Year | Collection Target | Recycling Efficiency |
|---|---|---|
| 2023-2029 | 50% of portable batteries sold | 65% material recovery (average) |
| 2030+ | 65% of portable batteries sold | 75% material recovery (target) |
| 2025+ | 95% of EV batteries in use (new cars) | 50% cobalt/nickel recovery |

**7. BATTERIES REGULATION REGISTRATION:**

Most EU countries still using **Directive 2006/66/EC** registration portals (transitioning):
- Germany: Stiftung EAR + LUCID system
- France: ADEME + regional authorities
- Italy: CONAI + RAEE consortium
- Costs: €200-€1,500/year depending on country

**BATTERIES REGULATION NON-COMPLIANCE:**
- Fines: €5,000-€50,000 per violation
- Product seizure: automatic
- Remediation cost: estimated €20,000-€200,000 (recall + disposal)
- Criminal liability: possible for serious breaches

### 3.4 TEXTILE REGULATION (EU 1007/2011) - FIBER COMPOSITION

**Overview:**
- Effective date: 1 May 2012
- Scope: All textiles marketed in EU (fibers, yarns, fabrics, finished articles)
- Core requirement: Accurate fiber composition labeling

**FIBER COMPOSITION RULES:**

**1. TOLERANCE MARGINS (Allowed variation from declared):**

| Fiber Type | Tolerance Margin | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Cotton (declared 100%)** | ±3% | Can be 97-100% |
| **Wool (declared 100%)** | ±2% | Can be 98-100% |
| **Synthetic fibers (declared 100%)** | ±2% | Can be 98-100% |
| **Blended fabrics (e.g., 65% cotton/35% polyester)** | ±3% for each fiber | 62-68% cotton, 32-38% polyester acceptable |
| **Blends with elastane ≤5%** | ±2% for elastane | Declared 2% elastane can be 0-4% |
| **Blends with elastane >5%** | ±3% for elastane | Declared 8% elastane can be 5-11% |
| **Animal hair fibers** | ±2% individual fibers | Must declare each type separately |

**2. MULTI-COMPONENT TEXTILE LABELING RULES:**

All fibers ≥1% of weight must be declared:
```
Example jacket label:
- Outer fabric: 100% wool
- Lining: 100% polyester
- Filling: 70% polyester / 30% duck down
- Elastane reinforcement: 2%
```

If component <1% of total weight:
```
Can state: "Contains elastane" (no percentage required)
```

**3. SPECIAL PRODUCT CATEGORIES:**

| Category | Labeling Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Felt/non-woven** | All fibers ≥1% must be listed; process name not sufficient |
| **Leather/fur/skin combinations** | Must declare: "100% genuine leather" or specify leather type |
| **Down/feather products** | Percentage of down vs feathers vs synthetics; fill power rating (loft) optional but if stated must comply with ISO 12130 |
| **Recycled fibers** | Can use term "recycled" if ≥50% post-consumer waste; must declare original fiber type |
| **Regenerated fibers** | Specify type: viscose, modal, lyocell, acetate (not just "regenerated") |
| **Natural rubber latex** | Must declare percentage if ≥1% |
| **Elastane/spandex** | Always declare separately if ≥1% |

**4. LANGUAGE & LABELING REQUIREMENTS:**

- Language: Official language(s) of country where marketed
- Format: Percentage (minimum of 1 decimal place): e.g., "82.5% cotton"
- Rounding: Permitted (82.45% rounds to 82.4%; 82.55% rounds to 82.6%)
- Arrangement: Descending order by weight (highest % first)
- Placement: Visible, durable, not easily confused with other text
- Size: Readable text (minimum 3-4 point font)
- Method: Label, tag, or permanent marking acceptable

**5. TESTING & VERIFICATION:**

- Testing per ISO 1833 series (18 parts covering fiber identification)
- Laboratory: ISO/IEC 17025 accredited
- Cost: €300-€800 per test
- Multiple fibers: €800-€2,000 (comprehensive analysis)

**TEXTILE REGULATION PENALTIES:**
- Germany: €2,000-€20,000 per product type
- France: €1,500-€15,000
- Italy: €500-€10,000
- Spain: €300-€5,000
- Consumer law fines can exceed these substantially

### 3.5 TOY SAFETY (2009/48/EC → NEW Toy Safety Regulation 2024/1688)

**Overview:**
- Previous regulation: Directive 2009/48/EC
- NEW regulation: Toy Safety Regulation (EU) 2024/1688
- Effective date: **1 December 2024** (for new products) / **1 June 2025** (compliance mandatory)
- Scope: All toys intended for children under 14 years

**NEW REGULATION KEY CHANGES:**
- Enhanced chemical limits (stricter thresholds)
- Phased ban on certain substances
- Stronger enforcement & traceability
- CPSC (Consumer Product Safety Commission) aligned standards

**EN 71 SERIES - COMPLETE TESTING REQUIREMENTS:**

| EN 71 Part | Subject | Key Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| **EN 71-1** | Mechanical & physical safety | Small parts test (choking hazard), sharp edges, sharp points, bite marks, migration of materials, impact testing |
| **EN 71-2** | Flammability | Surface flammability (Δt ≤60 sec); pile flammability (after ignition); special rules for masks, beards, costumes |
| **EN 71-3** | Toxicological safety (heavy metals) | Chemical element limits per table below |
| **EN 71-4** | Swallowing hazard (obsolete, merged into 71-1) | Historically covered small parts testing |
| **EN 71-5** | Non-electric or thermal energy toys | Safety of mechanical toys, thermal stability |
| **EN 71-6** | Graphical and textual warnings | Age labeling, warning symbols, instruction clarity |
| **EN 71-7** | Finger paint safety | Chemical composition limits; non-toxic formulation |
| **EN 71-8** | Activity toys (swings, slides, seesaws) | Structural stability, entrapment hazards, fall testing |
| **EN 71-9** | Organic chemical safety (phthalates & other compounds) | Phthalate limits (DEHP, DBP, BBP, DIBP ≤0.1% w/w each) |
| **EN 71-10** | Incomplete sets & scatters | Structural integrity after assembly |
| **EN 71-11** | Gross/fine motor skills (obsolete) | Historically covered developmental toy safety |
| **EN 71-12** | N,N-Dimethyl-p-toluidine (DMPT) restrictions | Limiting specific chemical in toy paints |
| **EN 71-13** | Migration from paints, coatings, adhesives, textiles | Testing for specific migration of lead, cadmium, etc. |

**HEAVY METALS LIMITS IN TOYS (EN 71-3, Table D.1 — EXACT THRESHOLDS):**

| Chemical Element | Limit (mg/kg) | Test Method | Product Category Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Lead (Pb)** | 160 mg/kg general; 300 mg/kg painted/coated surfaces | ICP-OES or XRF | Higher limit justified if surface material incompatible with paint |
| **Cadmium (Cd)** | 75 mg/kg | ICP-OES | No exceptions; complete ban in some applications |
| **Mercury (Hg)** | 560 mg/kg | Cold vapor AAS | Historical limit; mostly phased out |
| **Chromium (Cr) - Total** | 60 mg/kg | ICP-OES | Total chromium; Cr VI specifically banned at any level |
| **Chromium (Cr VI) - Hexavalent** | 0 mg/kg (ban) | ISO 17075 | Complete prohibition under new regulation |
| **Arsenic (As)** | 3.8 mg/kg | ICP-OES | Strict limit for toys for young children |
| **Barium (Ba)** | 1000 mg/kg | ICP-OES | Non-hazardous at typical limits |
| **Aluminum (Al)** | 15,400 mg/kg | ICP-OES | High limit; aluminum not primary concern |
| **Bromine (Br)** | 1200 mg/kg | ICP-OES | Covers brominated flame retardant residues |
| **Copper (Cu)** | 9200 mg/kg | ICP-OES | High threshold |
| **Nickel (Ni)** | 8300 mg/kg | ICP-OES | Dermal contact limit in EN 1811 if >0.5 μg/cm²/week |
| **Tin (Sn)** | 15,000 mg/kg | ICP-OES | High limit for toy metals |
| **Zinc (Zn)** | 39,000 mg/kg | ICP-OES | High limit |
| **Molybdenum (Mo)** | 8000 mg/kg | ICP-OES | Modern limit |
| **Selenium (Se)** | 330 mg/kg | ICP-OES | Significant limit |
| **Vanadium (V)** | 330 mg/kg | ICP-OES | Regulated limit |
| **Antimony (Sb)** | 45 mg/kg | ICP-OES | Relatively strict limit |
| **Boron (B)** | 1200 mg/kg | ICP-OES | High limit |
| **Cobalt (Co)** | 10.5 mg/kg | ICP-OES | Strict limit for skin-contact toys |
| **Thallium (Tl)** | 1 mg/kg | ICP-OES | Extremely strict; essentially banned |

**PHTHALATE LIMITS (EN 71-9 & NEW REGULATION):**

| Phthalate | Molecular Name | Limit (w/w) | Products Affected |
|---|---|---|---|
| **DEHP** | Di(2-ethylhexyl)phthalate | ≤0.1% | All PVC toys; soft plastics |
| **DBP** | Dibutyl phthalate | ≤0.1% | Adhesives, coatings in toys |
| **BBP** | Benzyl butyl phthalate | ≤0.1% | Toys with vinyl components |
| **DIBP** | Diisobutyl phthalate | ≤0.1% | Modern vinyl toys |
| **DIHP** | Dihexyl phthalate | ≤0.1% (new as of 2024) | Replacement phthalates |
| **DIPP** | Diisopentyl phthalate | ≤0.1% (new as of 2024) | Alternative phthalates |

**AGE-BASED WARNINGS & LABELING:**

| Age Category | Required Labeling | Choking Hazard Warning | Additional Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0-3 years** | Clear age marking: "Not suitable for children under 3 years" | "WARNING: Choking hazard — contains small parts" | Highest safety standards; no small detachable parts |
| **3-8 years** | "Not suitable for children under 3 years" (if applicable) | "WARNING: Choking hazard" (if small parts) | Moderate safety requirements |
| **8-14 years** | "Not suitable for children under 8 years" (if necessary) | Age-appropriate hazard warnings | Minimal choking hazard restrictions |
| **14+ years** | No mandatory age warning | None (general safety only) | Treated as general consumer product |

**SMALL PARTS TEST (EN 71-1, Section 5.10):**
- Test cylinder: 31.75 mm diameter × 31.75 mm deep ("test tube")
- Fits inside = potential choking hazard
- Cannot be placed on market for <8 year children if small parts present
- Exception: Small parts acceptable for 3-8 year olds if not small enough to fit entirely in mouth

**TOY SAFETY TESTING & COSTS:**
- Full EN 71 testing (parts 1-3 basic): €2,000-€5,000
- Complete test package (71-1,2,3,9,13): €5,000-€12,000
- Chemical testing (heavy metals + phthalates): €3,000-€8,000
- Batch testing (per production run): €1,500-€4,000

**TOY SAFETY NON-COMPLIANCE:**
- Germany: €10,000-€100,000 per product
- France: €5,000-€50,000
- Spain: €3,000-€30,000
- Product ban: automatic for serious chemical violations
- Criminal prosecution: possible for intentional non-compliance

### 3.6 FOOD INFORMATION REGULATION (EU 1169/2011)

**Overview:**
- Effective date: 13 December 2014
- Scope: ALL food products marketed in EU
- Core requirement: Mandatory nutrition facts & allergen declaration

**14 MANDATORY ALLERGENS (Must Always Declare):**

| # | Allergen | Common Sources |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Cereals containing gluten (wheat, barley, rye, oats, spelt, kamut) | Bread, pasta, beer, soy sauce |
| **2** | Crustaceans (shrimp, crab, lobster, crayfish) | Shellfish, seafood products |
| **3** | Eggs | Mayonnaise, pasta, baked goods |
| **4** | Fish | Sushi, fish sauce, Worcestershire sauce |
| **5** | Peanuts (legume) | Peanut butter, protein bars, Asian sauces |
| **6** | Soybeans | Soy sauce, tofu, many processed foods, lecithin |
| **7** | Milk (lactose, casein) | Cheese, butter, chocolate, creamy sauces |
| **8** | Tree nuts (almonds, hazelnuts, walnuts, cashews, pistachios, pecans, Brazil, macadamia, pine) | Nut butters, muesli, chocolates, desserts |
| **9** | Celery (including celeriac root) | Soups, broths, spice mixes |
| **10** | Mustard | Condiments, salad dressings, processed meats |
| **11** | Sesame seeds | Tahini, halva, bagels, Asian dishes |
| **12** | Sulfites (≥10 mg/kg) | Wine, dried fruit, processed foods |
| **13** | Lupine (lupin beans) | Lupine flour (bread substitute), pasta |
| **14** | Mollusks (clams, mussels, snails, squid, oysters) | Seafood, sauces, Asian products |

**ALLERGEN DECLARATION RULES:**

- **Bold or contrasting formatting** required: allergens must stand out
- **Clear language:** Common names (not chemical terminology)
- **Placement:** Ingredient list (with visual emphasis) or separate "Contains:" statement
- **Multiple products:** Allergen info per product (not just general)
- **Cross-contamination:** Optional disclosure; if stated, must be accurate

**Example compliant label:**
```
INGREDIENTS: Wheat flour, eggs, milk, sugar, butter, hazelnuts, vanilla extract.

**CONTAINS: WHEAT, EGGS, MILK, TREE NUTS (HAZELNUT)**
May also contain peanuts (processed in shared facility).
```

**NUTRITION DECLARATION (MANDATORY for all packaged foods):**

**7 MANDATORY NUTRIENTS per 100g/ml:**
1. **Energy value** (kJ and kcal)
2. **Fat** (total, in grams)
3. **Saturated fatty acids** (in grams)
4. **Carbohydrates** (total, in grams)
5. **Sugars** (in grams)
6. **Protein** (in grams)
7. **Salt** (in grams) = sodium × 2.5

**OPTIONAL ADDITIONAL NUTRIENTS:**
- Monounsaturated fatty acids
- Polyunsaturated fatty acids
- Polyols (sugar alcohols)
- Starch
- Fiber
- Specific vitamins/minerals (if present in significant quantity)

**NUTRITION CALCULATION METHOD:**
- Per 100g or per 100ml (mandatory)
- Per serving (optional but common)
- Per ounce (acceptable in markets using imperial)

**EXAMPLE NUTRITION LABEL:**
```
Nutritional information per 100g:
Energy: 2100 kJ / 500 kcal
Fat: 20 g
  of which saturated: 8 g
Carbohydrate: 62 g
  of which sugars: 30 g
Protein: 8 g
Salt: 0.5 g
```

**MANDATORY PARTICULAR LABELING:**

- **Product name:** Clear, descriptive
- **Net quantity:** In grams/milliliters (also can state in metric units)
- **Ingredient list:** All ingredients in descending order by weight
- **Manufacturer/importer:** Name & address (EU address required)
- **Country of origin:** Mandatory for certain products (honey, olive oil, beef)
- **Lot/batch number:** For traceability
- **Best before/use by date:** Format: DD/MM/YYYY
- **Storage conditions:** If special conditions required
- **Instructions for use:** If necessary for safety
- **Alcohol content:** If ≥1.2% ABV
- **Warning for specific substances:** Aspartame, caffeine, quinine

**TESTING & COMPLIANCE VERIFICATION:**
- Laboratory testing: €500-€2,000 per product
- Turnkey nutrition analysis service: €1,500-€5,000
- Cost includes: physical testing + label verification

**FOOD INFORMATION NON-COMPLIANCE PENALTIES:**
- Germany: €2,000-€30,000 per product
- France: €1,500-€15,000
- Italy: €1,000-€10,000
- UK: Up to £20,000 fine + seizure
- Product recall: automatic if allergen mislabeled

### 3.7 COSMETICS REGULATION (EC 1223/2009)

**Overview:**
- Effective date: 11 July 2013
- Scope: ALL cosmetic products marketed in EU (makeup, skincare, haircare, fragrances, etc.)
- Core requirement: CPNP notification + responsible person appointment

**CPNP NOTIFICATION PROCESS:**

**1. CPNP DATABASE (European Cosmetics Portal):**
- URL: https://ec.europa.eu/growth/tools-databases/nando/
- Access: Requires registration with EORI number
- Notification timeline: **Before market entry** (no grace period)
- Cost: Free
- Validity: Must be maintained/updated per product changes

**2. NOTIFICATION TIMELINE (STRICT DEADLINES):**

| Requirement | Timeline |
|---|---|
| CPNP notification submission | Before market entry (no exceptions) |
| Portal acceptance/confirmation | 48-72 hours (automatic acceptance) |
| Update after reformulation | Within 1 month of change |
| New ingredient added | Re-notification required (no delay acceptable) |
| Ingredient removed | Can be immediate (notification within 1 month) |
| Change of Responsible Person | Notification change within 1 month |

**3. CPNP REQUIRED DATA PER PRODUCT:**

A. **Product Identification:**
   - Trade name
   - INCI list (International Nomenclature of Cosmetic Ingredients)
   - Responsible Person details
   - Date of notification

B. **Responsible Person Information (MANDATORY):**
   - Full legal name
   - Registered address (MUST be in EU/EEA)
   - Email contact
   - Signatory authority

C. **Chemical Composition:**
   - Complete ingredient list with INCI names
   - Percentage concentration (for regulated substances)
   - CAS/EC number identification
   - pH (if ≤3.5 or ≥11.0 pH range)

D. **Substance Restrictions (Article 15):**
   - Check against banned substances list
   - Comply with restricted substance limits
   - Declare if substance in Candidate List

E. **Safety Assessment:**
   - Responsible Person must ensure Product Safety Assessment (PSA) completed
   - PSA must follow SCCS guidelines (Scientific Committee on Consumer Safety)
   - Physical copy kept (EU requirement)
   - Not uploaded to CPNP but required for inspection

**4. BANNED INGREDIENTS IN COSMETICS (1,600+ substances):**

**Selected Examples (High-Risk Substances):**

| Substance | Ban Type | Category |
|---|---|---|
| **Heavy metals (Pb, Cd, Hg, As)** | Complete ban | Carcinogenic/toxic |
| **Asbestos & asbestos-like fibers** | Complete ban | Carcinogenic |
| **Coal tar & derivatives** | Complete ban | Carcinogenic |
