### 2.1 EU Intra-Community Trade Framework

**Legal Basis:** Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union (TFEU) Articles 28-36

**Key Principle:** Free movement of goods between EU member states (Italy and Portugal)

**Simplified Logistics Benefits:**
- No customs duties or tariffs
- No import taxes on goods themselves (VAT handled separately)
- No customs clearance required
- Direct shipping allowed
- Simplified documentation (CMR waybill is sufficient)

**Applicable to:**
- All goods that are properly documented as EU-origin
- All sellers with valid VAT registration
- All shipping methods (parcel, letter, pallet)

## SECTION 4: TAX & VAT COMPLIANCE

### 4.1 VAT Registration Requirements (Portugal)

**Threshold for Mandatory VAT Registration:**

**EU Rules for B2C Sales:**
- **Old Rule (pre-2021):** EUR 100,000 threshold (now obsolete)
- **New Rule (Jan 1, 2021 onwards):** EUR 10,000 threshold applied
- **Current Rule (Jan 1, 2025 onwards):** EUR 0 threshold (ALL B2C sales to customers in Portugal require VAT registration)

**Practical Impact:** Vendiamonoi MUST register for Portuguese VAT even if selling just EUR 1 of goods to Portugal

**How to Register:**
- Cannot register directly in Portugal (as Italian company)
- Must use **One-Stop-Shop (OSS) scheme** (see Section 4.2)

**Consequence of Non-Registration:**
- Penalties up to 20% of unpaid VAT
- Account suspension on Worten
- Portuguese tax authority can fine Italian company

### 4.2 One-Stop-Shop (OSS) Registration (VAT Simplification Scheme)

**What is OSS?**

VAT One-Stop-Shop is an EU-wide simplified tax regime allowing sellers from one EU country to report VAT for all their EU-wide B2C (customer) sales in a single quarterly return, filed in their home country.

**How It Works for Vendiamonoi (Italian Seller):**

1. **File OSS Registration** with Italian tax authority (Agenzia delle Entrate)
2. **Declare Portugal as destination country** in OSS registration
3. **Each quarter (Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4):** File single OSS return in Italy listing all VAT owed to all EU countries
4. **Pay VAT** directly to Italian tax authority; Italy distributes to Portugal
5. **No separate Portuguese VAT return** needed

**Alternative: B2C OSS vs. IOSS**

| Scheme | For Sellers From... | For Goods Worth... | Quarterly Return? | Key Advantage |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|-------------------|---------------|
| **OSS B2C** | EU member states | Any amount | YES (each EU country listed) | Simplest; all suppliers |
| **IOSS** | Outside EU (UK, US, etc.) | Up to EUR 150 per package | YES (simplified) | Designed for dropshippers |

**Vendiamonoi Uses:** OSS B2C (because Italian company = EU seller)

### 4.3 VAT Rates in Portugal (2026)

**Mainland Portugal:**
- **Standard VAT rate:** 23%
- **Applies to:** Electronics, appliances, most consumer goods
- **Examples:** TV, laptop, phone, smart speaker → 23%

**Reduced VAT Rates (Special Items):**
- **6%:** Food, medicine, books
- **13%:** Restaurant services (not applicable to marketplace)
- **Exemptions:** Financial services, certain utilities

**Madeira (Autonomous Region):**
- **VAT rate:** 22% (1% lower than mainland)
- **Applies if:** Customer address is Madeira (Funchal, Ponta Delgada)

**Azores (Autonomous Region):**
- **VAT rate:** 16% (7% lower than mainland)
- **Applies if:** Customer address is Azores (islands)

**Practical Impact:** Must program Worten listing to apply correct VAT based on destination address

**Worten System Capability:** Yes, Worten can auto-apply VAT rates by postal code region

### 4.4 Pricing Strategy: Net Price vs. Gross Price

**Option 1: Display Gross Price (Recommended)**
- List price in Worten includes 23% VAT
- Customer sees final price (what they pay)
- Example: Sell ASUS laptop at EUR 799.99 (includes VAT)
- Itemization:
  - Net price: EUR 650.49
  - VAT (23%): EUR 149.51
  - **Total: EUR 799.99**

**Option 2: Display Net Price (Not Recommended)**
- List price is before VAT
- Customer sees tax separately at checkout
- Example: Sell ASUS laptop at EUR 650.49 (before VAT)
- At checkout:
  - Net: EUR 650.49
  - VAT (23%): +EUR 149.51
  - **Total: EUR 799.99**
- **Problem:** Customers confused; higher cart abandonment; Worten's system expects gross price

**Worten System:** Always displays gross price to customers (Worten calculates VAT component)

**Best Practice:** Set Worten price as gross price; Worten handles VAT calculation and reporting

### 4.5 VAT on Shipping Costs

**Portuguese Rule:** Shipping costs ARE subject to 23% VAT

**How It Works:**
1. Seller charges customer EUR 10 shipping
2. VAT on shipping: EUR 10 × 23% = EUR 2.30
3. Customer pays: EUR 12.30 total shipping

**Worten Handling:**
- Seller sets shipping fee in Worten (e.g., EUR 10)
- Worten automatically adds VAT at checkout
- Customer sees total with VAT included

**OSS Reporting:**
- Shipping VAT is reported as part of quarterly OSS return
- Include shipping VAT in total EU VAT liability calculation

### 4.6 Intrastat Reporting (Italy) — CRITICAL REQUIREMENT

**What is Intrastat?**

Intrastat is an EU system for collecting statistics on goods traded between member states. Italy REQUIRES all sellers to file intrastat declarations for cross-border trade.

**Seller's Obligation:**
- **File intrastat declaration** each month
- **Deadline:** 20th of following month (e.g., Jan sales due by Feb 20)
- **With:** Agenzia delle Entrate (Italian tax authority)
- **For:** All goods shipped to Portugal (even EUR 1)
- **No threshold exemption** for Italian sellers (unlike some other EU countries)

**What Info Must Be Declared:**
- Order date
- Customer name and address (Portugal)
- Product description
- HS code (Harmonized System code for tariff classification)
- Quantity
- Value (EUR)
- Weight (kg)
- Nature of transaction (sale, return, replacement, etc.)

**HS Code Examples (Electronics Category):**
- Laptop: 8471.30 (automatic data processing machines)
- Smartphone: 8517.62 (telephone sets)
- Smart TV: 8528.72 (television receivers)
- Tablet: 8471.30 (considered automatic data processing)

**Where to Find HS Codes:**
- Official EU tariff: https://ec.europa.eu/taxation_customs/business/calculation-customs-duties/rules-origin/introduction/hs-tariff_en
- Italian customs: https://www.agenziadogane.gov.it/
- Software (Recommended): Use accounting software with intrastat module (e.g., Debitoor, Wave, Fatture in Cloud)

**Penalty for Non-Filing:**
- Fine: EUR 250-500 per missing declaration
- Administrative penalty: Up to EUR 3,000
- Repeated violations: Potential account suspension, tax assessment

**Practical Management:**
1. **Monthly process:** Export Worten orders each month
2. **Classify products:** Assign HS codes to product catalog
3. **Aggregate data:** Sum by HS code, destination (Portugal), value
4. **File electronically:** Submit to Agenzia delle Entrate via:
   - AIDA software (free, official)
   - Commercialista/accountant (delegated)
   - Accounting software with intrastat feature
5. **Keep records:** Maintain documentation 5+ years for audit

**Cost to Manage:** EUR 0-300/month if self-filing; EUR 300-500/month if outsourced to accountant

## SECTION 6: SELLER PERFORMANCE & KPI METRICS

### 6.1 Worten Seller Performance Scoring System

**What is it?**

Worten ranks sellers using an algorithm that assigns a "performance score" (0-100 scale). This score determines:
- Visibility in search results
- Eligibility for "Buy Box" (default seller if multiple offer same product)
- Risk of account suspension
- Customer trust perception

**Key Metrics Tracked (Weighted):**

| Metric | Weight | Threshold | Impact |
|--------|--------|-----------|--------|
| **On-time Delivery** | 25% | 95%+ | Poor delivery = major score drop |
| **Return/Refund Rate** | 20% | <5% | High returns = score penalty |
| **Customer Complaints** | 20% | <2% | Complaints = immediate penalty |
| **Message Response Rate** | 15% | 90%+ within 24h | Slow responses hurt score |
| **Cancel Rate** | 10% | <2% | Canceling orders = penalty |
| **Product Accuracy** | 10% | 100% match to listing | Mismatches hurt score |

**Performance Score Bands:**

| Score | Status | Benefits | Risks |
|-------|--------|----------|-------|
| **85-100** | Excellent | Buy Box eligible; prominent search placement | None |
| **75-84** | Good | Moderate search visibility | Loses Buy Box in competitive niches |
| **65-74** | Fair | Lower visibility; customer skepticism | At risk of suspension |
| **<65** | Poor | Very low visibility; likely suspension warning | Suspension likely within 30 days |

### 6.2 On-Time Delivery Rate (Most Critical)

**Definition:** % of orders delivered within 5 business days of order placement

**How Worten Measures It:**
- Day 0 = order placed in Worten
- Day 5 = deadline for delivery confirmation (tracking code in system)
- Delivery = valid tracking code showing package delivered to destination
- NOT delivery = no tracking update by Day 5

**Threshold:** Worten wants 95%+ of orders delivered by Day 5

**Calculation Example:**
- Month: 100 orders placed
- 95 delivered within 5 days
- 5 delivered on Day 6-7
- On-time delivery rate: 95%
- **Result:** Score maintained; no penalty

**If Below 95%:**
- 90-95%: Score penalty -5 points
- 85-90%: Score penalty -10 points
- <85%: Warning + -15 points; potential suspension

**Improvement Tactics:**
1. **Ship immediately** (Day 0-1 after order)
2. **Use express carriers** (DHL) for tight deadlines
3. **Pick up daily** from carrier to avoid warehouse delays
4. **Track shipments real-time** and escalate delays
5. **Use ShippyPro or similar** to automate label generation (saves 30 min/day)

### 6.3 Return and Refund Rate

**Definition:** % of delivered orders that customers return within 14-day window

**Industry Benchmark:** 3-5% for electronics; 1-2% for appliances

**Worten Threshold:** <5% (goal is 2-3%)

**Calculation Example:**
- Month: 100 orders delivered
- 4 customers initiate returns
- Return rate: 4%
- **Result:** Within threshold; no penalty

**If Above 5%:**
- 5-7%: Performance score penalty -3 points; review required
- 7-10%: Score penalty -8 points; warning issued
- >10%: Major red flag; suspension review initiated

**Causes of High Return Rate:**
1. **Inaccurate product descriptions** ("I didn't expect this")
2. **Damage in shipping** (seller's carrier choice = seller's problem)
3. **Missing parts** (not properly verified before shipping)
4. **Customer's unrealistic expectations** (different from listing photos)
5. **Defective/dead-on-arrival products** (supplier quality issue)

**Reduction Tactics:**
1. **Detailed product photos** (multiple angles, size reference)
2. **Honest descriptions** (don't overstate features)
3. **Dimension/weight specs** (avoid "wow" factor surprises)
4. **Use quality carriers** (DHL, GLS) vs. cheapest option
5. **Inspect products** before shipping (spot-check 10% of orders)
6. **Provide excellent packaging** (professional, protective)

### 6.4 Customer Complaint Rate and Messaging

**Definition:** % of orders resulting in customer complaints or negative communications

**What Counts as Complaint:**
- Negative messaging ("this is not what I ordered")
- Return requests (initiated by customer, not seller)
- Reported damage/defects
- Delivery issues (late arrival)
- Seller behavior complaints (rude responses)

**Worten Threshold:** <2% complaint rate

**Message Response Rate Component:**
- Must respond to messages within 24 hours
- Response quality assessed (helpful vs. dismissive)
- Language: Portuguese preferred (translations accepted but slower)
- Goal: 90%+ response rate within 24h

**Impact:**
- <90% response rate: -5 score points + warning
- <80% response rate: -10 score points; account review
- <70% response rate: Suspension risk

**Response Best Practices:**
1. **First response within 2 hours** (faster than required; builds trust)
2. **Acknowledge the issue** ("I understand your concern")
3. **Provide solution** (refund, replacement, troubleshooting)
4. **Use professional tone** (avoid casual language)
5. **Close the loop** (confirm customer satisfied or escalate)

When multiple sellers offer the same product on Worten, customers see a "Select Seller" dropdown. The default (most prominent) seller shown is the "Buy Box" holder.

### 7.6 Chargebacks and Payment Disputes

Customer disputes payment with their credit card company or bank ("I didn't authorize this" or "Goods not received"), claiming refund. Bank investigates and typically sides with customer.

**Chargeback Impact on Seller:**
1. **Payout deduction:** Chargeback amount + fee

3. **Account risk:** Multiple chargebacks (5+ per year) can trigger review

**Chargeback Rate Threshold:** Worten wants <1% chargeback rate
- If >2%: Account review
- If >3%: Suspension risk

**Prevention:**
- Clear product descriptions (reduce "Not as described" disputes)
- Fast delivery (reduce "Not received" disputes)
- Professional communication (reduce "Customer frustration" disputes)

- Use tracking codes (proof of delivery)

- Spain VAT rate: 21% (not 23%)
- File quarterly OSS return listing both countries' sales
- Example Q1 OSS Return:
  - Portugal sales: EUR 10,000 @ 23% = EUR 2,300 VAT
  - Spain sales: EUR 5,000 @ 21% = EUR 1,050 VAT
  - Total VAT owed: EUR 3,350 (paid to Italian authority)

**Step 3: Product Translations**
- Translate all product listings to Spanish
- Update descriptions, specs, return policy
- Hiring cost: EUR 200-500 (one-time); or use automated translation + manual review

**Step 4: Consumer Protection (Spain)**
- Obtain Spanish consumer law compliance template
- Key difference: LSCA (Ley de Servicios de la Comunidad) has similar 14-day return but slight procedural differences
- Return shipping still paid by seller (same as Portugal)

**Step 5: WEEE/EPR (Spain)**
- If selling electronics: Register for Spanish WEEE scheme
- Similar to Portugal: Hire Authorized Representative (EUR 200-500/year)
- File with Spanish WEEE operator (SIGRE for medicines, Ecobat for batteries, etc.)

**Step 6: Shipping Configuration**
- Set up shipping partner for Italy→Spain route
- **Advantage:** Shorter distance than Portugal; faster delivery (2-4 days)
- **Carriers:** DHL, GLS, SEUR available
- **Shipping cost:** Similar to Portugal (EUR 2-8 per order)
- **SLA:** Still 5 business days (easily achievable)

### 8.3 Logistics Italy→Spain

**Transit Times:**
- **Distance:** Northern Italy to Spain: 1,200-1,500 km
- **Standard service:** 2-3 business days
- **Express:** 1 day (DHL)
- **Recommendation:** Faster than Portugal route; easier to meet 5-day SLA

**Carriers (Italy→Spain):**
1. **SEUR** (Iberian specialist): Best for Spain; 2-3 days; EUR 3-6/parcel

**Customs:** None (intra-EU; simplified like Portugal)

### 9.4 Inventory Management

**Key Decisions:**
1. **Held inventory vs. dropshipping**
2. **Supplier reliability** (lead time, quality)
3. **Cash flow impact**

**Held Inventory Approach (Recommended for Marketplace):**
- Buy stock from suppliers; store locally (or use warehouse)
- When order received: Ship from inventory
- Advantage: Fast fulfillment; meets 5-day SLA easily
- Disadvantage: Capital tied up; storage cost; risk of obsolescence

**Recommended Inventory Level (for EUR 500/month seller):**
- Months of supply: 2-3 months
- Inventory value: EUR 3,000-5,000
- Reason: Buffer for peak season; cover 1-2 weeks of sales delays

**Dropshipping Approach (Not Recommended for Worten):**
- Customer orders → Seller orders from supplier → Supplier ships directly to customer
- Advantage: No inventory cost
- Disadvantage: Supplier delays = seller misses SLA
- **Problem for Worten:** 5-day SLA difficult with dropshipping; suppliers often take 3-5 days to fulfill

**Best Practice:**
1. Stock fast-moving items locally (electronics, popular models)
2. Dropship slow-moving items (rare models, specialized products)
3. Monitor inventory weekly; reorder when 30% stock remaining
4. Use inventory management software (TradeGecko, Stitch, Cin7; EUR 50-200/month)

### 9.5 Pricing Strategy

**Competitive Analysis Process:**
1. **Identify direct competitors** (other sellers on Worten selling same product)
2. **Monitor competitor pricing** (check daily or weekly)
3. **Calculate your margin** (cost + Worten costs + desired profit)
4. **Price strategically:**
   - If want Buy Box: Price within 3-5% of lowest competitor
   - If want margin: Price higher; lose some volume; focus on service
   - Premium positioning: Price 10-15% higher; emphasize faster delivery, better service

**Example Pricing (ASUS Gaming Laptop):**

```
Competitor 1 (Worten): EUR 899
Competitor 2 (Amazon): EUR 875
Competitor 3 (Local shop): EUR 920

Vendiamonoi Cost: EUR 550 (from supplier)
Worten Costs (assume 10% commission + EUR 30 monthly + shipping + packaging): ~EUR 140 per unit
Desired Profit Margin: 20% = EUR 138

Required Price: EUR 550 + EUR 140 + EUR 138 = EUR 828 minimum

Worten Pricing Strategies:

Option A (Compete on Price): EUR 869
- Lower than Worten.pt competitor (EUR 899)
- Margin: EUR 869 - EUR 550 - EUR 140 = EUR 179 (20.6%)
- Expected: Likely win Buy Box; 70% of sales
- Monthly (100 units): EUR 17,900 revenue

Option B (Premium/Service Positioning): EUR 899
- Match competitor; emphasize faster delivery (1-2 days via DHL)
- Margin: EUR 899 - EUR 550 - EUR 140 = EUR 209 (23.2%)
- Expected: Lose Buy Box but attract quality-seekers; 30% of sales
- Monthly (100 units): EUR 20,900 revenue (higher margin despite lower volume)

Option C (Cost Leadership): EUR 849
- Beat all competitors by EUR 26-50
- Margin: EUR 849 - EUR 550 - EUR 140 = EUR 159 (18.7%)
- Expected: Win Buy Box decisively; 80%+ of sales
- Monthly (100 units): EUR 15,800 revenue
```

**Pricing Best Practice:**
- Start with Option B (premium positioning; better margins)
- After 3 months: Adjust based on sales velocity and margin targets
- Monitor competitor prices weekly; adjust monthly (not daily)
- Use automated price monitoring tools (CamelCamelCamel, Keepa, etc.) for data

### 9.6 Quality Assurance and Product Inspection

**Receiving Inspection (When Goods Arrive from Supplier):**
1. **Spot-check** first 5-10% of units (e.g., 5 of 50 laptops)
2. **Test functionality** (power on, check features)
3. **Verify packaging** (all accessories included; packaging intact)
4. **Document any defects** (photos; video)
5. **If defects found:** Contact supplier; request replacement or credit

**Pre-Shipping Inspection (Before Customer Ships):**
1. **Random sampling:** Check 2-5% of orders before shipping
2. **Verify:** Product matches listing; packaging undamaged; all accessories
3. **Test:** Power on; check features
4. **If problem found:** Don't ship; contact customer; offer alternative or refund

**Defect Handling:**
- Defect rate target: <1% (industry standard: 0.5-2%)
- If rate >2%: Negotiate with supplier (discounts, returns)
- If rate >5%: Find new supplier

**Cost of Quality Control:**
- Labor: 5 minutes per unit × EUR 10/hr = EUR 0.83 per unit (if checked)
- Recommendation: Check 2-3% of units = EUR 0.02-0.03 per unit cost
- ROI: Prevents returns (EUR 5-10 loss per return); pays for itself
