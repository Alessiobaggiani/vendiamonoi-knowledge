# EU PRODUCT COMPLIANCE REGULATIONS

## 1. CE Marking

### What It Means

CE marking (Conformité Européenne) is a declaration by a manufacturer that a product meets the applicable EU requirements. It is not a certification mark but rather a symbol that confirms the product has been assessed against EU legislation and meets the essential health and safety requirements. The manufacturer is responsible for affixing the CE mark at their sole responsibility.

**Legal Basis:** CE marking is mandated by various EU directives and regulations specific to product categories. It is not a general requirement for all products—only those covered by specific EU legislation.

### Product Categories Requiring CE Marking

The following product categories require CE marking before placement on the EU market:

| Product Category | Applicable Legislation | Key Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| **Toys** | Toy Safety Directive 2009/48/EC | EN 71 standards compliance; age warnings; testing |
| **Electrical & Electronic Equipment** | RoHS Directive 2011/65/EU; RED 2014/53/EU | Restricted substances compliance; radio equipment testing |
| **Batteries** | Battery Regulation (EU) 2023/1542 | Effective Feb 2024; labeling; performance data |
| **Machinery** | Machinery Regulation 2023/1230 | Technical documentation; risk assessment |
| **Medical Devices** | MDR 2017/745 | Classification-dependent; clinical evaluation |
| **Recreational Crafts/Watercraft** | RCD 2013/62/EU | Design compliance; stability testing |
| **Pressure Equipment** | PED 2014/68/EU | Certification for pressurized products |
| **Gas Appliances** | GAR 2016/426 | Safety certification; installation requirements |
| **Drones** | Implementing Regulations (EU) 2019/947 | Airworthiness; pilot certification |
| **Harmonized Radio Equipment** | RED 2014/53/EU | EMC testing; frequency compliance |
| **Lifts** | Lifts Regulation (EU) 2014/33 | Installation; annual testing |

### How to Verify CE Marking Compliance

1. **Identify applicable legislation:** Check which EU directives/regulations apply to your specific product
2. **Technical documentation:** Maintain complete technical documentation showing how the product meets essential requirements
3. **Conformity assessment:** Conduct appropriate conformity assessment procedure (self-declaration, third-party testing, etc.)
4. **Declaration of Conformity (DoC):** Prepare detailed DoC in English
5. **Affixing the mark:** CE mark must be affixed visibly to the product or packaging
6. **Record retention:** Keep all documentation for 10 years minimum

**Testing Standards:** Products typically require testing against harmonized European standards (EN standards). Third-party notified bodies (indicated by four-digit identification number) must perform certain assessments depending on product risk classification.

## 2. REACH Regulation

### Overview

Regulation (EC) No 1907/2006 (REACH - Registration, Evaluation, Authorization and Restriction of Chemicals) is the EU's chemical safety regulation. For distributors, the key obligation is ensuring products don't contain restricted substances and communicating the presence of Substances of Very High Concern (SVHCs).

**Key Reference:** [ECHA - Understanding REACH](https://echa.europa.eu/regulations/reach/understanding-reach)

### Substances of Very High Concern (SVHC)

**SVHC Candidate List (as of February 2026):** The list contains **253 substances** and grows regularly. Common SVHCs include:

- Lead and lead compounds
- Cadmium and cadmium compounds
- Mercury compounds
- Hexavalent chromium
- Polybrominated biphenyls (PBB) and diphenyl ethers (PBDE)
- Phthalates (DEHP, BBP, DBP, DIBP)
- Formaldehyde
- Carcinogenic, mutagenic, toxic (CMR) substances

**Current Additions (2025-2026):**
- n-hexane
- 4,4'-[2,2,2-trifluoro-1-(trifluoromethyl)ethylidene]diphenol and its salts

### Seller Obligations for SVHC Articles

**1. Communication Requirements (0.1% w/w threshold)**

If your article contains an SVHC above 0.1% by weight:

- **Notify customers:** Provide information allowing safe use of the article
- **Respond to consumer inquiries:** Respond within **45 days** to any consumer asking about SVHC presence
- **Supply chain notification:** Inform your supply chain partners of SVHC presence
- **No extra cost:** Information must be provided free of charge

### REACH Registration & Notification

**For Importers:** If you import articles containing substances above the 0.1% w/w threshold, you must:

1. **Verify supplier documentation:** Ensure your supplier provides information on substance content
2. **SCIP database registration:** Register relevant articles on the SCIP (Substances of Concern In articles as such or in Products) database operated by ECHA if SVHCs are present above the threshold
3. **Notification timeline:** SCIP submissions became mandatory on January 7, 2022 for articles containing SVHCs
4. **No cost for registration:** ECHA provides free access to the SCIP database

**Key Point:** The importer (you, as a distributor importing into the EU) is responsible for ensuring SVHC communication and SCIP notification, not the marketplace.

### Restricted Substances

Beyond SVHCs, REACH specifies **absolute restrictions** on certain chemicals in consumer products:

| Substance | Restriction | Affected Products |
|---|---|---|
| **Asbestos (all forms)** | Banned entirely | Building materials, insulation, textiles |
| **Lead in consumer articles** | Max 0.05% for most uses | Toys, jewelry, ceramics with glaze |
| **Cadmium** | Max 0.01% in plastics | Stabilizers in PVC, some colorants |
| **Chromium VI** | Restricted concentrations | Leather, textiles, coatings |
| **Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs)** | Max 0.2 mg/kg | Oils, rubbers, coatings |
| **Formaldehyde in textiles** | Max 30 ppm for children's clothing | Apparel, bedding (children's items stricter) |
| **Phthalates (DEHP, BBP, DBP, DIBP)** | Banned in toys and childcare articles | Toys, children's products |

### Marketplace Implications

**Amazon, eBay, Kaufland, Bol.com** all enforce REACH compliance as a condition of sale. Violations result in:
- Product removal
- Seller suspension
- Account closure
- Legal liability for health damages

**Best Practice:** Obtain supplier declarations confirming SVHC and restricted substance absence, and maintain these documents for at least 10 years.

## 3. RoHS Directive (2011/65/EU)

### Overview

The Restriction of Hazardous Substances (RoHS) Directive restricts 10 hazardous substances in electrical and electronic equipment (EEE). The directive applies to manufacturers and importers of EEE placed on the EU market.

**Effective Date:** July 22, 2019 (amended version)

### Ten Restricted Substances

| Substance | Maximum Concentration | Affected Categories |
|---|---|---|
| **Lead (Pb)** | 0.1% by weight | All EEE; exceptions for specific components |
| **Mercury (Hg)** | 0.1% by weight | All EEE; strict limits in lamps |
| **Cadmium (Cd)** | 0.01% by weight | All EEE |
| **Chromium VI (Cr VI)** | 0.1% by weight | Steel coatings, leather components |
| **Polybrominated Biphenyls (PBB)** | 0.1% by weight | Flame retardants in plastics |
| **Polybrominated Diphenyl Ethers (PBDE)** | 0.1% by weight | Flame retardants in textiles, foams |
| **Bis(2-ethylhexyl) phthalate (DEHP)** | 0.1% by weight | Plastics, cables, seals |
| **Butyl benzyl phthalate (BBP)** | 0.1% by weight | Plastics, coatings |
| **Dibutyl phthalate (DBP)** | 0.1% by weight | Plastics, inks |
| **Diisobutyl phthalate (DIBP)** | 0.1% by weight | Plastics, rubber components |

### Exemptions & Derogations

RoHS applies to most EEE but has exemptions:

- **Large-scale industrial tools** (>30 ton installation weight)
- **Industrial monitoring equipment** performing under hazardous conditions
- **Medical devices** (covered by MDR 2017/745 instead)
- **Spare parts for EEE placed on market before RoHS implementation**
- **Specific component exemptions** (e.g., lead-free solder compatibility in older equipment)

### Importer/Distributor Obligations

1. **Product verification:** Ensure suppliers provide RoHS compliance documentation
2. **Technical documentation:** Maintain records of conformity assessment
3. **Declaration of Conformity (DoC):** Prepare for each product category
4. **CE marking:** Affix to products or packaging
5. **Record retention:** Keep compliance documentation for 10 years
6. **Traceability:** Maintain supplier identification and testing records

### Testing Requirements

- **Homogeneous materials:** Test each material separately
- **Sampling strategy:** Test representative samples from production batches
- **Accredited labs:** Use ISO/IEC 17025-accredited testing facilities
- **Standards:** Test to EN 62321-7 series (RoHS testing standards)

### Marketplace Enforcement

**Amazon Project Zero Transparency Program**, **eBay**, and **Kaufland** require RoHS certification for electronics. Non-compliance results in:
- Suspension of electronic product listings
- Account restriction
- Potential legal action

## 4. WEEE Directive (2012/19/EU)

### Overview

The Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment (WEEE) Directive requires manufacturers and importers to manage the end-of-life treatment of EEE through established collection, recycling, and recovery systems. For distributors, this means registering as a producer per country and contributing to collection and recycling targets.

**Key Reference:** [WEEE Compliance Hub](https://www.weeeforum.org/)

### Producer Definition & Registration

**Producer Definition:** Any natural or legal person that places EEE on the EU market for the first time is considered a producer, regardless of whether they manufactured it. **This includes importers and online marketplace sellers.**

### Country-Specific Registration & Costs

| Country | WEEE Registry | Registration Fee | Annual Contribution Model | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Germany** | Stiftung Elektro-Altgeräte-Register (EAR) | €40-150 annually (per category) | Dual system; producer must join compliance scheme | Mandatory for all EEE sellers; strict enforcement |
| **France** | ADEME-registered bodies | €150-400 annually (per category) | Dual system; individual or collective scheme | Triman logo required on products |
| **Italy** | ERICA | €200-600 annually | Collective scheme; producer contribution to WEEE management | Electronic reporting mandatory |
| **Spain** | SCRAP/Ecolec | €100-250 annually | Collective scheme | Producer registration in national WEEE registry |
| **Belgium** | Recupel (Flanders); Sud Recycling (Wallonia) | €100-400 per system | Dual system depending on region | Flemish region stricter enforcement |
| **Netherlands** | Stibat (batteries) + NVMP (EEE) | €150-500 | Producer participation in collective scheme | Strict data reporting requirements |
| **Sweden** | El-Kretsen (collective) | €100-300 | Collective scheme | Low fees, high compliance expectations |
| **Portugal** | Gerp (collectively managed) | €100-200 | Collective scheme | Growing enforcement; cross-border registrations |
| **Poland** | PZWEE + Katalizator | €200-500 | Dual system | Emerging compliance infrastructure |
| **Greece** | HELLASCO | €100-250 | Collective scheme | Registration mandatory upon market entry |

### Collection & Recycling Targets

**Collection Targets (Effective 2024-2026):**

- **Minimum 65% by weight** of all EEE placed on market in previous 3 years (historic baseline method) OR
- **45 kg per capita** from electronic waste collected (extended producer responsibility method)

**By 2030:** Target increases to 80% by weight or 65 kg per capita

**Recycling Efficiency Requirements:**
- **Metal recovery:** Minimum 85%
- **Plastic recovery:** Minimum 55%
- **Glass recovery:** Minimum 70%

### Producer Obligations

1. **Registration in each country** where you place EEE on market
2. **Contribution fees** to national WEEE schemes (annual, per product category)
3. **Data reporting:** Report quantities of EEE placed on market annually
4. **Collection point information:** Provide end-users with collection point details
5. **Record retention:** Maintain registration and reporting documentation for 4 years minimum

### WEEE Categories & Classification

| Category | Examples | Registration Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **Large appliances** | Refrigerators, washing machines, dishwashers | Category 1 registration |
| **Small appliances** | Kettles, toasters, blenders, vacuum cleaners | Category 2 registration |
| **IT & Telecom equipment** | Computers, laptops, mobile phones, routers | Category 3 registration |
| **Consumer electronics** | TVs, audio systems, cameras, game consoles | Category 4 registration |
| **Lighting equipment** | LED bulbs, fluorescent tubes, halogen lamps | Category 5 registration |
| **Tools** | Electric drills, saws (non-industrial only) | Category 6 registration |
| **Toys & leisure** | Electronic toys, drones, RC cars (>100W) | Category 7 registration |
| **Medical devices** | Portable diagnostic equipment, nebulizers | Category 8 registration |
| **Monitoring & control instruments** | Industrial sensors, smart meters | Category 9 registration |
| **Automatic dispensers** | Vending machines, ATMs | Category 10 registration |

### Key Enforcement Points

- **Amazon Germany:** Requires proof of WEEE registration (EAR Stiftung certificate) for all EEE listings
- **eBay:** Enforces country-specific WEEE compliance on electronics
- **Kaufland:** Requests WEEE compliance documentation for electronics sales
- **Fines:** Violations result in product removal, seller suspension, and potential €10,000+ fines per country

## 5. Textile Regulation (EU 1007/2011)

### Overview

The Textile Regulation mandates that all textiles sold in the EU must be labeled with the official fiber composition in accordance with EU fiber nomenclature. Sellers cannot use unapproved fiber names.

**Key Reference:** [OEKO-TEX® Certification](https://www.oeko-tex.com/en/Brands/Our_Standards/100-standard)

### Official Fiber Names

The regulation specifies **45 officially recognized fiber names**. Only these names can be used in product descriptions and labels. Key approved fibers:

**Natural Fibers:**
- Cotton
- Wool
- Silk
- Linen
- Hemp
- Jute
- Ramie
- Abaca
- Flax

**Synthetic Fibers:**
- Polyester (PES)
- Nylon (PA)
- Polypropylene (PP)
- Acrylic (PAN)
- Polyurethane (PU/Elastane)
- Polyethylene (PE)

**Regenerated Fibers:**
- Viscose
- Lyocell
- Modal
- Cupro

**Prohibited/Non-Compliant Names:**
- "Cashmere-like" (not approved; must be "Acrylic" or specific fiber)
- "Wool-touch" (not approved; must state actual content)
- "Silk-feel" (not approved; must state actual fiber)
- "Eco-friendly material" (vague; must list actual fibers)
- "Synthetic blend" (too vague; must list specific fibers)

### Content & Labeling Requirements

**1. Minimum Textile Content Threshold**

Products must contain **at least 80% by weight of textile fibers** to be classified as textiles. Products below this threshold are regulated differently (as clothing/apparel products).

**2. Fiber Composition Declaration**

- **Percentages required:** Each fiber component must be listed by percentage (by weight) in descending order
- **Tolerance:** ±2% tolerance permitted for components under 10%; ±1% for components ≥10%
- **Rounding rules:** Components <1% can be listed as "other fibers" collectively
- **English mandatory:** Labeling must include English descriptions

**3. Denier Requirements (for certain products)**

Specific textile categories (pantyhose, stockings, socks) must declare denier per square centimeter (den/cm²) on the label.

### Marketplace Listing Compliance

**Amazon:** Enforces textile fiber nomenclature compliance; non-standard fiber names result in listing removal.

**eBay:** Requires accurate fiber composition; "luxury" fiber claims (cashmere, silk, wool) are subject to enhanced scrutiny.

**Kaufland:** Textile Product Information Database (PINDB) submission required for all textile listings in specific categories.

### Testing & Certification

- **ISO 1833 series:** Standard methods for fiber identification and quantification
- **Accredited labs:** Testing should be performed by ISO/IEC 17025-accredited textile testing laboratories
- **Retention:** Test reports should be retained for 10 years minimum

## 6. Food Information Regulation (Regulation 1169/2011 - FIR)

### Overview

The Food Information Regulation (FIR) mandates comprehensive labeling and information provision for all food products sold in the EU. For marketplace distributors, this includes mandatory nutrition labeling, allergen disclosure, and ingredient transparency.

**Scope:** All pre-packaged food products placed on EU market

### Mandatory Nutrition Label Information

**Front-of-Pack (FOP) Information Required:**

- **Energy value:** In kilocalories (kcal) AND kilojoules (kJ)
- **Fat:** In grams
- **Saturated fat:** In grams
- **Carbohydrates:** In grams
- **Sugars:** In grams
- **Protein:** In grams
- **Salt:** In grams

**Format Requirements:**
- **Per 100g/100ml** (reference servings)
- **Optional:** Per serving (if declared on packaging)
- **Readable font:** Minimum 1.2 mm height
- **Contrasting colors:** Black text on white or white text on black background

### Allergen Disclosure (14 Major Allergens)

**Mandatory allergen identification:**

The following allergens must be clearly identified in the ingredient list using **bold, capital letters, or highlighted color**:

1. **Cereals containing gluten** (wheat, barley, oats, rye, etc.)
2. **Crustaceans** (shrimp, crab, lobster, etc.)
3. **Mollusks** (mussels, oysters, squid, snails, etc.)
4. **Fish**
5. **Peanuts**
6. **Tree nuts** (almonds, hazelnuts, walnuts, cashews, pistachios, Brazil nuts, pecans, etc.)
7. **Sesame**
8. **Soy**
9. **Milk** (including lactose)
10. **Eggs**
11. **Sulfites** (preservative; concentrations ≥10 mg/kg or L must be declared)
12. **Celery**
13. **Mustard**
14. **Lupin**

### Ingredient Declaration

**Complete ingredient list required:**
- List all ingredients in **descending order by weight** at time of manufacture
- **E-number format:** Permitted food additives can be listed as E-numbers (E100, E200, etc.) or common names
- **Sub-ingredients:** Compound ingredients ≥25% must declare sub-ingredients if they contain allergens

### Origin Labeling Rules

**Country of Origin Mandatory When:**
- **Meat products:** Country of origin labeling required (regulation 1337/2013 for meat products)
- **Honey:** Must declare country/region of origin
- **Olive oil:** Origin must be declared
- **Fish:** Origin of catch and processing location must be declared

**Optional for other products** but when declared, must comply with EU origin rules.

### Marketplace Enforcement

**Amazon:** Requires Food Information Label Compliance Checklist; non-compliant listings are removed.

**eBay:** Food category has enhanced verification; allergen information is mandatory.

**Kaufland:** Food compliance is mandatory; violations result in immediate delisting.

## 7. Cosmetics Regulation (Regulation 1223/2009)

### Overview

The Cosmetics Regulation applies to all cosmetic products (personal care items) placed on the EU market. Key requirements include responsible person designation, product notification, and safety substantiation.

**Definition:** Cosmetics are substances or mixtures intended for external application to the skin, hair, teeth, nails, lips, or external genitals to clean, perfume, protect, or change appearance.

### Responsible Person (RP) Requirement

**Key Principle:** All cosmetic products must have a **Responsible Person (RP)** established in the EU.

**RP Definition:** A natural or legal person established in the EU who is responsible for ensuring compliance with the regulation.

**RP Duties:**
- Ensure product safety assessment before market placement
- Maintain detailed product information file (PIF)
- Verify notification with CPNP (Cosmetic Products Notification Portal)
- Ensure compliance with banned/restricted substances
- Respond to safety inquiries
- Report adverse events to CPNP
- Maintain documentation for 10 years after product discontinuation

**If you are outside the EU:** You must appoint an EU-based Responsible Person. Many distributors use **contract manufacturing organizations (CMOs)** or **regulatory consultants** as RPs.

### CPNP Notification

**Cosmetic Products Notification Portal (CPNP):**
- **Mandatory registration** for all cosmetics before placement on EU market
- **Notification data:** Product name, responsible person, intended use, type of product
- **Annual update:** Product information must be updated annually
- **No cost:** CPNP registration is free
- **Timeline:** Notification should occur before product launch but can be updated within a reasonable timeframe

### Banned Substances in Cosmetics

The Cosmetics Regulation provides a **strict prohibition list** (Annex II) containing **1,800+ banned substances** in cosmetics. Key categories:

| Substance Category | Examples | Exception |
|---|---|---|
| **Heavy metals** | Lead, mercury, cadmium, arsenic | None (absolute ban) |
| **Carcinogenic substances (Category 1A/1B)** | Benzene, formaldehyde | Limited exceptions for certain concentrations |
| **Asbestos & asbestos derivatives** | All forms | Complete ban |
| **Quartz (crystalline)** | If concentration >1% | Limited exceptions |
| **Pesticides** | Most synthetic pesticides | None in cosmetics |
| **Chloroform** | — | Complete ban |

### Restricted Substances in Cosmetics

Beyond banned substances, certain ingredients are **restricted** to specific concentrations:

| Substance | Maximum Concentration | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Aluminum compounds** | Varies by product type | Max 15% in anti-perspirants |
| **Formaldehyde (in rinse-off products)** | 0.2% | Higher limits for non-rinse-off |
| **Hydroxyacetic acid (AHA)** | Max 10% | pH >3.5 in products |
| **Lead** | Trace amounts <10 ppm | Use as impurity in colorants |
| **Lithium compounds** | Specific limits | Often used in sunscreens |
| **Parabens** | Varies by paraben type | Propylparaben & butylparaben now restricted |
| **Phenoxyethanol** | Max 1% | Common preservative |
| **Salicylic acid** | 0.5-2% depending on product | Varies by rinse-off vs. leave-on |
| **Zinc pyrithione** | Max 2% | Dandruff shampoos |

### Product Information File (PIF) Retention

**10-year retention requirement:**
- Physical or digital file containing:
  - Safety assessment report
  - Product formulation & manufacturing details
  - Stability testing data
  - Raw material certificates
  - Supplier documentation
  - Adverse event reports (if applicable)

### Adverse Event Reporting

**Serious adverse events** must be reported to CPNP within **10 days** of awareness. Examples:
- Severe allergic reactions
- Chemical burns
- Eye damage
- Systemic toxicity

## 8. Toy Safety Directive (2009/48/EC)

### Overview

The Toy Safety Directive applies to all toys placed on the EU market, including online sales through marketplaces. Toys are defined as articles designed or intended for children under 14 years. The directive requires CE marking and compliance with strict safety standards.

**Key Testing Standards:**
- **EN 71 series** (mechanical and physical properties, flammability, chemical safety)
- **EN 62115** (electronic toys safety)

### Product Categories & Age Warnings

**Toys subject to enhanced requirements:**

| Category | Age Group | Key Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| **Toys for children <8 years** | 0-3, 3-8 | Small parts ban (<31.75 mm); soft goods safety; mechanical safety; non-toxic materials |
| **Soft-filled toys** | All ages | Flammability testing; stitching strength |
| **Balloons** | All ages | Choking hazard warning required |
| **Plastic bags/films** | <14 years | Warning label if thickness <0.038 mm |
| **Electronic toys** | All ages | EN 62115 compliance; electrical safety; EMC testing |
| **Projectile toys** | All ages | Impact testing; velocity limits |
| **Toys with magnets** | All ages | Ingestion hazard assessment; magnet strength limits |

### Restricted Chemicals in Toys

**Heavy metal limits (ppm by migration):**

| Element | Limit (ppm) | Typical Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Lead** | 90 | Paint, dyes, certain coatings |
| **Cadmium** | 75 | Yellow/red dyes, metal coatings |
| **Chromium (VI)** | 60 | Metal coatings, leather |
| **Mercury** | 60 | Electronics, certain paints |
| **Arsenic** | 25 | — |
| **Copper** | 3,813 | Metal components, jewelry |

**Phthalate ban:** DEHP, BBP, DBP, DIBP banned entirely in toys and childcare articles.

### CE Marking & Declaration of Conformity

**Requirements:**
1. **Technical documentation:** Maintain complete testing reports
2. **Declaration of Conformity (DoC):** Detailed document stating compliance with EN 71 standards
3. **CE mark affixing:** Mark must be visible on product and packaging
4. **Notification body:** For certain toy categories, third-party notified body testing is required
5. **Record retention:** Documentation retained for 10 years

### Marketplace Compliance

**Amazon Toys:** Requires CE documentation and Declaration of Conformity.

**eBay Toys Category:** Requires proof of toy safety certification.

**Kaufland:** Toy listings subject to documentation verification.

## 9. General Product Safety Regulation 2024 (GPSR 2024/2023/988)

### Overview

GPSR (Regulation 2023/988) replaces the General Product Safety Directive (2001/95/EC) and became effective **December 13, 2024**. It introduces new requirements for online marketplace sellers, including mandatory "responsible person" designation for EU-based point of contact.

**Key Impact:** GPSR significantly increases obligations for marketplace sellers operating in the EU.

### Core Obligations Under GPSR

**1. Product Safety Responsibility**

Every product placed on EU market must be safe for consumers under normal/foreseeable conditions. Sellers must:
- Conduct risk assessment for each product category
- Ensure products meet applicable EU legislation
- Test products for hazards
- Maintain product safety documentation

**2. Responsible Person (RP) Requirement (NEW)**

**Mandatory for non-EU manufacturers/importers:**
- Must appoint an **EU-based Responsible Person** (natural or legal person)
- RP acts as point of contact for enforcement authorities
- RP must be clearly identified on product/packaging with name, address, telephone, email
- RP is legally liable for product safety compliance

**Alternative:** Appoint a **European importer** as the responsible party.

**Cost Impact:** Most non-EU sellers need to hire a regulatory consultant or use a service provider as RP (€2,000-10,000 annually).

### Marketplace Seller Obligations

**All marketplace sellers must:**

1. **Product Risk Assessment:** Conduct documented safety assessment for each product type
2. **Technical Documentation:** Maintain compliance files showing how products meet applicable EU laws
3. **Responsible Person Appointment (if non-EU):** Provide EU contact for legal/enforcement matters
4. **Consumer Information:** Provide safety instructions and warnings in consumer language (English minimum)
5. **Product Traceability:** Maintain records of product origins and distribution chains
6. **Rapid Alert System (RAPEX) Monitoring:** Monitor and comply with product recall notices
7. **Non-Compliance Reporting:** Report non-compliant products to competent authorities
8. **Consumer Complaint Response:** Respond to consumer safety complaints within 10 business days

### GPSR Prohibited Practices

**Sellers cannot:**
- Deliberate safety violations (criminal offense)
- Misleading safety information
- Circumvent safety standards through cross-border sales
- Conceal product defects
- Resell recalled products
- Misrepresent country of origin to avoid compliance

### GPSR Enforcement & Penalties

| Violation Type | Potential Penalty |
|---|---|
| **No Responsible Person (non-EU sellers)** | Product removal + €5,000-20,000 fine per country + possible account suspension |
| **Missing safety documentation** | Fine up to €10,000-50,000 per product category |
| **False safety claims** | Fine up to €50,000-500,000 + product removal + criminal liability |
| **Selling recalled products** | Fine up to €100,000-500,000 + account closure + criminal prosecution |

### Marketplace Platform Obligations Under GPSR

**Amazon, eBay, Kaufland, Bol.com must:**
- Provide seller information to authorities upon request
- Remove non-compliant products immediately upon notification
- Suspend sellers repeatedly selling unsafe products
- Implement compliance verification systems

**Practical Impact:** Non-EU sellers without documented responsible persons face automatic suspension.

# MARKETPLACE-SPECIFIC COMPLIANCE

## 1. Amazon Compliance Framework

### Amazon Project Zero & Transparency Program

**Project Zero (Anti-Counterfeiting):**
- Provides brands with tools to remove counterfeit listings
- Requires product serialization for certain categories
- Sellers must verify authenticity of high-risk products
- Violations result in automatic suspension (2-strike policy)

**Transparency Program:**
- Brand owners can require product codes (serial numbers) on items
- Products must be scanned in via barcode at storage facility
- Non-compliance results in listing removal
- Applicable to electronics, beauty, watches, jewelry

### Amazon CE & Safety Requirements

**Documentation Required:**
- Declaration of Conformity (DoC) for CE-marked products
- WEEE registration proof (Germany: EAR Stiftung certificate)
- Toy Safety certification (EN 71) for toy category
- REACH/RoHS certificates for electronics
- RoHS documentation for appliances

**File Upload:** All documents must be uploaded to seller central under "Compliance Documents" section.

**Verification Timeline:** 5-10 business days; non-compliance results in listing suspension.

### Amazon Hazardous Material Classification

Products classified as hazardous require:
- Hazmat classification codes
- Safety Data Sheet (SDS) documentation
- Proper labeling with hazard symbols
- Regional shipping restrictions (some hazmat cannot ship to certain regions)

**Common hazmat categories:**
- Batteries (flammable/corrosive)
- Aerosols
- Chemicals (cleaners, solvents)

# PRODUCT CLAIMS & ADVERTISING RULES

## 1. Unfair Commercial Practices Directive (2005/29/EC - UCPD)

### Overview

The UCPD prohibits unfair commercial practices in consumer transactions. "Unfair" practices include misleading actions, misleading omissions, and aggressive practices.

### Misleading Actions

**Examples of prohibited claims:**
- **False origin:** Labeling a Chinese product "Made in Germany" or "EU-made"
- **Exaggerated certifications:** Claiming "FDA-approved" when product only has EU CE marking
- **Fake reviews:** Posting positive reviews written by the seller or paid reviewers
- **Ambiguous composition:** Claiming "natural" when product is 100% synthetic
- **Misleading prices:** Original price inflated to show larger discounts
- **False performance claims:** Claiming product "cures disease" without medical substantiation

### Misleading Omissions

**Critical information that MUST be disclosed:**
- **Material facts:** Significant limitations or warnings (e.g., "Warning: Choking hazard")
- **Price terms:** Final price including all taxes and shipping (if not already clear)
- **Warranty information:** Limitation of warranty periods must be transparent
- **Product defects:** Known defects or recalls must be disclosed

---

## 2. Health Claims Regulation (Regulation 1924/2006)

### Overview

The Health Claims Regulation restricts nutrition and health claims for food and dietary supplements. **Only approved claims can be used in product listings.**

### Approved Claims

**General Function Claims (pre-approved):**
- "Supports immune function" (not "boosts immunity")
- "Contributes to bone health" (with specified calcium content)
- "Supports normal digestion" (not "cures digestive disorders")
- "Maintains healthy cholesterol levels" (with qualifying statements)

**Prohibited Claims:**
- "Cures" any disease
- "Prevents" disease
- "Treats" medical conditions
- "Detoxifies" the body
- "Boosts metabolism" (weight loss claims)
- "Increases energy" (implies medical benefit)

### Substantiation Requirements

**For any health claim, documentation must include:**
- Scientific studies (published, peer-reviewed)
- Clinical trials (for supplement claims)
- Expert panel approval (EFSA for EU health claims)
- Links to official EFSA database (https://ec.europa.eu/food/safety/labelling-and-nutrition/health-claims_en)

**Marketplace enforcement:** Amazon, eBay, and Kaufland actively remove listings with unsubstantiated health claims.

---

## 3. Environmental Claims Rules (Green Claims Directive 2024/2867)

### Overview

The Green Claims Directive (effective from 2026, enforcement from 2027) regulates environmental claims on products and requires substantiation with scientific evidence.

**Scope:** Any claim suggesting environmental benefit (eco-friendly, carbon-neutral, sustainable, etc.)

### Prohibited Environmental Claims

**Absolute bans (regardless of substantiation):**
- **"Eco" or "eco-friendly" generically** without specific environmental benefit claim
- **Carbon-neutral claims** without verified carbon offset program
- **Plastic-free claims** if packaging contains any plastic
- **Biodegradable claims** without LCA (Life Cycle Assessment) evidence
- **Compostable claims** without certification to EN 14995 standard

### Required Substantiation for Environmental Claims

**If claiming "sustainable," "responsible," or similar:**
- Third-party certification (ISO 14024 Type I) OR
- Scientific LCA (Life Cycle Assessment) OR
- EFTA (Environmental Footprint) compliance OR
- Carbon Trust/similar verified program

**Burden of proof:** Seller must provide documentation within 10 days of marketplace inquiry.

### Trademarks & Marketing Claims

| Claim Type | Requirement | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **"Organic"** | EU organic certification (IFOAM) | "Organic cotton" — must show GOTS/IFOAM cert |
| **"Fair trade"** | Fairtrade certification or equivalent | "Fair trade coffee" — must show Fair Trade mark |
| **"Rainforest-friendly"** | Rainforest Alliance/FSC certification | "Rainforest-friendly" — must show label |
| **"Carbon-neutral"** | Verified offset program + third-party audit | Need carbon offset documentation |
| **"Plastic-free packaging"** | 0% plastic in direct packaging | Cannot have plastic film, boxes, or labels |

---

## 4. Origin Claims & Made-In Labels

### "Made In" Rules (Origin Regulation 2015/2424)

**Rules for origin claims:**

| Claim | Requirements |
|---|---|
| **"Made in [Country]"** | At least 45% of production cost incurred in that country OR last substantial transformation in that country |
| **"EU-Made"** | 50%+ of production cost in EU countries OR all manufacturing stages in EU |
| **"Handmade"** | Substantially produced by hand; industrial production <10% |
| **"Locally made"** | Specific region claims require ≥70% of value added in that region |

### Prohibited Origin Claims

- **No misleading regional claims:** Cannot claim "Made in Italy" if only assembled there
- **No fake country references:** Cannot use country names if association is misleading
- **No "EU-made" if sourced outside:** If components from Asia/other regions, full chain must be transparent
- **No geographic name misuse:** "Champagne" limited to French region only; "Parmigiano" limited to Italy

## 1. Trademark Rights & Cross-Border Sales

### EU Trademark Registration (EUTM)

**EUTM provides protection across all 27 EU member states + UK + 5 EEA countries (Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway, Switzerland, Turkey).**

**Trademark owner rights:**

- Right to control distribution channels
- Right to prevent use by competitors

- **Seller response window:** 7-10 days to provide proof of authorization
- **Account penalties:** Multiple violations (3+) result in permanent suspension

### Parallel Imports & EU Exhaustion Principle

**Doctrine of Exhaustion (EU Law):**
- Once a product is legally sold in the EU by the brand owner, the trademark rights are "exhausted"

**Example:** You can buy Nike shoes from an authorized Italian retailer and resell them on Amazon Germany legally, even without Nike's permission.

**Exception:** Luxury brands with selective distribution agreements may restrict resale through unauthorized channels (litigation ongoing in EU courts).

### Supplier vs. Deemed Supplier Status

**Threshold for change (Jan 1, 2022):**
- Sales to consumers **under €10,000/year** = Supplier status (apply customer's country VAT)
- Sales to consumers **over €10,000/year** = Deemed Supplier status (apply own country VAT if using FBA/marketplace fulfillment)

**Marketplace Deemed Supplier Rule:**
If you use **Amazon FBA, eBay managed fulfillment, or Kaufland logistics**, the marketplace is deemed to be the "supplier" for VAT purposes. You apply:
- **Your country's VAT** (not customer's country)
- Marketplace deducts VAT at source when paying you

**Example:** German seller using Amazon FBA selling to France — VAT charged is 19% (German rate), not 20% (French rate). Amazon calculates this automatically.

## 2. One-Stop-Shop (OSS) System

### What is OSS?

The One-Stop-Shop allows sellers to register for **multi-country VAT compliance via a single registration** instead of registering in each country.

**Benefit:** Simplified administration; single monthly VAT return covers all EU countries.

**Platform:** [europa.eu - VAT OSS Portal](https://ec.europa.eu/taxation_customs/business/vat/eu-vat-assistance-oss_en)

### OSS Eligibility

**You can use OSS if:**
- You are a **non-EU seller** importing goods into the EU, OR
- You exceed the **€10,000 threshold** in any individual EU country AND are **not already VAT-registered** there

**You cannot use OSS if:**
- You are already registered for VAT in an EU member state
- Your business is EU-based but not registered in destination country

### OSS Registration Process

1. **Create Europa account** at [ec.europa.eu](https://ec.europa.eu)
2. **Select OSS scheme** (Import OSS or Union OSS depending on your status)
3. **Register from your home country** (non-EU sellers register in their country's OSS)
4. **Receive OSS identification number** (e.g., OSS-EU-123456789)
5. **Apply at each EU marketplace** (provide OSS number; platforms auto-calculate VAT)

**Timeline:** 5-10 business days for approval.

**Cost:** Free.

### OSS Monthly Compliance

**Each month, you must:**
- File VAT return showing sales by destination country
- Pay total VAT owed (combined across all countries)
- Maintain sales records by country
- Report to your tax authority

**Example:** If you have:
- €50,000 sales to Germany (19% VAT = €9,500)
- €30,000 sales to France (20% VAT = €6,000)
- €20,000 sales to Italy (22% VAT = €4,400)
- **Total VAT owed:** €19,900 (single OSS payment)

---

## 3. Import One-Stop-Shop (IOSS)

### What is IOSS?

IOSS is a simplified VAT scheme for goods imported from outside the EU with a value **under €150**. It allows the importer/distributor to apply VAT at import rather than at sale.

**Scope:** Only applies to goods from non-EU countries (e.g., China, USA, India).

**Advantage:** VAT is collected at import, consumers pay VAT-inclusive price.

### IOSS Eligibility

**IOSS applies if:**
- **Goods shipped from outside EU** (China, USA, etc.) to customer in EU
- **Customs value <€150** (excluding shipping/insurance)
- **Product is not excisable** (no alcohol, tobacco, fuel)
- You have an **IOSS registration number**

**IOSS does NOT apply to:**
- Goods over €150 (duty-free threshold)
- Goods shipped EU-to-EU (use OSS instead)
- Excisable goods (alcohol, cigarettes, fuel)

### IOSS Registration

**If you import from non-EU suppliers:**
1. Register for IOSS number at the EU import authority
2. Provide IOSS number to non-EU suppliers
3. Suppliers show IOSS number on shipping label
4. Customs collects VAT at import; you're compliant

**Note:** If using a fulfillment partner (e.g., Amazon Global, AliExpress logistics), **they typically handle IOSS registration** on your behalf.

# DATA PROTECTION (GDPR)

## 1. GDPR Applicability for Marketplace Sellers

### Scope

The General Data Protection Regulation (Regulation 2016/679) applies to any seller processing customer personal data, regardless of marketplace location or seller location.

**Personal data includes:**
- Name, address, email
- Phone number
- IP address
- Cookie identifiers
- Payment information
- Purchase history
- Behavioral tracking data

### Controller vs. Processor

**Marketplace sellers are usually Data Controllers:**
- You determine **purpose** (selling products) and **means** (customer contact for order fulfillment)
- You decide how customer data is used
- **You are liable** for GDPR compliance

**Marketplace platforms (Amazon, eBay) may be co-controllers or processors:**
- Amazon/eBay processes data on seller's behalf (fulfillment, payment)
- Shared responsibility under Data Processing Agreements (DPA)

---

## 2. Lawfulness of Data Processing

### Legal Basis for Processing

**To collect customer data, you need a legal basis (Lawful Processing Condition). The six options:**

1. **Consent (Article 6(1)a):**
   - Customer explicitly agrees to data processing
   - Clear, unambiguous affirmative action (checkbox must be affirmative; pre-ticked boxes are illegal)
   - Can be withdrawn anytime
   - **Risk:** Easy to withdraw; not ideal for ongoing communications

2. **Contract (Article 6(1)b):**
   - Data necessary to fulfill sales contract
   - Email, address, phone for shipping
   - **Strongest basis** for e-commerce
   - Does NOT cover marketing communications

3. **Legal Obligation (Article 6(1)c):**
   - Required by EU/national law (tax, accounting)
   - Applies to tax records, invoice retention
   - **Timeframe:** 10 years minimum for tax documents

4. **Vital Interests (Article 6(1)d):**
   - Rare; applies to emergency health situations
   - Not applicable for typical e-commerce

5. **Public Task (Article 6(1)e):**
   - Government agencies, public authorities
   - Not applicable for private marketplace sellers

6. **Legitimate Interests (Article 6(1)f):**
   - Balancing test: seller's interest vs. customer privacy
   - Can include marketing IF reasonably expected
   - Must be transparent; customer must be able to opt-out

### Data Processing for Marketing

**Email marketing (opt-in requirement):**
- **B2C (business-to-consumer):** Pre-consent required before sending marketing emails
- **B2B (business-to-business):** Softer rules; can email if business-related interest exists
- **Soft opt-in:** Can email existing customers about similar products if they were informed they could opt-out

**Unsubscribe option mandatory:**
- Every marketing email must include working unsubscribe link
- Unsubscribe request must be honored within 10 days

---

## 3. Customer Rights Under GDPR

### Right of Access (Article 15)

**Customer can request:**
- All personal data you hold about them
- Purpose of processing
- Categories of recipients
- Retention timeline

**Your obligation:**
- Respond within **30 days** (can extend 60 days for complex requests)
- Provide data in common format (CSV, PDF)
- No fee (unless request is excessive)

### Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten") — Article 17

**Customer can request deletion if:**
- Data no longer needed for original purpose
- Processing is unlawful
- No legal basis exists for processing
- Customer withdraws consent

**You can refuse if:**
- Legal obligation requires retention (tax law: 10 years)
- Establishment of legal claims
- Public interest (rare)

**Your timeline:** 30 days to comply or explain why you cannot.

### Right to Data Portability (Article 20)

**Customer can request:**
- Copy of their data in structured, commonly used format
- Transfer data to another service provider
- Applies to data provided by customer OR generated through use

**Examples:**
- Purchase history export
- Customer profile data
- Behavioral/interaction data (if tracked)

**Timeline:** 30 days; can extend 60 days for complex requests.

### Right to Restrict Processing (Article 18)

**Customer can demand you stop using their data** while they dispute accuracy or lawfulness of processing.

**You must:**
- Stop processing (but keep storing)
- Inform customer before removing restriction
- Maintain data security during restriction period

---

## 4. Data Retention & Deletion Obligations

### Mandatory Retention Periods

| Data Type | Minimum Retention | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Tax records** | 10 years | Tax law (EU/national) |
| **Invoices** | 10 years | Accounting directives |
| **Payment records** | 10 years | VAT/tax compliance |
| **Customer disputes/complaints** | 1-3 years | Legal claim establishment |
| **Marketing opt-in records** | As long as consent valid | GDPR proof of consent |
| **Employee/supplier records** | 5-10 years | Employment law |

### Data Deletion Timeline

**After data is no longer needed:**
- Delete within **30 days** of request/retention period expiration
- Inform customer of deletion
- Cannot re-use data for new purposes
- Anonymization acceptable alternative (cannot re-identify)

---

## 5. Privacy Policy & Transparency

### Required Privacy Policy Elements

**Your privacy policy MUST include:**

1. **Identity of controller:** Your name, address, contact info
2. **Processing purpose:** "To fulfill orders," "To send marketing emails" (separate for each purpose)
3. **Legal basis:** Which of the six lawful bases applies
4. **Data categories:** What data you collect (name, email, IP address, etc.)
5. **Recipients:** Who data is shared with (payment processors, fulfillment partners, etc.)
6. **Retention periods:** How long you keep each data category
7. **Customer rights:** How to exercise rights (access, erasure, portability, restriction)
8. **Complaints procedure:** Contact info for data protection authority
9. **Profiling/automated decisions:** If using data for automated decision-making
10. **Third-party transfers:** If data shared with non-EU processors (adequacy decision status)

### Marketplace-Specific Privacy Notices

**On product pages/checkout, disclose:**
- How customer data will be used (fulfillment, marketing, analytics)
- Who has access (seller, marketplace, fulfillment partner, payment processor)
- Whether tracking cookies will be used (Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, etc.)

---

## 6. Data Processing Agreements (DPA)

### DPA Requirement

**If marketplace (Amazon, eBay) or fulfillment partner processes your customer data, you MUST have a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) in place.**

**DPA covers:**
- Processor responsibilities (data security, sub-processors)
- Seller (controller) responsibilities (lawfulness, purpose)
- Data breach notification procedures
- Audit/compliance rights
- Data deletion protocols

**Most platforms provide standard DPAs:**
- Amazon: Included in service terms; separate DPA available on request
- eBay: Provided in seller agreement
- Kaufland: Available upon request

---

## 7. Data Breach Notification

### Breach Definition

A **personal data breach** is unauthorized access, loss, or disclosure of customer data. Examples:
- Hacking/cyberattack
- Accidental exposure (email sent to wrong recipient)
- Data leaked on public server
- Lost/stolen devices with customer data

### Notification Obligations

**If breach affects customers:**

1. **Notify Data Protection Authority (within 72 hours):**
   - File breach report with national DPA (e.g., ICO in UK, CNIL in France)
   - Include: What happened, who was affected, what actions taken
   - No cost for notification

2. **Notify Affected Customers (without undue delay):**
   - Email, phone, or mail affected customers
   - Include: What data was breached, what steps taken to secure data
   - If risk is low, notification can be delayed

3. **Document the breach:**
   - Keep internal record of breach
   - Maintain for at least 3 years
   - Include remediation steps

**Penalties for delayed/missing notification:**
- €5,000-10,000 fine per day of delay
- Up to €20 million fine or 4% turnover (whichever higher) for serious violations

## 2. Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) by Country

### EPR Labeling Requirements

**Mandatory EPR markings vary by country:**

| Country | Required Label | Placement | Format |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Germany** | LUCID registration number + dual system logo | Back/side of package | Logo (Der Grüne Punkt, Interseroh, etc.) |
| **France** | Triman logo + Info-Tri label with QR code | Front or back; 5mm min. | Color logo + French sorting instructions |
| **Italy** | CONAI mark + material symbols | Back of package | Color logo; material identification numbers |
| **Spain** | Ecolec/Scrap logo (if member) | Package back | Logo of scheme membership |
| **Belgium (Flanders)** | Recupel/Fost Plus logos | Back of package | Separate logos for packaging waste systems |
| **Netherlands** | NVMP membership symbol (if registered) | Back of package | Logo or text indicating system participation |
| **Sweden** | Registration confirmation via PRV database (label optional) | Recommended on back | Text or symbol (low enforcement of labeling) |

### Germany VerpackG Labeling

**Most critical for German sales:**

1. **LUCID Registration Number:**
   - Format: "DE" + 8-digit number (e.g., DE12345678)
   - Must appear on packaging (front or back)
   - Font: Minimum 4pt

2. **Dual System Logo:**
   - One of 5 approved logos (Der Grüne Punkt is most common)
   - Must be exact size/colors
   - Placement: Back or side of package

3. **Material Identification:**
   - Material symbols (♻1, ♻2, ♻5, etc.) or text (e.g., "cardboard," "plastic")
   - If composite packaging, all materials must be identified
   - Helps sorting at recycling centers

### France Citeo Labeling

**Required for all French sales:**

1. **Triman Logo:**
   - Color logo (blue/white/black)
   - Minimum 5mm × 5mm
   - Placement: Front or back of package

2. **Info-Tri Instructions:**
   - French language required
   - Either printed on package OR QR code to Citeo website
   - Informs consumer how to sort packaging

3. **Logo Placement Examples:**
   - Text on label: "For sorting instructions: [Citeo website or QR code]"
   - Logo always visible (not hidden inside package)

## 3. Digital Product Passport (DPP)

### EU Digital Product Passport Regulation (2024/1670)

**Status:** Adopted December 2024; implementation begins **January 1, 2030** (phased by product category).

**Scope:** Applies to batteries, textiles, electronics, plastics initially; expanding to other sectors.

**What it requires:**
- Product ID (QR code or RFID chip)
- Sustainability data (carbon footprint, recycled content, repairability)
- Compliance information (certifications, safety data)
- Maintenance/repair instructions
- End-of-life disposal instructions

### Current DPP Requirements (2026)

**For affected product categories:**
- Batteries (EU 2023/1542): Battery Information File (BIF) required in BATID database
- Textiles (proposed): Sustainability data by 2028
- Electronics: Spare parts availability, repair instructions by 2030

**Marketplace implications:**
- Amazon, eBay, Kaufland will require DPP data for uploads
- Missing DPP data may result in delisting (starting 2026 for batteries)

**Estimated timeline:** Phased implementation 2025-2030 across product categories.

## 4. Language & Accessibility Requirements

### Language Rules by Country

| Country | Primary Language Requirement | Secondary Language Allowed | Exceptions |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Germany** | German mandatory | English acceptable for international products | Niche/imported products |
| **France** | French mandatory (Toubon Law) | English NOT acceptable for packaging | Strict enforcement; fines up to €5,000 |
| **Italy** | Italian preferred | English accepted on secondary labels | Marketing materials can be English-only |
| **Spain** | Spanish mandatory (Castilian) | Regional languages acceptable | Bilingual acceptable |
| **Belgium** | Dutch (Flanders) or French (Wallonia) | English acceptable | Depends on region of sale |
| **Netherlands** | Dutch or English | Either acceptable | English widely accepted |
| **UK** | English mandatory | — | Post-Brexit; strict enforcement |
| **Scandinavia** | Local language + English | English widely accepted | English packaging normal for international products |

### Food Labeling Language Requirements

**CRITICAL:** Food Information Regulation (1169/2011) mandates:
- **Ingredient list:** Must be in customer's language (Italian, French, German, etc.)
- **Allergen warnings:** Must be in customer's language
- **Nutrition information:** May be in English (if FOP is local language)

**Violation consequence:** Product can be seized from marketplace; seller fined up to €10,000.

## 5. Age Restriction Markings

### Mandatory Age Warnings

**Products requiring age warnings:**

| Category | Marking | Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **Toys for children <3** | Age warning | "Not suitable for children under 3 years" (on packaging + listing) |
| **Balloons** | Choking hazard | "Warning: Choking hazard for children under 8 years" |
| **Plastic bags/films** | Suffocation hazard | "Warning: Keep away from infants" (if film <0.038mm) |
| **Electronics with small parts** | Choking hazard | Age warning if parts <31.75mm diameter |
| **Hobby/model building** | Age warning | Recommended age label (e.g., "8+", "14+") |
| **Sharp objects** | Injury warning | "Keep away from children" + first aid instructions |
